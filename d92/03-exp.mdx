title: Expander
---

<details class="definition" open>
<summary>expander</summary>

bipartite graph $G=(U,V,E)$ is $D$ **regular** iff 

$$
\forall u\in U\quad |N(u)|=D
$$

- that is, every left vertex has $D$ neighbors

bipartite graph $G=(U,V,E)$ is $(K,A)$ **expander** iff

$$
\forall S\subseteq U\quad |S|\le K\implies |N(S)|\ge A|S|
$$

$D$ regular bipartite $\Gamma:[N]\to [D]\to [M]$ is $(K,A)$ **expander** iff

$$
\forall S\subseteq[N]\quad |S|=K\implies |\Gamma(S)|\le AK
$$

**good** expander is

$$
D=\mathcal{O}(1)\quad K=\Omega(N)\quad A\text{ close to } D
$$
</details>

<details class="theorem" open>
<summary>half of balanced regular bipartite are good expanders</summary>

<div class="flex justify-center">

$\forall D\exists \alpha\forall N$ half of $\text{Bip}_{N,D}$ are $(\alpha N,D-2)$ expanders

</div>

- where $\text{Bip}_{N,D}$ is set of $D$ regular bipartite with $N$ left and $N$ right vertices

<details class="list-none">
<summary>**Proof.**</summary>

1. notice not $(\alpha N,D-2)$ expander means

<div class="flex justify-center">

$\exists K\le\alpha N\quad\exists S\subseteq[N]\quad |S|=K$ but $|N(S)|\lt AK=KD-2K$
</div>

   - that is, has $2K$ repeated right vertices
2. given $K,S,2K$, the probability that the $1\le i\le KD$ th vertex repeat (with previous) is 

$$
\frac{i-1}N\le \frac{KD}N
$$

2. given $K,S$, the probability that $\exists 2K$ repeats is at most

$$
\left(\frac{KD}N\right)^{2K}
$$

3. given $K$, the probability that $\exists S\exists2K$ is at most


$$
\binom{KD}{2K}\left(\frac{KD}N\right)^{2K}
$$

4. the probability that $\exists K\exists S\exists 2K$ is at most

$$
\binom NK\binom{KD}{2K}\left(\frac{KD}N\right)^{2K}\le\left(\frac{e^3D^4}4\cdot \frac KN\right)^K
$$

  - notice $\binom nk\le(ne/k)^k$

5. thus let $\alpha=1/e^3D^4\ge K/N$, we have

$$
\left(\frac{e^3D^4}4\cdot \frac KN\right)^K\le \left(\frac{e^3D^4}4\cdot\alpha\right)^K=4^{-K}
$$

6. finally

$$
\text{Pr}_{G\in\text{BiP}_{ND}}[G\text{ bad}]\le\sum_{K=1}^{\alpha N}4^{-K}\le1/2\enspace\square
$$
</details>

</details>

<details class="theorem" open>
<summary>spectral expansion iff vertex expansion</summary>


- spectral expansion $\gamma\implies  (N/2,1+\gamma)$ expander<br/>

- $(N/2,1+\delta)$ expander $\implies \Omega((\alpha/D)^2)$ spectral expansion

</details>

<details class="example" open>
<summary>discrete torus</summary>

let $[N]=\mathbb{Z}_M\oplus\mathbb{Z}_M$

$$
\Gamma(x,y)=\{(x,y),(x+1,y),(x,y+1),(x,x+y),(-y,x)\}
$$

this is $D=5$ regular with $N=M^2$

- has spectral expansion $\gamma=\Omega(1)$

- thus is $(N/2,1+\gamma)$ expander

</details>
