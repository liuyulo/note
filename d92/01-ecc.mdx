## Error Correcting Codes

> just injective maps duh

<details class="definition" open>
<summary>error correcting code</summary>

for alphabet $\Sigma$ and $k,n$ with Hamming distance $d$

<div class="flex justify-center">
$C:\Sigma^k\to\Sigma^n$ is a **ECC**
</div>
where

- $n$ is the **code length** (depends on $k$)
- $C(\Sigma^k)$ is the set of **codewords**
- $(\Sigma^n,d)$ is a metric space
</details>

<details class="definition" open>
<summary>code rate, distance, relative distance</summary>

for $C:\Sigma^k\to\Sigma^n$

- $k/n$ is **rate** of $C$
  - high rate means low redundency
- $d(C)=\min\{d(C(x_1),C(x_2)):x_1\ne x_2\in \Sigma^k\}$ is **distance** of $C$
  - high distance means corrects more errors
- $d(C)/n$ is **relative distance** of $C$
</details>

<details class="definition" open>
<summary>linear code</summary>
$C:\mathbb F_q^k\to\mathbb F_q^n$ is **linear** means $C(x+y)=C(x)+C(y)$

- $C$ is $k\times n$ matrix over $\mathbb F_q$, called **generating matrix**
- $w(y)=d(y,0)$ is **weight** of $y\in\mathbb F_q^n$
- $d(C)=\min\{w(C(x)):x\ne0\in\mathbb F_q^k\}$
</details>

## Random Codes

> constructive proof? never heard of that

<details class="theorem" open>
<summary>probabilistic method</summary>
> (ab)used by Paul Erdős [ɛrdøːʃ]

let predicate $Q$ (over some universe)

$\because \nexists aQ(a)\implies P(Q(a))=0\in[0,1]$

$\therefore P(Q(a))\gt0\implies\exists aQ(a)$

<details open>
<summary>other uses</summary>

$$
\begin{align*}
P(Q(a))\lt 1&\implies\exists a\neg Q(a)\\
\exists a(X(a)\lt E(X))&\implies\exists a(X(a)\gt E(X))\\
\exists a(X(a)\ge E(X))
\end{align*}
$$
</details>

</details>

<details class="theorem" open>
<summary>good code exists and most codes are good</summary>
for any relative distance $\delta\lt 1/2$, large enough $k$, any $t$ such that

$$
n\ge\frac{k+t}{1-H_2(\delta)}
$$

- where $H_2(\alpha)=\alpha\log_2(1/\alpha)+(1-\alpha)\log_2(1/(1-\alpha))$ is the entropy function

$$
P(d(C)/n\ge\delta)\gt1-2^{-t}
$$

- where $C:\mathbb F_2^k\to\mathbb F_2^n$ is a random code

## Walsh-Hadamard Code

> long, but relative distance 1/2

<details class="definition" open>
<summary>WH code</summary>
let alphabet $\mathbb F_2$ and length $n=2^k$
- let domain $\mathbb F_2^k=\{\alpha_1,\cdots,\alpha_n\}$

$$
\begin{align*}
  C(x)&=(x\cdot \alpha_i)_{i=1}^n
\end{align*}
$$
</details>

<details class="theorem" open>
<summary>relative distance of half</summary>
$\because w(C(x))=2^{k-1}$ for any $x\in \mathbb F_2^k$

$\therefore$ relative distance is $2^{k-1}/2^{k}=1/2$
</details>
