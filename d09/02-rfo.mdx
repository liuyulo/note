## Relation 

<details class="definition" open>
<summary>ordered pairs</summary>

for sets $a,b$, the **ordered pair** of $a$ and $b$ is

<div class="flex justify-center">

$(a,b)=\{\{a\},\{a,b\}\}$

</div>

- N.B. $(a,a)=\{\{a\}\}$

<details>
<summary>**Construction.** using axioms</summary>

- pair $a$ to $\{a\}$
- pair $a,b$ to $\{a,b\}$
- pair to $\{\{a\},\{a,b\}\}$
</details>
</details>

<details class="theorem" open>
<summary>pair equality</summary>
<div class="flex justify-center">

$(a_1,b_1)=(a_2,b_2)\iff a_1=a_2$ and $b_1=b_2$
</div>
</details>

<details class="definition" open>
<summary>(binary) relation</summary>

set $R$ is **relation** iff

$$
\forall p\in R\quad\exists x\exists y\quad p=(x,y)
$$

$aRb$ denotes $(a,b)\in R$
</details>

<details class="definition" open>
<summary>domain, range, field</summary>

for relation $R$

- **domain** of $R$ is 
  $$
  \begin{align*}
  \operatorname{dom} R&=\{a:\exists b\enspace aRb\}\\
  \end{align*}
  $$
- **range**  of $R$ is 
  $$
  \operatorname{ran} R=\{b:\exists a\enspace aRb\}
  $$
- **field** of $R$ is 
  $$
  \operatorname{field}R=\operatorname{dom} R\cup\operatorname{ran} R
  $$

notice $\operatorname{dom} R,\operatorname{ran}R\sube\cup(\cup A)$
</details>

<details class="definition" open>
<summary>image, inverse image, inverse</summary>
for relation $R$ 

- **image** of set $A$ under $R$ is 
  $$
  R[A]=\{b\in\operatorname{ran}R:\exists a\in A\enspace aRb\}
  $$
- **inverse image** of set $B$ under $R$ is
  $$
  R^{-1}[B]=\{a\in\operatorname{dom}R:\exists b\in B\enspace aRb\}
  $$
- **inverse** of $R$ is
  $$
  R^{-1}=\{(b,a):aRb\}
  $$
<details class="list-none">
<summary>**Proof.** inverse image is image of inverse</summary>

let any relation $R$ and set $B$

$\because\operatorname{dom} R=\operatorname{ran} R^{-1}$

$\therefore$ let any $a\in\operatorname{dom}R$

- $a$ is member of inverse image of $B$ under $R$
- $\iff \exists b\in B\enspace aRb$
- $\iff\exists b\in B\enspace bR^{-1}a$
- $\iff a$ is member of image of $B$ under $R^{-1}\quad\square$
</details>
</details>

<details class="definition" open>
<summary>relation composition</summary>
for relation $R$ and $S$

- **composition** of $R$ and $S$ is 
  $$
  S\circ R=\{(a,c):\exists b\enspace aRb\enspace bSc\}
  $$
</details>

<details class="definition" open>
<summary>special relations</summary>

for set $A$ and $B$

- (cartesian) **product** of $a$ and $b$ is
  $$
  a\times b=\{(a,b): a\in a\enspace b\in b\}\sube\mathcal P(\mathcal P(a\cup b))
  $$
- **membership** relation on $a$ is
  $$
  \in_a=\{(a,b)\in a\times a:a\in b\}
  $$
- **identity** relation on $a$ is
  $$
  1_a=\{(a,a):a\in a\}
  $$
</details>

<details class="definition" open>
<summary>relation properties</summary>

relation $R$ on set $A$ is


<div class="flex justify-center">


| | |
|-:|:-|
|**reflexive**|$\forall a(aRa)$|
|**symmetric**|$\forall a\forall b(aRb\implies bRa)$|
|**transitive**|$\forall a\forall b\forall c(aRb\implies bRc\implies aRc)$|
|**equivalence** | reflexive, symmetric, transitive|

</div>
</details>

<details class="definition" open>
<summary>equivalence class</summary>
for equivalence $E$ on $A$, the **equivalence class** of $a\in A$ modulo $E$ is

$$
[a]_E=\{b\in A:aEb\}
$$

the **system of equivalence classes** on $A$ modulo $E$ is

$$
A/E=\{[a]_E:a\in A\}
$$

</details>

## Function

<details class="definition" open>
<summary>function</summary>
relation $F$ is **function** iff

$$
\forall a\forall b_1\forall b_2\enspace aFb_1\implies aFb_2\implies b_1=b_2
$$

that is

$$
\forall a\in\operatorname{dom}F\enspace\exists!b\enspace aFb
$$

- $F(a)$ denotes such unique $b$
- $F:A\to B$ denotes "$F$ is function where $\operatorname{dom}F=A$ and $\operatorname{ran} F\sube B$"
</details>

<details class="theorem" open>
<summary>function equality</summary>
for function $f$ and $g$

$$
f=g\iff\operatorname{dom} f=\operatorname{dom}g\text{ and }\forall x\enspace f(x)=g(x)
$$
</details>

<details class="definition" open>
<summary>terminology</summary>

function $f$ is

- **on** $A\iff\operatorname{dom}f=A$
- **into** $B\iff\operatorname{ran} f\sube B$
- **onto** $B\iff\operatorname{ran}f=B$

a **restriction** of $f$ to set $A$ is
$$
f\upharpoonright A=\{(a,b)\in f:a\in A\}
$$

- $f$ is then an **extension** of its restriction
</details>

<details class="theorem" open>
<summary>function composition</summary>
for function $f$ and $g$, the **composition** $gf$ is function where

$$
\operatorname{dom}(g\circ f)=\operatorname{dom}f\cap f^{-1}[\operatorname{dom}g]
$$
that is, $x$ is defined on $gf\iff x$ is defined in $f$ and $f(x)$ is defined in $g$

- $(g\circ f)(x)=g(f(x))$ for any $x\in\operatorname{dom}(g\circ f)$

</details>

<details class="definition" open>
<summary>index system of sets (aka function)</summary>

let function $S$ with domain $I$

$$
\{S_\alpha\}_{\alpha\in I}
$$

is an **indexed** system 

</details>

<details class="definition" open>
<summary>product</summary>

the product of $S$ is

$$
\prod_{\alpha\in I}S_\alpha=\{f:\forall\alpha\in I\enspace f(\alpha)\in S_\alpha\}
$$
</details>

<details class="theorem" open>
<summary></summary>

if $\exists B\forall\alpha\in I\quad S_\alpha=B$ then

$$
\prod_{\alpha\in I}S_\alpha
$$
</details>

<details class="definition" open>
<summary>indexed</summary>

set $A$ is **indexed** by $S$ iff

$$
A=\{S_\alpha:\alpha\in I\}=\operatorname{ran}S
$$

</details>

## Order

<details class="definition" open>
<summary>order</summary>

for relation $R$ on $A$

<div class="flex justify-center">
$a,b\in A$ are **comparable** $\iff aRb\lor bRa$
</div>

relation $R$ on set $A$ is

<div class="flex justify-center">

| | |
|-:|:-|
|**antisymmetric**| $\forall a\forall b(a Rb\implies bRa\implies a=b)$
|**asymmetric**|$\forall a\forall b(aR b\implies b\cancel Ra)$|
|**partial order**|reflexive, antisymmetric, transitive|
|**strict order** | asymmetric, transitive
|**total order** | $\forall a\forall b(aRb\lor bRa)$
|**well order**| total order, and any nonempty subset has a least element

</div>

- partial ordered set is **poset**
- strictly ordered set is **soset**
- total ordered set is **loset** (linear ordered set)
- well ordered set is **woset**
</details>

<details class="theorem" open>
<summary>poset to soset, soset to poset</summary>
for poset $(A,\le)$, the *strict order* is defined

$$
\forall a\forall b\quad a\lt b\iff a\le b\land a\ne b
$$

for soset $(A,\lt)$, the *partial order* is defined

$$
\forall a\forall b\quad a\le b\iff a\lt b\lor a=b
$$
</details>

<details class="definition" open>
<summary>poset isomorphism</summary>
for poset $A$ and $B$

- $A\cong B\iff\exists f: A\stackrel\sim\to B$ st

$$
\forall a_1,a_2\in A(a_1\le a_2\iff f(a_1)\le f(a_2))
$$
</details>


<details class="definition" open>
<summary>least, minimal</summary>

for poset $(A,\le)$ and $B\subseteq A$

| | |
|-|-|
| $b\in B$ is **least** of $B$ in $\le$ | $\forall x\in B\quad b\le x$
| $b\in B$ is **maximal** of $B$ in $\le$ | $\forall x\in B\quad x\le b\implies  x=b$
| $b\in B$ is **greatest** of $B$ in $\le$|$\forall x\in B\quad b\ge x$
|$b\in B$ is **maximal** of $B$ in $\le$|$\forall x\in B\quad x\ge b\implies x=b$

- least is also called **left endpoint**
- greatest is also called **right endpoint**
- **endpoint** is either least or greatest
</details>

<details class="theorem" open>
<summary></summary>

- $B$ has at most one least element
- any least is maximal

similar for greatest/maximal
</details>

<details class="definition" open>
<summary>bound</summary>

for poset $(A,\le)$ and $B\subseteq A$

-  $a\in A$ is **lower bound** of $B$ in $(A,\le)\iff\forall x\in B\quad a\le x$
- $a\in A$ is **infimum** of $B$ in $(A,\le)\iff a$ is greatest in set of all lower bounds of $B$ in $(A,\le)$
-  $a\in A$ is **upper bound** of $B$ in $(A,\le)\iff\forall x\in B\quad a\ge x$
- $a\in A$ is **supremum** of $B$ in $(A,\le)\iff a$ is least in set of all upper bounds of $B$ in $(A,\le)$
</details>

<details class="theorem" open>
<summary></summary>

- $B$ has at most one infimum
- least $\implies $ infimum
- $a$ is infimum and $a\in B\implies a$ is least
- $a\in A$ is infimum of $B$ in $(A,\le)\iff$
  - $\forall x\in B\quad a\le x$
  - $\forall a'\in A(\forall x\in B\quad a\le x)\implies a'\le a$

same for supremum
</details>
