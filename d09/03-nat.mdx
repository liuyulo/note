## Natural 

<details class="definition" open>
<summary>successor function</summary>

for set $x$, the **successor** of $x$ is

$$
S(x)=x+1=x\cup\{x\}
$$
</details>

<details class="definition" open>
<summary>inductive set</summary>

set $I$ is **inductive** iff all

- $0\in I$
- $\forall n(n\in I\implies n+1\in I)$
</details>

<details class="definition" open>
<summary>naturals</summary>

$$
\begin{align*}
  0&=\varnothing\\
  1&=S(0)=\{0\}=\{\varnothing\}\\
  2&=S(1)=\{0,1\}=\{\varnothing,\{\varnothing\}\}\\
  3&=S(2)=\{0,1,2\}\\
\end{align*}
$$
</details>

<details class="definition" open>
<summary>naturals</summary>

let $A$ be some inductive set, define $\mathbb N\sube A$ where

<div class="flex justify-center">
$x\in\mathbb N\iff x\in I$ for any inductive $I$
</div>

- $\mathbb N$ is inductive
- let $\lt$ be canonical order of $\mathbb N$ where $a\lt b\iff a\in b$
  - strict, total, well-ordered
</details>

<details class="theorem" open>
<summary>induction principle</summary>

$$
\bold P(0)\land\forall n(\bold P(n)\implies\bold P(n+1))\implies\forall n\bold P(n)
$$
</details>


<details class="theorem" open>
<summary>recursion theorem</summary>
for any set $A$, element $a\in A$, function $g:A\times\mathbb N\to A$

- $\exists !f:\mathbb N\to A$ such that

$$
\begin{align*}
  f(0)&=a\\
  \forall n\enspace f(n+1)&=g(f(n),n)
\end{align*}
$$
</details>
