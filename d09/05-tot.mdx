## Total Order

<details class="definition" open>
<summary>similar</summary>

loset $(A,\le_A)$ and $(B,\le_B)$ **similar** (aka same order type) $\iff A\cong B$

**Corollary.** similar is equivalence *relation*
</details>

<details class="theorem" open>
<summary>finite sets of same cardinality are similar</summary>

for any finite loset $(A,\le_A),(B,\le_B)$

$$
|A|=|B|\iff|A|\cong |B|
$$

<details class="list-none">
<summary>**Lemma.** any total order on a finite set is well-order</summary>

(induction on $|A|=n$)

base $n=1$, obvious

let $|A|=n+1$

- $\therefore A=A'\sqcup\{a\}$ for some $a\in A-A'$
- $\therefore A'$ has some least element $a'$<span class="float-right">(IH)</span>
- $\therefore a$ or $a'$ is least element of $A\enspace\square$

</details>

<details class="list-none">
<summary>**Proof.**</summary>

$(\impliedby )$ by definition

$(\implies )$ induction on $|A|=|B|=n$

base $n=0$, then $\varnothing\cong\varnothing$

let any $|A|=|B|=n+1$, assume IH

- $\because(A,\le_A),(B,\le_B)$ are wosets
- let $a$ be least of $A$ and $b$ be least of $B$
- let $A'=A-\{a\}$ and $B'=B-\{b\}$
- $\therefore A'\cong B'$ by some $\varphi'$<span class="float-right">(IH)</span>
- let $\varphi'=\varphi\cup\{(a,b)\}$
- $\therefore A\cong B$ by $\varphi\enspace\square$

</details>

</details> 

### Sum

<details class="definition" open>
<summary>sum of losets</summary>

for **disjoint** losets $A$ and $B$, the sum $A+B$ is the loset $A\sqcup B$ where

$$
\begin{align*}
  a,b\in A\land a\lt_A b&\implies a\lt b\\
  a,b\in B\land a\lt_B b&\implies a\lt b\\
  a\in A\land b\in B&\implies a\lt b\\
\end{align*}
$$

otherwise $a\not\lt b$

**N.B.** such sum is not commutative

</details>


### Countable Dense Losts

<details class="definition" open>
<summary>dense</summary>

for $(X,\lt)$ with $|X|\ge2$

<div class="flex justify-center">

$X$ is **dense** $\iff\forall a,b\in X\quad\exists x\in X\quad (a\lt b\implies  a\lt x\lt b)$
</div>

<details class="list-none">
<summary>**Proof.** any closed interval of a dloset is infinite</summary>

BWOC assume $[a,b]\subseteq A$ finite

let $[a,b]=\{a,a_1,a_2,\cdots,a_n,b\}$ where

$$
a\lt a_1\lt a_2\lt\cdots\lt a_n\lt b
$$

$\because A$ dense

$\therefore\exists c\in A\quad a\lt c\lt a_1$

$\therefore a\le c\lt a_1\le b$, thus $c\in[a,b]$

$\because c\ne a$ and $c\ne a_1$

$\therefore c\in\{a_2,\cdots,a_n,b\}$, contradicts that $c\lt a_1\enspace\square$

</details>

</details>

<details class="theorem" open>
<summary>countable dense losets without endpoints are similar</summary>

for any countable dense loset $(P,\lt)$ without endpoints

$$
P\cong \mathbb{Q}
$$

</details>

<details class="theorem" open>
<summary>countable losets are similar</summary>
any countable dense loset is similar to a subset of any countable loset without endpoints
</details>



## Complete Total Order

<details class="definition" open>
<summary>complete</summary>

dense loset $(P,\lt)$ is **complete** iff

<div class="flex justify-center">

any nonempty $A\subseteq P$ bounded above has a supremum
</div>

</details>

<details class="theorem" open>
<summary>unique completion</summary>

for any dense loset $(P,\lt_P)$ without endpoint

<div class="flex justify-center">

there is a unique (up to isomorphism) complete loset $(C,\lt_C)$ without endpoints 
</div>

- $P\subseteq C$ and $\lt_P\subseteq\lt_C$

$$
\forall p,q\in P\quad(p\lt_P q\iff p\lt _Cq)
$$
- $P$ is dense in $C$

$$
\forall p,q\in P\quad \exists c\in C\quad(p\lt q\implies  p\lt c\lt q)
$$

</details>

<details class="definition" open>
<summary>reals</summary>

<div class="flex justify-center">

$\mathbb{R}$ is the completion of $\mathbb{Q}$
</div>

$\mathbb{R}$ is the unique (up to isomorphism) complete dense loset without endpoints that contains a countable dense subset

<details class="list-none">
<summary>**Proof.**</summary>

let any countable dense loset $C$ without endpoints that contains a countable dense subset $P$

- $\therefore P\cong \mathbb{Q}$
- $\therefore C\cong\mathbb{R}\enspace\square$
</details>

<details class="list-none">
<summary>**Proof.** $|\mathbb{R}|\gt\aleph_0$</summary>

$\because\mathbb{R}$ is dloset without endpoints

BWOC assume $|\mathbb{R}|=\aleph_0$

$\therefore\mathbb{R}\cong\mathbb{Q}$

but $\mathbb{R}$ is complete whereas $\mathbb{Q}$ is not $\enspace\square$
</details>
</details>
