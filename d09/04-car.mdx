## Cantor-Berstein

<details class="definition" open>
<summary>equipotence</summary>

$A$ and $B$ **equipotence**, denoted $|A|=|B|$

$$
|A|=|B|\iff\exists f:A\stackrel\sim\to B
$$

**Corollary.** equipotence is equivalence *relation*
</details>

<details class="definition" open>
<summary>less cardinality</summary>

$$
|A|\le|B|\iff\exists f:A\hookrightarrow B
$$

**Corollary.** *reflexive, transitive, antisymmetric*
</details>

<details class="theorem" open>
<summary>Cantor Berstein</summary>

for sets $A,B$

$$
|A|\le|B|\text{ and }|B|\le|A|\implies |A|=|B|
$$

<details class="list-none">
<summary>**Lemma.** $A_1\subseteq B\subseteq A$ and $|A_1|=|A|\implies |A|=|B|$</summary>

let $f:A\stackrel\sim\to A_1$

let $A_0,A_1,\cdots$ and $B_0,B_1,\cdots$ defined by Recursion Theorem

- let $A_0=A$ and $B_1=B$
- for any $n\in\mathbb{N}$

$$
A_{n+1}=f[A_n]\quad B_{n+1}=f[B_n]\quad C_n=A_n-B_n
$$

- let 

$$
C=\bigcup_{n=0}^\infty C_n\quad D=A-C
$$

thus

$$
\begin{align*}
    \because&&f[C_n]&=f[A_n-B_n]=f[A_n]-f[B_n]=A_{n+1}-B_{n+1}=C_{n+1}\\
    \therefore&&f[C]&=f\left[\bigcup_{n=0}^\infty C_n\right]=\bigcup_{n=0}^\infty f[C_n]=\bigcup_{n=1}^\infty C_n
\end{align*}
$$

let $g:A\to B$ where

$$
\begin{align*}
    g(x)&=f(x) && x\in C\\
    g(x)&=x&& x\in D
\end{align*}
$$

$\therefore g\upharpoonright C$ inj, and $g\upharpoonright D$ inj

$\because g[C]=f[C]\sube C$ disjoints $A-C=D=g[D]$

$\therefore g=g\upharpoonright C\sqcup g\upharpoonright D$ inj

$\therefore g:A\stackrel\sim\to\operatorname{ran}g=f[C]\cup D=B\enspace\square$
</details>

<details class="list-none">
<summary>**Proof.**</summary>

let $f:A\hookrightarrow B$ and $g:B\hookrightarrow A$

$\therefore(g\circ f)[A]\subseteq g[B]\subseteq A$ where $|A|=(g\circ f)[A]$ since $g\circ f$ injective

$\therefore|B|=|g[B]|=|A|\enspace\square$
</details>

**Corollary** $|X|\lt|\mathcal{P} (X)|$ for any nonempty $X$
</details>

<details class="definition" open>
<summary>cardinal number</summary>

for any set $X$, there is a unique **cardinal number** for $|X|$
</details>

## At Most Countable

<details class="definition" open>
<summary>finite</summary>

set $X$ **finite** $\iff |X|=n$ for some $n\in\mathbb{N}$

- **infinite** $\iff$ not finite
</details>

<details class="definition" open>
<summary>countable</summary>

set $X$ **countable** $\iff|X|=|\mathbb{N}|$ (i.e. has enumeration $(a_n)=f:\mathbb{N}\twoheadrightarrow A$)

set $X$ **at most** countable $\iff|X|\le|\mathbb{N}|$


</details>

<details class="theorem" open>
<summary></summary>

- infinite subset of countable is countable
- at most contable $\iff$ finite or countable
- countable union of countable is countable (axiom of choice)
- $A,B$ countable $\implies A\times B$ countable (axiom of choice)

<details class="list-none">
<summary>**Proof.** any system of nonempty disjoint open intervals is at most countable</summary>

let $\mathcal{C}\subseteq\mathcal{P}(\mathbb{R})$ be such system

let $f:\mathcal{C}\to \mathcal{P}(\mathbb{Q})$ be

$$
f(I)=I\cap\mathbb{Q}
$$

$\because\mathcal{C}$ mutually disjoint

$\therefore f[\mathcal{C}]$ mutually disjoint

$\because I\cap\mathbb{Q}$ nonempty for any $I\in\mathcal{C}$<span class="float-right">($\mathbb{Q}$ dense and $|I|\ge\aleph_0$)</span>

$\therefore f:\mathcal{C}\hookrightarrow \mathcal{P}(\mathbb{Q})$<span class="float-right">($\mathcal{C}$ mutually disjoint)</span>

$\therefore f:\mathcal{C}\stackrel\sim\to f[\mathcal{C}]$

$\because\exists g:f[\mathcal{C}]\to \mathbb{Q}$ such that<span class="float-right">(Choice)</span>

$$
g(f(I))\in f(I)\subseteq\mathbb{Q}
$$

$\because f[\mathcal{C}]=\{f(I):I\in\mathcal{C}\}$ mutually disjoint

$\therefore g:f[C]\hookrightarrow \mathbb{Q}$

$\therefore g\circ f:\mathcal{C}\hookrightarrow \mathbb{Q}$, thus $|\mathcal{C}|\le|\mathbb{Q}|=\aleph_0\enspace\square$

</details>

</details>


## Cardinal Arithmetic

<details class="definition" open>
<summary>addition</summary>

for disjoint sets $X,Y$

$$
|X|+|Y|=|X\cup Y|
$$
</details>

<details class="theorem" open>
<summary></summary>


- $0$ is additive identity
- associative, commutative, nondecreasing

$$
\kappa\le\mu\implies \kappa+\nu\le\mu+\nu
$$
under AoC, for $\kappa$ or $\mu$ infinite

$$
\kappa+\mu=\max\{\kappa,\mu\}
$$
</details>

<details class="definition" open>
<summary>multiplication</summary>

for sets $X,Y$

$$
|X||Y|=|X\times Y|
$$
</details>

<details class="theorem" open>
<summary></summary>

- $\kappa0=0\kappa=0$
- $\kappa\mu=0\implies\kappa=0\lor\mu=0$
- $1$ is multiplicative identity
- associative, commutative, nondecreasing
- distributive over addition

under AoC, for $\kappa$ or $\mu$ infinite and both nonzero

$$
\kappa\mu=\max\{\kappa,\mu\}
$$

</details>

<details class="definition" open>
<summary>exponentiation</summary>

for sets $X,Y$

$$
|X|^{|Y|}=|X^Y|
$$
</details>

<details class="theorem" open>
<summary></summary>

- $\kappa^0=1$, in particular $0^0=1$ (the empty function)
- for $\mu\ge1\quad 0^\mu=0$
- $1^\mu=1$
- $\kappa^1=\kappa$
- $\kappa^{\mu+\nu}=\kappa^\mu\kappa^\nu$
- $(\kappa\lambda)^\mu=\kappa^\mu\lambda^\mu$
- nondecreasing

$$
\begin{align*}
  1\le\kappa\text{ and }\nu\le\mu&\implies \kappa^\nu\le\kappa^\mu\\
  \kappa\le\lambda&\implies \kappa^\nu\le\lambda^\nu
\end{align*}
$$

under AoC
</details>
