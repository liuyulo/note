## Axioms

<details class="definition" open>
<summary>notation</summary>

- `[A-Z]` denotes sets (of sets)
- `[a-z]` denotes sets (as objects)
- $\in$ (membership) is a binary (meta) relation
- $\bold P$ denotes any meta relation
- assume classical logic
- assume law of excluded middle
</details>

<details class="definition" open>
<summary>ZFC axioms</summary>

existence of **empty set**

$$
\exists A\forall x(x\notin A)
$$

axiom of **extensionality**

$$
\forall A\forall B(\forall x( x\in A\lrarr x\in B )\to A=B)
$$

axiom of **comprehension** 
$$
\forall A\exists B(\forall x(x\in B\lrarr x\in A\land\bold P(x)))
$$
- denote such $B$ as $\{x\in A:\bold P(x)\}$


axiom of **pair**
$$
\forall a\forall b\exists C(\forall x(x\in C\lrarr x=a\lor x=b))
$$
- denote $C$ as $\{a,b\}$ or $\{a\}$ when $a=b$

axiom of **union**
$$
\forall S\exists U(\forall x(x\in U\lrarr\exists A(A\in S\land x\in A)))
$$
- denote such $U$ as $\cup S$

axiom of **powerset**
$$
\forall S\exists P(\forall x(x\in P\lrarr x\sube S))
$$
- denote such $P$ as $\mathcal P[S]$

axiom of **infinity**

<div class="flex justify-center">
an inductive set exists
</div>

axiom of **foudation**

<div class="flex justify-center">
all sets are well-founded, thus $\forall x(x\notin x)$
</div>

axiom of **replacement**

- for any $\bold P$ where $\forall x\exists!\bold F(x)$ such that $\bold P(x,\bold F(x))$

$$
\forall A\exists B\forall x(x\in A\implies \bold F(x)\in B)
$$

- denote such $B$ as $\{\bold F(x):x\in A\}$

axiom of **choice**

- for nonempty system $\mathcal{S}$ of nonempty sets
  - there is a *choice* function $f:\mathcal{S}\to\bigcup\mathcal{S}$ where

  $$
  \forall X\in\mathcal{S}\quad f(X)\in X
  $$
</details>

<details class="definition" open>
<summary>subset</summary>

for set $A$ and $B$
<div class="flex justify-center">

$A\sube B\iff\forall x(x\in A\lrarr x\in B)$
</div>
</details>
<details class="theorem" open>
<summary>unrestricted extensionality</summary>

<div class="flex justify-center">
$\exists A(\forall x(\bold P(x)\implies x\in A))\implies\{x:\bold P(x)\}$ is set
</div>
</details>

<details class="theorem" open>
<summary>pair any finite tuple</summary>
<details>
<summary>**Example.**</summary>
to make $\{a,b,c\}$ where $a,b,c$ distinct

- pair $a,b$ to $\{a,b\}$
- pair $c$ to $\{c\}$
- pair to $\{\{a,b\},\{c\}\}$
- union to $\{a,b,c\}$
</details>
</details>

## Elementary Operations

<details class="theorem" open>
<summary>subset</summary>
- $\sube$ is transitive and reflexive
- $A\sube B$ and $B\sube A\implies A=B$
</details>

<details class="definition" open>
<summary>set operations</summary>

| name | notation | definition
|:-:|:-:|:-:|
|intersection | $A\cap B$|$\{x\in A:x\in B\}$
|union |$A\cup B$|$\cup\{A,B\}$
|difference |$A-B$|$\{x\in A:x\notin B\}$
|symmetric difference |$A\triangle B$|$(A-B)\cup(B-A)$
</details>

<details class="theorem" open>
<summary></summary>
- $A\cap(B-C)=(A\cap B)-C$
- $A-B=\varnothing\iff A\sube B$
- $A\triangle A=\varnothing$
- $\triangle$ commutes and associates
</details>

<details class="definition" open>
<summary>intersection of nonempty system of sets</summary>
for non empty system of sets $S$

$$
\cap S=\{x:\forall A(A\in S\to x\in A)\}
$$
</details>
