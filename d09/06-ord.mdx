## Well Order

<details class="definition" open>
<summary>initial segment</summary>

for loset $(T,\lt)$

<div class="flex justify-center">

$S\subset T$ is an **initial segment** $\forall s\in S\quad\forall x\in T\quad x\lt s\implies x\in S$

</div>

for woset $(W,\lt)$

- the **initial segment** given by $a\in W$ is


$$
W[a]=\{x\in W:x\lt a\}
$$

**Lemma.** $W[a]$ is also woset

**Lemma.** any initial segment of woset $W$ is given by some $a\in W$

<details class="list-none">
<summary>**Proof.**</summary>

let any initial segment $S$ of any woset $W$

let $A=W-S$ be complement of $S$

$\therefore A$ nonempty<span class="float-right">($S\subset W$)</span>

$\therefore A$ has a least element $a\in W$<span class="float-right">($W$ well ordered)</span>

let any $x\in W$

- BWOC assume $x\lt a$ and $x\notin S$
  - $\therefore x\in A$, contradicts that $a$ in least in $A$
- BWOC assume $x\ge a$ and $x\in S$
  - $\therefore a\in S$, contradicts that $a\in A=W-S$<span class="float-right">($S$ is initial segment)</span>

$\therefore x\in S\iff x\lt a$, thus $S=W[a]\enspace\square$
</details>
</details>

<details class="theorem" open>
<summary>wosets are comparable</summary>

for any woset $W_1,W_2$, only one holds:

(a) $W_1\cong W_2$

(b) $W_1[a]\cong W_2$ for some $a\in W_1$

(c) $W_1\cong W_2[a]$ for some $a\in W_2$


in any case, the isomorphism is unique

<details>
<summary>**Lemma.** $\varphi(x)\ge x$ for any endo<span title="increasing function">morphism</span> $\varphi$ on any woset $W$ at any $x\in W$</summary>

let $A=\{x\in W:f(x)\lt x\}\subseteq W$

BWOC assume $A$ nonempty

$\therefore A$ has a least element $a\in A$<span class="float-right">($W$ well ordered)</span>

$\therefore \varphi(a)\lt a$<span class="float-right">($a\in A$)</span>

$\therefore \varphi(\varphi(a))\lt\varphi(a)$<span class="float-right">($\varphi$ homomorphism)</span>

$\therefore\varphi(a)\in A$, thus contradicts that $a$ is least in $A$

</details>

<details>
<summary>**Corollary.** (1) no woset is isomorphic to its initial segement</summary>

BWOC assume $\varphi:W\stackrel\sim\to W[a]$ for some $a\in W$

$\therefore \varphi(a)\in W[a]$, thus $\varphi(a)\lt a$<span class="float-right">($W[a]$ is initial segment given $a$)</span>

$\therefore$ contradicts lemma<span class="float-right">($\varphi$ is homomorphism from $W$ to $W\supseteq W[a]$)</span>

</details>

<details>
<summary>**Corollary.** (2) the only automorphism of any woset is identity</summary>

let any $\varphi:W\stackrel\sim\to W$, notice $\varphi^{-1}:W\stackrel\sim\to W$

let any $x\in W$

$\therefore \varphi(x)\ge x$ and $\varphi^{-1}(x)\ge x$<span class="float-right">(lemma)</span>

$\therefore\varphi(x)\ge x$ and $x\ge\varphi(x)$, thus $\varphi(x)=x$

thus $\varphi=1_W\enspace\square$

</details>

<details>
<summary>**Corollary.** (3) there is only one isomorphism between any isomorphic wosets</summary>

let any $\varphi,\psi:W\stackrel\sim\to V$

$\therefore\varphi^{-1}\circ\psi:W\stackrel\sim\to W$ is automorphism 

$\therefore\varphi^{-1}\circ\psi=1_W$ , thus $\varphi=\psi\enspace\square$

</details>

<details class="list-none">
<summary>**Proof.**</summary>

<details>
<summary>**1.** (a) (b) (c) mutually exclusive</summary>

BWOC assume $\varphi:W_1\stackrel\sim\to W_2[a_2]$ and $\psi:W_2\stackrel\sim\to W_1[a_1]$ for some $a_i\in W_i$

$\therefore\psi\circ\varphi:W_1\stackrel\sim\to W_1[a_1]$, contradicts corollary 1

</details>

- **2.** uniqueness of isomorphism is show in Cor. 3

let $\varphi\subseteq W_1\times W_2$ defined as

$$
\varphi=\{(a,b): W_1[a]\cong W_2[b]\}
$$

<details>
<summary>**3.** wts $\varphi$ is function</summary>

assume $a\varphi b$ and $a\varphi b'$ for any $(a,b),(a,b')\in\varphi$

$\therefore W_2[b]\cong W_1[a]\cong W_2[b']$

$\therefore b=b'$, otherwise one would be initial segment of the other, thus contradicts Cor. 1
</details>

<details>
<summary>**4.** wts $\varphi$ injective</summary>

assume $\varphi(a)=\varphi(a')$ for any $a,a'\in\operatorname{dom}\varphi$

$\therefore W_1[a]\cong W_2[\varphi(a)]= W_2[\varphi(a')]\cong W_1[a']$

$\therefore a=a'$ (otherwise contradicts Cor. 1)

</details>

<details>
<summary>**5.** wts $\varphi$ is homomorphism (aka increases)</summary>

let any $a,a'\in\operatorname{dom}\varphi$, assume $a\lt a'$

let $\psi:W_1[a']\stackrel\sim\to W_2[\varphi(a')]$

$\therefore\psi\upharpoonright W_1[a]:W_1[a]\stackrel\sim\to W_2[\psi(a)]$<span class="float-right">($W_1[a]\subseteq W_1[a']$)</span>

$\therefore\psi(a)=\varphi(a)$ where $\psi(a)\lt\psi(a')$<span class="float-right">($\psi$ homomorphism)</span>

$\therefore\varphi(a)\lt\varphi(a')$

</details>

$\therefore\varphi:\operatorname{dom}\varphi\stackrel\sim\to \operatorname{ran}\varphi$ where $\operatorname{dom}\varphi\subseteq W_1$ and $\operatorname{ran}\varphi\subseteq W_2$

<details>
<summary>**6.** wts not (a) and not (b) implies (c)</summary>

assume $\operatorname{dom}\varphi\ne W_1$

wts $\operatorname{dom}\varphi$ is initial segment of $W_1$

- let any $a\in\operatorname{dom}\varphi$ and any $x\in W_1$ such that $x\lt a$
- let $\psi:W_1[a]\stackrel\sim\to W_2[\varphi(a)]$
- $\therefore\psi\upharpoonright W_1[x]\stackrel\sim\to W_2[\psi(x)]$
- $\therefore (x,\psi(x))\in \varphi$, thus $x\in\operatorname{dom}\varphi$

let $W_1[a]=\operatorname{dom}\varphi$ for some $a\in W_1$

wts $\operatorname{ran}\varphi=W_2$, BWOC assume not

- $\therefore\varphi:W_1[a]\stackrel\sim\to W_1[b]$ for some $b\in W_2$
- $\therefore (a,b)\in\varphi$
- $\therefore a\in\operatorname{dom}\varphi W_1[a]$, thus $a\lt a$, a contradiction $\enspace\square$
</details>
</details>

## Ordinal

<details class="definition" open>
<summary>ordinal</summary>

set $\alpha$ is an **ordinal** number iff

- $(\alpha,\in_\alpha)$ is woset
- $\alpha$ is transitive
  - $a\in \alpha\implies a\subseteq\alpha$
  - $a\in b\in \alpha\implies a\in \alpha$

<details class="list-none">
<summary>**Proof.** any element of an ordinal is ordinal</summary>

let any element $a$ in any any ordinal $\alpha$

wts $a$ transitive, let any $u\lt v\lt a$, wts $u\lt a$

- $\because a\subseteq\alpha$<span class="float-right">($\alpha$ transitive)</span>
- $\therefore v\in \alpha$, thus $v\subseteq\alpha$<span class="float-right">($\alpha$ transitive)</span>
- $\therefore u\in\alpha$
- $\therefore u\lt a$<span class="float-right">($u,v,a\in\alpha$ where $\alpha$ is loset)</span>

wts $a$ woset

- $\because a\subseteq\alpha$ where $\alpha$ is woset
- $\therefore a$ is woset

$\therefore a$ ordinal $\enspace\square$
</details>

<details class="list-none">
<summary>**Proof.** no ordinal contains itself</summary>

BWOC assume $\alpha\in\alpha$

- $\therefore$ for $a=\alpha,b=\alpha\in \alpha$, we have that $a\in_\alpha b$ and $b\in_\alpha a$
- $\therefore$ contradicts $\in_\alpha$ asymmetric
</details>

<details class="list-none">
<summary>**Proof.**  any element of an ordinal is an initial segment</summary>

let any element $\beta$ in any ordinal $\alpha$

$\because\beta$ is ordinal

$\therefore\beta=\{\gamma\in \alpha:\gamma\lt\beta\}=\alpha[\beta]$

</details>

<details class="list-none">
<summary>**Proof.** any strict subset of an ordinal is an element</summary>

assume $\alpha\subset\beta$, wts $\alpha\in\beta$

$\because \varnothing\subset\beta-\alpha\subset\beta$ where $\beta$ is woset

$\therefore\beta-\alpha$ has some least element $\gamma\in\beta$

wts $\alpha=\gamma$, thus $\alpha\in\beta$

$(\subseteq)$ BWOC assume $\exists\delta\in\gamma-\alpha$

- $\therefore\delta\in\beta$<span class="float-right">($\delta\in\gamma\in\beta$ where $\beta$ transitive)</span>
- $\therefore\delta\in\beta-\alpha$ where $\delta\lt\gamma$
- $\therefore$ contradicts $\gamma$ is least of $\beta-\alpha$

$(\supseteq)$ BWOC assume $\exists\delta\in\alpha-\gamma$

- $\therefore\delta\ge\gamma$<span class="float-right">($\delta,\gamma\in\beta$ where $\beta$ loset)</span>
- $\therefore\gamma\lt\alpha$<span class="float-right">($\gamma\le\delta\lt\alpha$ where $\in_\beta$ transitive)</span>
- $\therefore$ contradicts that $\gamma\in\beta-\alpha$, that is, $\gamma\notin\alpha\enspace\square$

</details>

omega

$$
\omega\stackrel{\text{def}}=\mathbb{N}
$$

less than

$$
\alpha\lt\beta\iff\alpha\in\beta
$$
</details>

<details class="theorem" open>
<summary></summary>

- every natural number is an ordinal
- $\mathbb{N}$ is an ordinal
</details>


<details class="definition" open>
<summary>successor</summary>

the successor of ordinal $\alpha$ is

$$
\alpha+1\stackrel{\text{def}}=\mathbf S(\alpha)\stackrel{\text{def}}=\alpha\cup\{\alpha\}
$$

an ordinal is a **successor** ordinal iff its the successor of some ordinal

an ordinal is a **limit** ordinal iff is not successor ordinal

<details class="list-none">
<summary>**Proof.** any successor of ordinal is ordinal</summary>

let any ordinal $\alpha$

① $\alpha+1$ is a set<span class="float-right">(Union)</span>

② wts $\alpha+1$ transitive

- let any $a\in\alpha+1$
- $\therefore a\subseteq\alpha$<span class="float-right">($\alpha$ transitive)</span>
- $\therefore a\subseteq\alpha+1$

③ wts $\alpha+1$ woset

- wts $\in_{\alpha+1}$ transitive, let any $a\in b\in c$ in $\alpha+1$
  - $\therefore b\subseteq c$<span class="float-right">($c\in\alpha$ is ordinal, thus transitive)</span>
  - $\therefore a\in c$<span class="float-right">($a\in b\subseteq c$)</span>

- wts $\in_{\alpha+1}$ asymmetric, let any $a\in b$ is $\alpha+1$

  - $\therefore b\subseteq\alpha$<span class="float-right">($\alpha$ transitive)</span>
  - $\therefore a\in \alpha$<span class="float-right">($a\in b\subseteq\alpha$)</span>
  - $\therefore a\subseteq\alpha$<span class="float-right">($\alpha$ transitive)</span>
  - BWOC assume $b\in a$
  - $\therefore b\in\alpha$<span class="float-right">($b\in a\subseteq\alpha$)</span>
  - $\therefore$ contradicts asymmetry in $(\alpha,\in_\alpha)$
- wts $\in_{\alpha+1}$ linear
  - 1. any pair in $\alpha$ is comparable by $\in_\alpha$
  - 2. any $a\in \alpha$ is comparable with $\alpha$, by $a\in \alpha$
- wts $\in_{\alpha+1}$ well order
  - let any nonempty $A\subseteq\alpha+1$
  - case $A\subseteq\alpha$, thus has least element since $\alpha$ is woset
  - case $A=\{\alpha\}$, thus $\alpha$ is least of $A$
  - case $A=B\cup\{\alpha\}$ for some nonempty $B\subseteq \alpha$
    - $\therefore B$ has some least element $\beta\in\alpha$
    - $\therefore\beta$ is least of $A$<span class="float-right">($\beta\lt\alpha$)</span>

$\therefore\alpha+1$ is woset $\enspace\square$
</details>

<details class="list-none">
<summary>**Proof.** ordinal is successor iff has greatest element</summary>

$(\implies )$ any successor ordinal is in form $\beta+1$, where $\beta$ is the greatest element of $\beta+1=\beta\cup\{\beta\}$

$(\impliedby )$ let $\beta$ be the greatest element of any ordinal $\alpha$

wts $\alpha=\beta\cup\{\beta\}$

$(\subseteq)$ let any $\delta\in\alpha$

- $\therefore\delta\le\beta$, thus $\delta\in\beta$ or $\delta=\beta$<span class="float-right">($\beta$ is upper bound in $\alpha$)</span>

- $\therefore\delta\in\beta\cup\{\beta\}$

$(\supseteq)$ 

- $\because\beta\in\alpha$
- $\therefore\beta\subseteq\alpha$ <span class="float-right">($\alpha$ transitive)</span>
- $\therefore\beta\cup\{\beta\}\subseteq\alpha\enspace\square$

</details>
</details>

## Ordinals are Well Ordered

<details class="theorem" open>
<summary>$\in$ on ordinals is</summary>

- *transitive*
- *asymmetric*
- *comparable*
- *well-ordered*

<details class="list-none">
<summary>**Proof.** *transitive, asymmetric*</summary>

1. $\alpha\lt\beta\lt\gamma\implies \alpha\lt\gamma$ since $\gamma$ is transitive

2. BWOC assume $\alpha\lt\beta$ and $\beta\lt\alpha$

   - $\therefore \alpha\lt\alpha$ by *transitivity*
   - $\therefore\alpha\in\alpha$, contradicts $\alpha\notin\alpha$
</details>

<details class="list-none">
<summary>**Proof.** *comparable*</summary>

let any ordinal $\alpha,\beta$

$\therefore\alpha\cap\beta\subseteq\alpha$ and $\alpha\cap\beta\subseteq\beta$

case $\alpha\cap\beta=\alpha$, thus $\alpha\subseteq\beta$ and $\alpha\le\beta$

case $\alpha\cap\beta=\beta$, thus $\beta\subseteq\alpha$, thus $\alpha\le\beta$

BWOC assume $\alpha\cap\beta\subset\alpha$ and $\alpha\cap\beta\subset\beta$

- $\therefore\alpha\cap\beta\subset\alpha\cap\beta$ where $\alpha\cap\beta$ is ordinal

- $\therefore \alpha\cap \beta\in\alpha\cap\beta$, contradicts $\alpha\cap\beta\notin\alpha\cap \beta$
</details>

<details class="list-none">
<summary>**Proof.** *well-ordered*</summary>

let any nonempty set $A$ of ordinals

$\therefore\exists \alpha\in A$

case $\alpha\cap A$ empty, wts $\alpha$ is least of $A$

- BWOC assume $\exists\beta\in A$ such that $\beta\lt \alpha$
- $\therefore \beta\in \alpha$ and $\beta\in A$
- $\therefore\beta\in\alpha\cap A$, contradicts $\alpha\cap A$ empty

case $\alpha\cap A$ nonempty, thus has some least $\beta\in\alpha$

- wts $\beta$ is least of $A$, BWOC assume $\exists\gamma\in A$ such that $\gamma\in\beta$
- $\therefore\gamma\ge\alpha$, thus $\alpha\le\gamma\lt\beta$<span class="float-right">($\beta$ is least of $\alpha\cap A$)</span>
- $\therefore\alpha\lt\beta$<span class="float-right">(*transitivity*)</span>
- $\therefore$ contradicts *asymmetric*<span class="float-right">($\beta\lt\alpha$)</span>
</details>
</details>

<details class="theorem" open>
<summary>no set of ordinals contains all ordinals</summary>

<details class="list-none">
<summary>**Lemma.** for any set $A$ of ordinals, its supremum is $\sup A=\bigcup A$</summary>

1. for any $\alpha\in A$
   - $\therefore \alpha\subseteq\bigcup A$, thus $\alpha\le\bigcup A$
2. for any upper bound $\beta$ of $A$
   - $\therefore\alpha\subseteq\beta$ for any $\alpha\in A$
   - $\therefore\bigcup A\subseteq\beta$, thus $\bigcup A\le\beta$
</details>

<details class="list-none">
<summary>**Lemma.** $\sup A=\bigcup A$ is ordinal</summary>

wts $\bigcup A$ transitive

- let any $a\in \bigcup A$
- $\therefore\exists \alpha\in A$  such that $a\in \alpha$
- $\therefore a\subseteq\alpha\subseteq\bigcup A$<span class="float-right">($\alpha$ ordinal)</span>
- $\therefore a\subseteq\bigcup A$

wts $\bigcup A$ woset

- $\because\forall a\in\bigcup A\quad\exists\alpha\in A\quad a\in \alpha$ where $\alpha$ is ordinal, thus $a$ is ordinal
- $\therefore\bigcup A$ is set of ordinals, thus woset
</details>

<details class="list-none">
<summary>**Proof.** $\sup A+1\notin A$</summary>

let $\alpha=\sup A+1\gt\sup A$

BWOC assume $\alpha\in A$

- $\therefore \alpha\subseteq\bigcup A$
- $\therefore \alpha\le\sup A\lt\alpha$
- $\therefore \alpha\lt\alpha$, contradicts *asymmetry*

</details>
</details>

<details class="theorem" open>
<summary>any woset is isomorphic to an unique ordinal</summary>

<details class="list-none">
<summary>**Lemma.** isomorphic ordinals are equal</summary>

BWOC assume $\alpha\cong\beta$ but $\alpha\ne\beta$

WLOC assume $\alpha\lt\beta$<span class="float-right">(ordinal *comparable*)</span>

$\therefore \alpha\in\beta$ is an initial segment of $\beta$, contradicts that $\alpha\cong\beta$

- (no woset is isomorphic to its initial segment)

$\therefore\alpha=\beta$

</details>

<details class="list-none">
<summary>**Proof.**</summary>

let any woset $W$

let $A$ be set of all $a\in W$ for which $W[a]$ is isomorphic to some ordinal

$\therefore$ for any $a\in A$, there is an unique ordinal $\alpha_a$ isomorphic to $W[a]$<span class="float-right">(Lemma)</span>

let $\alpha=\{\alpha_a\}_{a\in A}$<span class="float-right">(Replacement)</span>

$\therefore(\alpha,\in_\alpha)$ is woset<span class="float-right">($\alpha$ is set of ordinals)</span>

wts $\alpha$ transitive

- let any $\beta\in\alpha_a\in\alpha$
- let $\varphi:W[a]\stackrel\sim\to\alpha_a$ be isomophism<span class="float-right">($a\in A$)</span>

<details>
<summary>let $b=\varphi^{-1}(b)$, thus $W[b]\cong\beta$ by $\varphi\upharpoonright W[b]$</summary>

let any $c\in W[b]$, that is, $c\lt b$

$\therefore\varphi(c)\lt\varphi(b)=\beta$, where $\varphi(c),\beta\in\alpha_a$

$\therefore\varphi(c)\in\beta$
</details>

$\therefore\alpha$ ordinal

let isomorphism $\varphi:A\stackrel\sim\to\alpha$ by $\varphi(a)=\alpha_a$

$\because A,W$ are wosets and $A\subseteq W$

$\therefore A=W$ or $A=W[c]$ for some $c\in W$

BWOC assume $A=W[c]$

- $\therefore W[c]\cong\alpha$ by $\varphi$, thus $c\in A$, contradicts that $c\notin W[c]$<span class="float-right">(asymmetry)</span>

$\therefore A=W$, thus $W\cong\alpha$ by $\varphi\enspace\square$
</details>

</details>
