## 14 Reliability Theory of Knowledge
<details class="theorem"><summary>Reliability Theory of Knowledge</summary>
\begin{align*}
  S\textsf{ knows P}\iff&\,P\textsf{ is true}\land S\textsf{ believes }P\\
  &\land S\textsf{ believes }P\implies P\textsf{ is true}\tag{\sf reliable}\\
  &\textsf{(in the circumstance where $S$ is in)}
\end{align*}
</details>
<details class="example" open><summary>Thermometer</summary>

- in a given circumstance (**right environment** and **correct interal makeup**)<br>
a thermometer is reliable
- $\therefore$ if $P$ is not true<br>then I won't believe $P$ is true through the **reliable** thermometer
</details>
