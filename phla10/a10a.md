- ∵ deductive reasoning is using one or more premises to reach a logical conclusion
- inductive reasoning is making inferences using a sample of a population
- ∴ a deductively valid argument can give absolute guarantee

- the logic of confirmation is to use an observation to confirm what a theory predicts
- the logic of disconfirmation is to use an observation that doesnt follow what the theory predicts to falsify it
- the logic of disconfirmation is deductively valid because the counterexample creates a contradiction, which implies that the theory is false.
</details>

- a structure of statement that is designed to prove a point
- consists of premises and a conclusion
</details>

## Surprise Principle
> O favors H1 over H2 only if when H1 is true, O is expected, and when H2 is true, O is not expected<br>
> aka relative probability

suppose observation `O` and hypothesis `H1` and `H2`
<div class="inference"></div>
- `H1 -> O` expected
- `H2 -> ¬O` expected
- `O` is strongly favor `H1` voer `H2`

example: `H1` = has heartattack, `H2` = `¬H1`

- bad `O` = has a heart <div class="inference"></div>
  - `H1 -> O` expected
  - `H2 -> O` expected
  - `O` is not a **strong evidence** of `H1`
- good `O` = erratic ECG <div class="inference"></div>
  - `H1 -> O` expected 
  - `H2 -> O` not expected
  - `O` is a **strong evidence** of `H1`

# 9 Testability of God's Existence

## Testability Theory of Meaning
> if `S` is a meaningful sentence, then it is possible to figure out whether `S` is true<br>

i.e. if `S` is *a priori* or *a posteriori*, then `S` is meaningful

- positivists: "God ∃" is neither a priori nor a posteriori, ∴ the claim is meaningless

## Analytical vs Synthetic Truth

- **analytical truth** are decidable from definition alone (truth is contained within the definition of the terms in it)
  - e.g. people die if they are killed, people are born on their birthday
  - positivists: all a priori truth are analytical
- **synthetic truth** is not analytical truth
  - e.g. I got 4.0 in PHLA10
	- positivists: all a posteriori truth are synthetic
- positivists:
	- "God ∃" cant be decided from definition alone ∴ not analytical
	- "God ∃" makes no predictions, ∴ not testable ∴ not synthetic
	- if a theory make a prediction that can be checked observationally, then it runs the risk of being refuted by observations
