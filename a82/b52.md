# 2D ChVar
<details class="definition" open><summary>for $X,Y$ and $h:\R^2\to\R^2$</summary>
$$h(X,Y)=(h_1(X,Y),h_2(X,Y))=(Z,W)$$
</details>
<details class="algorithm" open><summary>PMF Method</summary>
for discrete $X,Y$ with joint PMF $p_{XY}(x,y)$<br>
the joint PMF of $(Z,W)=h(X,Y)$ is
$$p_{ZW}(z,w)=P(Z=z,W=w)=\sum_{(x,y)\in h^{-1}(z,w)}p_{XY}(x,y)$$

where $h^{-1}(x,y)=\{(x,y):h_1(x,y)=z\enspace h_2(x,y)=w\}$

- i.e. $h^{-1}(x,y)=(h_1^{-1}(x,y),h_2^{-1}(x,y))$
</details>
<details class="algorithm" open><summary>CDF Method</summary>
for $X,Y$ with $F_{XY}(x,y)$, CDF of $(Z,W)=h(X,Y)$

$$F_{ZW}(z,w)=P(h_1(X,Y)\le z,h_2(X,Y)\le w)$$
</details>
<details class="example"><summary>CDF of $Z=aX+b,W=cY+d$ wrt $F_{XY}$</summary>
$$\begin{align*}
  F_{ZW}(z,w)&=P(Z\le z,W\le w)=P(aX+b\le z,cY+d\le w)\\
  &=P\left(X\le\frac{z-b}a,Y\le\frac{w-d}c\right)=F_{XY}\left(\frac{z-b}a,\frac{w-d}c\right)
\end{align*}$$
</details>
<details class="example"><summary>CDF Method, recognise expectation</summary>
given $f_{XY}(x,y)=e^{-y}\quad 0<x<y<\infty$ find $Z=X/Y$

$$\begin{align*}
  F_Z(z)&=P(Z\le z)=P(X/Y\le z)=P(X\le zY)\\
  &=\int_0^{\infty}\int_0^{zy}e^{-y}\,dx\,dy=z\int_0^{\infty}ye^{-y}\,dy\\
  &=z\tag{$E(\textsf{Exp}(1))$}\\
  \therefore Z&\sim U(0,1)
\end{align*}$$
</details>
<details class="algorithm" open><summary>PDF Method</summary>
for $f_{XY}(x,y)$ with $(Z,W)=h(X,Y)$

- where $h_i$ is **injective** and **diffable**

$$f_{ZW}(z,w)=\frac{f_{XY}(h^{-1}(z,w))}{|J(h^{-1}(z,w))|}$$

- where 

$$J(x,y)=|Dh(x,y)|=\begin{vmatrix}\dfrac{\partial h_1}{\partial x}&\dfrac{\partial h_2}{\partial x}\\[1em]\dfrac{\partial h_1}{\partial y}&\dfrac{\partial h_2}{\partial y}\end{vmatrix}$$
</details>

# Order Statistics
<details class="definition" open><summary>Ordered Value</summary>
for $(X_i)$ as a random sample (iid RV)

- $X_{(i)}$ is the ith smallest value
- e.g. $X_{(1)}=\textsf{min}(X_i)$
- e.g. $X_{(n)}=\textsf{max}(X_i)$
</details>
<details class="theorem" open><summary>Distribution of Max and Min</summary>
for iid $(X_i)$ with cdf $F(x)$ and pdf $f(x)$

- distribution of $X_{(n)}$ (max)
   $$F_{(n)}(x)=F(x)^n\quad f_{(n)}(x)=nF(x)^{n-1}f(x)$$
- distribution of $X_{(1)}$ (min)
  $$F_{(1)}(x)=1-(1-F(x))^n\quad f_{(1)}(x)=n(1-F(x))^{n-1}f(x)$$
- better use CDF to recognise distribution of min/max
</details>
<details class="theorem" open><summary>Distribution of k-th order</summary>
for iid $(X_i)$ with cdf $F(x)$ and pdf $f(x)$

- $\displaystyle  F_{X_{(k)}}(x)=\sum_{i=k}^n\binom niF(x)^i(1-F(x))^{n-i}$
- $\displaystyle f_{X_{(k)}}(x)=\frac{n!}{(k-1)!(n-k)!}F(x)^{k-1}(1-F(x))^{n-k}f(x)$
</details>
<details class="example" open><summary>$(U_i)_1^n$ idd $U(0,1)$, recognise $X=U_{(k)}$</summary>
$$\begin{align*}
\because f_{U_i}(x)&=1\quad F_{U_i}(x)=x\\
  f_{U_{(k)}}(x)&=\frac{n!}{(k-1)!(n-k)!}F(x)^{k-1}(1-F(x)^{n-k})f(x)\\
&=\frac{n!}{(k-1)!(n-k)!}x^{k-1}(1-x)^{n-k}\\
&=\frac{\Gamma(n+1)}{\Gamma(k)\Gamma(n-k+1)}x^{k-1}(1-x)^{n-k}\\
\therefore U_{(k)}&\sim\textsf{Beta}(k,n-k+1)\\
\end{align*}$$
</details>

# 4 Sampling Distributions

## Central Limit Theorem
<details class="theorem" open><summary>CLT</summary>
for any **iid** $(X_i)_1^n$ with same $|\mu|<\infty$ and same $\sigma^2<\infty$

$$Z_n=\frac{\overline X_n-\mu}{\sigma/\sqrt n}\stackrel D\to N(0,1)$$

$\therefore\overline X_n\stackrel{appr}\sim N(\mu,\sigma^2/n)$ for large $n$
<br><br>
for any $n$

- $(X_i)_1^n$ iid $N(\mu,\sigma^2)\implies \overline X_n\stackrel D\to N(\mu,\sigma^2/n)$
</details>

## Large Number Laws
<details class="theorem" open><summary>WLLN vs SLLN</summary>
**Weak**: for any independent $(X_i)$ with same $\mu$ and $\sigma_i^2<\infty$
$$\overline X_n\stackrel P\rarr\mu$$

**Strong**: for any iid $(X_i)$ with same $|\mu|\le\infty$
$$\overline X_n\stackrel{a.s}\to\mu$$
</details>

## 4.2 Convergence
<details class="definition" open><summary>Converges in Probability</summary>
$(X_n)$ **converges in probability** to $X$ iff
: $\displaystyle\forall\varepsilon>0\enspace\lim_{n\to\infty}P(|X_n-X|\ge\varepsilon)=0$
: $X_n\stackrel P\rarr X$

Theorem: for any continuous $g:\R\to\R$
$$X_n\stackrel P\rarr X\implies g(X_n)\stackrel P\rarr g(X)$$
</details>
<details class="definition" open><summary>Converges in Distribution</summary>
$$\forall x\enspace P(X=x)=0\implies\lim_{n\to\infty}P(X_n\le x)=P(X\le x)$$

i.e. $X_n\stackrel D\rarr X$
</details>
<details class="definition" open><summary>Converges Almost Surely</summary>
$$P\left(\lim_{n\to\infty} X_n=X\right)=1$$

i.e. $X_n\stackrel{a.s}\rarr X$
</details>
<details class="theorem" open><summary>Implication of Convergences</summary>
$$X_n\stackrel{a.s}\rarr X\implies X_n\stackrel P\rarr X\implies X_n\stackrel D\rarr X$$
</details>
<details class="definition" open><summary>Average (Sample Mean)</summary>
for any $(X_i)$ with common mean $\mu$ and common variance $\sigma^2$

$$\overline X_n=\frac1n\sum X_i$$

then $E(\overline X_n)=\mu\quad V(\overline X_n)=\frac{\sigma^2}n$
</details>
<details class="definition" open><summary>Sample Variance</summary>
for $(X_i)$ as random sample, the sample variance is

$$S^2=\frac1{n-1}\sum(X_i-\overline X)^2$$
</details>
<details class="example"><summary>let $(X_i)$ iid $N(\mu,\sigma^2)$, find mgf of $\overline X_n$</summary>
$$\begin{align*}
  \because m_{X_i}(t)&=e^{\mu t+\frac12\sigma^2t^2}\\
  \therefore m_{\sum X_i}(t)&=\prod m_{X_i}(t)=m_{X_i}(t)^n=e^{\mu nt+\frac12n\sigma^2t^2}\tag{\sf iid}\\
  \therefore m_{\overline X_n}(t)&=m_{\frac1n\sum X_i}(t)=e^{\mu n(\frac tn)+\frac12n\sigma^2\left(\frac tn\right)^2}=e^{{\color{orange}\mu }t+\frac12{\color{orange}\frac{\sigma^2}n}t^2}\enspace t\in\R\\
  \therefore\overline X_n&\sim N(\mu,\sigma^2/n)
\end{align*}$$
</details>

## 3.6 Inequality
<details class="theorem" open><summary>Markov's Inequality (Markov's Upper Bound)</summary>
for any $X\ge0$ and $a>0$
$$P(X\ge a)\le\frac{E(X)}a$$
</details>
<details class="theorem" open><summary>Chebychev's Inequality (Chebychev's Upper Bound)</summary>
for any $X$ with $|\mu_X|<\infty$ and $a>0$

$$P(|X-\mu_X|\ge a)\le\frac{V(X)}{a^2}$$
</details>
<details class="theorem" open><summary>Cauchy-Schwarts Inequality</summary>
$$E(XY)^2\le E(X^2)E(Y^2)\quad\textsf{Cov}(X,Y)^2\le V(X)V(Y)$$
</details>
<details class="theorem" open><summary>Jensen Inequality</summary>
for any convex $g:\R\to\R$ (i.e. $\forall x\enspace g''(x)>0$)

$$E(g(X))\ge g(E(X))$$
</details>
<details class="example"><summary>Jensen: if concave, use negative to find upper</summary>

given $X>0$ with $E(X)=0$<br>
find upper of $E(\ln\sqrt X)$ with Jensen

$$\begin{align*}
  E(\ln\sqrt X)&=E\left(\frac12\ln X\right)=-\frac12E(-\ln X)=-\frac12E(g(X))\\
  &\le-\frac12g(E(X))\tag{$-\ln X\textsf{ is convex}$}\\
  &=-\frac12g(10)=\frac12\ln10
\end{align*}$$
</details>

## 3.4 Generating Functions
<details class="definition" open><summary>Unique PGF $r_X:\R\to\R$ and Unique MGF $m_X:\R\to\R$ for each dist</summary>
Probability Generating Function $r_X(t)=E(t^X)=m_X(\ln t)$

Moment Generating Function $m_X(t)=E(e^{tX})=r_X(e^t)$

: $m_{X\sim N(\mu,\sigma^2)}(t)=e^{\mu t+\frac12\sigma^2t^2}$

<details><summary>**Proof.** $m_{X\sim N(0,1)}(t)=e^{\frac12t^2}$</summary>
$$\begin{align*}
  m_X(t)&=E(e^{tX})=\int_{-\infty}^\infty e^{tx}\frac1{\sqrt{2\pi}}e^{-\frac12x^2}\,dx=\int_{-\infty}^\infty\frac1{\sqrt{2\pi}}e^{-\frac12x^2+tx}\,dx\\
  &=\int_{-\infty}^\infty\frac1{\sqrt{2\pi}}e^{-\frac12(x^2-2tx+t^2)+\frac12t^2}\,dx=\int_{-\infty}^\infty\frac1{\sqrt{2\pi}}e^{-\frac12(x-t)^2}e^{\frac12t^2}\,dx\\
  &=e^{\frac12t^2}\int_{-\infty}^\infty\frac1{\sqrt{2\pi}}e^{-\frac12(x-t)^2}\,dx=e^{\frac12t^2}\\
\end{align*}$$
</details>
</details>
<details class="theorem" open><summary></summary>
$$\begin{align*} r_X^{(k)}(0)&=k!P(X=k)\\ m_X^{(k)}(0)&=E(X^k)\\ \end{align*}$$
: $m_{a+bX}(t)=e^{at}m_X(bt)$
: $(\perp X_i)\implies m_{\sum X_i}(t)=\prod m_X(t)$

<details class="example"><summary>$X\sim\textsf{Uni}(0,1)$ recognise $Y=-\ln X\sim\textsf{Exp}(1)$ by MGF</summary>

<details class="example"><summary>$Z\sim N(0,1)$ recognise $X=Z^2\sim\textsf{G}(1/2,1/2)$ by MGF</summary>
$$\begin{align*}
  m_X(t)&=E\left(e^{tX}\right)=E\left(e^{tZ^2}\right)=\int_{-\infty}^{\infty}e^{tz^2}\frac1{\sqrt{2\pi}}e^{\frac12z^2}\,dz\\
  &=\int_{-\infty}^{\infty}\frac1{\sqrt{2\pi}}e^{-\frac12(1-2t)z^2}\,dz\tag{\sf normal dist}\\
  \textsf{let }1-2t&=\frac1{\sigma^2}\implies\sigma=\left(\frac1{1-2t}\right)^{1/2}\\
  &=\sigma\int_{-\infty}^{\infty}\frac1{\sigma\sqrt{2\pi}}e^{\frac12\frac{z^2}{\sigma^2}}\,dz=\sigma\tag{\sf pdf of $N(0,\sigma^2)$}\\
  &=\left(\frac1{1-2t}\right)^{1/2}=\left(\frac{1/2}{1/2-t}\right)^{1/2}\\
  \therefore Z^2&\sim\textsf{Gamma}(1/2,1/2)
\end{align*}$$
</details>

## 3.3 Variance $\sigma_X^2$
<details class="definition" open><summary>Variance $V(X)$</summary>
$$V(X)=E((X-E(X))^2)=E(X^2)-\mu_X^2=\sigma_X^2$$
where $\mu_X=E(X)$ and $\sqrt{\sigma^2}=\sigma$ is **Standard Deviation** (SD)

- $V(aX+b)=a^2V(X)+V(b)=a^2V(X)$
- $X\perp Y\implies V(X+Y)=V(X)+V(Y)$
</details>
<details class="definition" open><summary>Covariance and Correlation</summary>
Covariance: 
$$\mathrm{Cov}(X,Y)=E((X-\mu_X)(Y-\mu_y))=E(XY)-E(X)E(Y)$$

- $\mathrm{Cov}(aX+bY,c+Z)=a\mathrm{Cov}(X,Z)+b\mathrm{Cov}(Y,Z)$
- $\mathrm{Cov}(X,X)=V(X)$
- $\displaystyle V\left(\sum X_i\right)=\sum V(X_i)+2\sum_{i<j}\mathrm{Cov}(X_i,X_j)$
- $X\perp Y\implies\mathrm{Cov}(X,Y)=0$

Correlation: 
$$\rho_{XY}=\dfrac{\mathrm{Cov}(X,Y)}{\sigma_X\sigma_Y}\in[-1,1]$$

</details>

## 2.8 Conditional Distributions

### 2.8.3 Indep RV
<details class="theorem" open><summary>$X\perp Y$ iff</summary>
$$\begin{align*}
  P(a\le X\le b,c\le Y\le d)&=P(a\le X\le b)P(c\le Y\le d)\\
  p_{XY}(x,y)&=p_X(x)p_Y(y)\quad f_{XY}(x,y)=f_X(x)f_Y(y)\\
  p_{Y|X}(y|x)&=p_Y(y)\quad f_{Y|X}(y|x)=f_Y(y)
\end{align*}$$

- $X\perp Y\implies\forall f,g:\R\to\R\enspace f(X)\perp g(Y)$
- $p_{XY}(x,y)=g(x)h(y)$ or $f_{XY}(x,y)=g(x)h(y)\implies X\perp Y$
</details>
<details class="example"><summary>given $X\sim\textsf{Exp}(\lambda_1)\perp Y\sim\textsf{Exp}(\lambda_2)$, find $P(X<Y)$</summary>
:::{style="width:50%"}
$$\usetikzlibrary{patterns}\begin{tikzpicture}[scale=4]
    \draw[->](-1/8,0)--(1.25,0)node[anchor=north east]{$X$};
    \draw[->](0,-1/8)--(0,1.25)node[anchor=north east]{$Y$};
    \draw(1/2,1/2)node[anchor=north west]{$Y>X$};
    \draw(0,0)--(1.25,1.25);
    \draw[pattern=north east lines](1.2,1.2)--(0,0)--(0,1.2);
\end{tikzpicture}$$
:::
$$\begin{align*}
  P(X<Y)&=\int_0^\infty\int_0^yf_{XY}(x,y)\,dx\,dy=\int_0^\infty\int_0^yf_X(x)f_Y(y)\,dx\,dy\\
  &=\int_0^\infty\int_0^y\lambda_1e^{-\lambda_1x}\underline{\lambda_2e^{-\lambda_2y}}\,dx\,dy\\
  &=\int_0^\infty\underline{\lambda_2e^{-\lambda_2y}}\int_0^y\lambda_1e^{-\lambda_1x}\,dx\,dy\\
  &=\int_0^\infty\lambda_2e^{-\lambda_2y}\left[-e^{-\lambda_1x}\right]_0^y\,dy=\int_0^\infty\lambda_2e^{-\lambda_2y}(1-e^{-\lambda_1y})\,dy\\
  &=\int_0^\infty\left(\lambda_2e^{-\lambda_2y}-\lambda_2e^{-(\lambda_1+\lambda_2)y}\right)\,dy\\
  &=1-\left[-\frac{\lambda_2}{\lambda_1+\lambda_2}e^{-(\lambda_1+\lambda_2)y}\right]_0^\infty=1-\frac{\lambda_2}{\lambda_1+\lambda_2}\\
  &=\frac{\lambda_1}{\lambda_1+\lambda_2}
\end{align*}$$
</details>

## 2.7 Multivariate Distributions

### 2.7 Multivariate Discrete
<details class="definition" open><summary>Joint CDF $F_{XY}:\R^2\to[0,1]$ of $X$ and $Y$</summary>
$$F_{XY}(x,y)=P(X\le x,Y\le y)=P(\{X\le x\}\cap\{Y\le y\})$$
</details>
<details class="definition" open><summary>Marginal CDF from joint CDF $F_{XY}$</summary>
$$\begin{align*}
  F_X(x)&=\lim_{y\to\infty}F_{XY}(x,y)\\
  F_X(y)&=\lim_{x\to\infty}F_{XY}(x,y)\\
\end{align*}$$
</details>
<details class="definition" open><summary>Marginal PMF from joint PMF</summary>
$$\begin{align*}
  p_X(x)=\sum_yp_{XY}(x,y)=\sum_yP(X=x,Y=y)
\end{align*}$$
</details>
<details class="example"><summary>$p_{XY}(x,y)=p^2q^{x+y}$ for $x,y\in\mathbb N$</summary>
$$\begin{align*}
  p_X(x)&=\sum_{y=0}^\infty p_{XY}(x,y)=\sum_y p^2q^xq^y\\
  &=p^2q^x\sum_yq^y=\frac{p^2q^x}{1-q}=pq^x\quad y\in\mathbb N\\
  F_{XY}(x,y)&=P(X\le x,Y\le y)=\sum_{i=0}^x\sum_{i=0}^yp_{XY}(i,j)=\sum_i^x\sum_j^yp^2q^iq^j\\
  &=p^2\sum_i^xq^i\sum_j^yq^j=p^2\cdot\frac{1-q^{x+1}}{1-q}\cdot\frac{1-q^{y+1}}{1-q}\\
  &=\left(1-q^{x+1}\right)\left(1-q^{y+1}\right)\quad x,y\in\mathbb N\\\
  P(X=Y)&=\sum_{i=0}^\infty p_{XY}(i,i)=\sum p^2q^{2i}=\frac{p^2}{1-q^2}\\
  &=\frac{p^2}{(1-q)(1+q)}=\frac p{1+q}=\frac p{2-p}
\end{align*}$$
</details>

### 2.7 Multivariate AC
<details class="definition" open><summary>Joint PDF $f:\R^2\to\R$</summary>

## 2.6 1D Change of Variable
<details class="definition" open><summary>Discrete $Y=h(X)$</summary>
$$p_Y(y)=\sum_{x\in h^{-1}(y)}p_X(x)$$
where $h^{-1}(y)=\{x:h(x)=y\}$
</details>
<details class="definition" open><summary>Continuous $Y=h(X)$</summary>
- PDF method (only monotone and diffable $h$)
$$f_Y(y)=\frac{f_X(h^{-1}(y))}{|h'(h^{-1}(y))|}$$
- CDF method
  1. find 
  1. find $F_Y(y)$ wrt $F_X$
  2. find $f_Y(y)=\frac d{dy}F_Y(y)$ wrt $f_X$
  3. use def of $f_X(x)$ and find domain
</details>
<details class="example"><summary>$f_X(x)=2xe^{-x^2}$ for $x>0$, find $f_Y(y)$ where $Y=1/X$</summary>
- CDF
$$\begin{align*}
  \because F_Y(y)&=P(Y\le y)=P(1/X\le y)=P(X\ge 1/y)\\
  &=1-F_X(1/y)\\
  \therefore f_Y(y)&=\frac d{dy}(1-F_X(1/y))=-f_X(1/y)(1/y)'\\
  &=y^{-2}f_X\left(y^{-1}\right)=y^{-2}\left(2y^{-1}e^{-y^{-2}}\right)\\
  &=2y^{-3}e^{-y^{-2}}\quad\forall 1/y>0\implies\forall y>0\\
  f_Y(y)&=0\textsf{ otherwise}\\
\end{align*}$$

- PDF $\because Y=1/X=h(X)$ is monotone on $x>0$ i.e. supp$(X)$
$$\begin{align*}
  \because h^{-1}(y)&=y^{-1}\quad h'(y)=-y^{-2}\implies |h'(h^{-1}(y))|=y^2\\
  \therefore f_Y(y)&=\frac{f_X(h^{-1}(y))}{|h'(h^{-1}(y))|}=y^{-2}f_X\left(y^{-1}\right)=2y^{-3}e^{-y^{-2}}\quad\forall y>0\\
  f_Y(y)&=0\textsf{ otherwise}
\end{align*}$$
</details>
<details class="example"><summary>$f_X(x)=\dfrac3{665}x^2$ for $4<x\le9$, and $Y=4-3X$</summary>
- find $P(Y\le-20)$
$$\begin{align*}
  P(Y\le-20)&=P(4-3X\le-20)=P(X\ge8)=\int_8^\infty f_X(x)\,dx\\
  &=\int_8^9\frac3{665}x^2\,dx=\frac3{665}\left[\frac{x^3}3\right]_8^9=\frac{9^3-8^3}{665}\\
\end{align*}$$
- find $f_Y(y)$ (CDF)
$$\begin{align*}
  \because F_Y(y)&=P(X\le y)=P(4-3X\le y)=P\left(X\ge\frac{4-y}3\right)\\
  &=1-P\left(X\le\frac{4-y}3\right)=1-F_X\left(\frac{4-y}3\right)\\
  \therefore f_Y(y)&=\frac d{dy}\left(1-F_X\left(\frac{4-y}3\right)\right)=-f_X\left(\frac{4-y}3\right)\left(\frac{4-y}3\right)'\\
  &=\frac13f_X\left(\frac{4-y}3\right)=\frac13\frac3{665}\left(\frac{4-y}3\right)^2\quad 4<\frac{4-y}3\le9\\
  &=\frac{(4-y)^2}{5985}\quad -23<y\le-8\\
  f_Y(y)&=0\textsf{ otherwise}
\end{align*}$$
</details>

## 2.5 Cumulative Distribution Functions
<details class="definition" open><summary>CDF of $X$ is $F_X(x):\R\to[0,1]$</summary>
$$F_X(x)=P(X\le x)=\int_{-\infty}^x f_X(t)\,dt$$

| $P(a< X\le b)=F_X(b)-F_X(a)$
| $\displaystyle P(a\le X\le b)=\lim_{n\to\infty}P(a-1/n<x\le b)=F_X(b)-F_X(a^-)$



- $\forall x\in\R\enspace 0\le F_X(x)\le1$
- $x\le y\implies F_X(x)\le F_X(y)$ (nondecreases)
- $\displaystyle\lim_{n\to\infty}F_X(x)=1$
- $\displaystyle\lim_{n\to-\infty}F_X(x)=0$
</details>

## 2.4 Continuous Distributions
<details class="definition" open><summary>Continuous $\implies$ uncountably infinite</summary>
$X$ is **continuous** iff
\begin{align*}
  \forall x\in\R\enspace P(X=x)&=0\\
  \forall a<b\in\R\enspace P(X\in(a,b))&=P(a<X<b)
\end{align*}

(but $P(X\in\R)=1$)
</details>
<details class="definition" open><summary>Probability Density Function (PDF) $f_X:\R\to\R$</summary>
$f_X:\R\to\R$ is a **density fn** iff
$$\forall x\in\R\enspace f_X(x)\ge0\textsf{ and }\int_{-\infty}^\infty f_X(x)\,dx=1$$
</details>
<details class="definition" open><summary>Absolutely continuous</summary>
$X$ is **abs cont** iff
$$\exist f_X:\R\to\R\textsf{ st }P(a\le X\le b)=\int_a^bf_X(x)\,dx$$

- abs cont $\implies$ cont
- interval bounds does not matter
- $f_X(x)=F'_X(x)$
</details>
<details class="example"><summary>use $N(0,10^2)$ to find $k$ for $f(x)=ke^{-\frac1{200}x^2}\quad x\ge10$</summary>
$$\begin{align*}
  \because 1&=\int_{10}^\infty ke^{-\frac1{200}x^2}\,dx=k\int_{10}^\infty e^{-\frac1{200}x^2}\,dx\\
  &=k10\sqrt{2\pi}\int_{10}^\infty\frac1{10\sqrt{2\pi}}e^{-\frac12\left(\frac x{10}\right)^2}\,dx\\
  &=k10\sqrt{2\pi}P(X>10)\quad\textsf{for }X\sim N(0,10^2)\\
  \because P(X>10)&=\Phi(-z)=\Phi\left(-\frac{10-\mu}\sigma\right)=\Phi\left(-\frac{10-0}{10}\right)\\
  &=\Phi(-1)\\
  \therefore1&=k10\sqrt{2\pi}P(X>10)=10\sqrt{2\pi}k\Phi(-1)\\
  \therefore k&=\frac1{10\sqrt{2\pi}\Phi(-1)}
\end{align*}$$
</details>

## 1.5 Conditional Probability & Indep

### 1.5.1 Conditional Probability

<details class="definition" open><summary>given events $A,B$ st $P(B)>0$</summary>
$$P(A|B)=\frac{P(A\cap B)}{P(B)}=\frac{|A\cap B|}{|B|}$$

$\iff P(A\cap B)=P(A)P(B|A)$
</details>
<details class="definition" open><summary>$P(A|BC)=P(A|B,C)=P(A|B\cap C)$</summary>
$$P(A|BC)=\frac{P(A\cap B\cap C)}{P(B\cap C)}$$
</details>
<details class="theorem" open><summary>Law of Total Probability</summary>
given $\{A_i\}$ as **partition** of $S$
$$\begin{align*}
  \forall B\enspace P(B)&=\sum P(A_i)P(B|A_i)\\
  &=P(A)P(B|A)+P(\overline A)P(B|\overline A)
\end{align*}$$
</details>
<details class="theorem" open><summary>Bayes' Theorem</summary>
$$P(A|B)=\frac{P(A)}{P(B)}P(B|A)=\frac{P(A)}{P(A)P(B|A)+P(\overline A)P(B|\overline A)}P(B|A)$$
</details>
<details class="example"><summary>Draw Veinn to Save Life</summary>
given
: $P(A\cup B)\quad P(A\cap B)$
: $E=(A\cap\overline B)\cup(\overline A\cap B)$

find $P(E|A\cup B)$

1. $\displaystyle P(E|(A\cup B))=\frac{P(E\cap(A\cup B))}{P(A\cup B)}$
2. draw veinn to see $E\sube A\cup B$ and $P(E)=P(A\cup B)-P(A\cap B)$
3. $\displaystyle\therefore P(E|(A\cup B))=\frac{P(A\cup B)-P(A\cap B)}{P(A\cup B)}$

</details>

### 1.5.2 Indep

<details class="definition" open><summary>(Mutual) Indep wrt $P$</summary>
$$\begin{align*}
  A\perp B&\iff P(A\cap B)=P(A)P(B)\\
  &\iff P(A|B)=P(A)\\
  (\perp A_i)&\iff\forall B\sube A\enspace P(\cap B_i)=\prod P(B_i)
\end{align*}$$

(a) $\implies A\perp\overline B$
(b) $\implies \overline A\perp\overline B$
</details>

<details class="definition" open><summary>Pairwise Indep wrt $P$</summary>
$$\forall i\ne j\enspace P(A_i\cap A_j)=P(A_i)P(A_j)$$
</details>

### Conditional Indep
<details class="definition" open><summary>$A$ and $B$ are conditionally indep wrt $P_C$ iff</summary>
$$P(A\cap B|C)=P(A|C)P(B|C)$$
</details>

## 1.6 Continuity

<details class="definition" open><summary></summary>
| increases | decreases |
|-:|:-|
| $A_1\sube A_2\sube\cdots$ and $\cup A_n=A$ | $A_1\supe A_2\supe\cdots$ and $\cap A_n=A$ |
| $\{A_n\}\nearrow A$ | $\{A_n\}\searrow A$ |
| $\displaystyle\lim_{n\to\infty}A_n=A$ | $\displaystyle\lim_{n\to\infty}A_n=A$

\begin{align*}
  \lim_{n\to\infty}A_n=A\implies\lim_{n\to\infty}P(A_n)&=P(A)\\
\end{align*}
</details>

<details class="theorem" open><summary></summary>
\begin{align*}
  \{A_n\}\searrow A&\implies\{\overline{A_n}\}\nearrow\overline A\\
  \lim_{n\to\infty}P(\cup\overline{A_n})&=\lim_{n\to\infty}P(\overline{\cap A_n})
\end{align*}
</details>

<details class="example"><summary>Geometric series</summary>
given $S=\mathbb Z^+\quad\forall s\in S\enspace P(s)=2^{-s}$<br>
find $P(A=\{5,6,7,8,\cdots\})$
\begin{align*}
\textsf{let }A_n&=\{5,6,\cdots,n\}\\
\therefore\{A_n\}&\nearrow A\\
\therefore P(A)&=\lim_{n\to\infty}P(A_n)\\
  &=\lim_{n\to\infty}P(\{5,6,\cdots,n\})\\
  &=\lim_{n\to\infty}(P(5)+P(6)+\cdots+P(n))\\
  &=\lim_{n\to\infty}\sum_{i=5}^n P(i)=\sum_{i=5}^\infty\frac 1{2^i}=\frac{2^{-5}}{1-1/2}=2^{-4}\\
\end{align*}

<details><summary>**Proof.** $E(X)$ Binomial$(n,p)$</summary>
$$\begin{align*}
  E(X)&=\sum_{x=0}^nxp_X(x)=\sum_{x=1}^nxp_X(x)\tag{$0p_X(0)=0$}\\
  &=\sum_{x=1}^nx\binom nxp^kq^{n-x}=\sum_{x=1}^n\frac{xn!}{x!(n-x)!}p^xq^{n-x}\\
  &=\sum_{x=1}^n\frac{n(n-1)!}{(x-1)!(n-x)!}p^xq^{n-x}=\sum_{x=1}^nn\binom{n-1}{x-1}p^xq^{n-x}\\
  &=n\sum_{i=0}^{n-1}\binom{n-1}ip^{i+1}q^{n-i-1}\tag{$i=x-1$}\\
  &=np\sum_{i=0}^m\binom mip^iq^{m-i}=np(p+q)^m=np\tag{$m=n-1$}\\
\end{align*}$$
</details>
<details><summary>**Proof.** $E(X)$ Geometric$(p)$</summary>
$$\begin{align*}
  E(X)&=\sum_{n=0}^\infty np_X(n)=\sum_{n=1}^\infty np_X(n)=p\sum_{n=1}^\infty nq^n\tag{1}\\
  \therefore qE(X)&=p\sum_{n=0}^\infty nq^{n+1}=p\sum_{n=1}^\infty(n-1)q^n\tag{2}\\
  \therefore(1-q)E(X)&=p\sum_{n=1}^\infty(n-(n-1))q^n\tag{$1)-(2$}\\
  \therefore pE(X)&=p\sum_{n=1}^\infty (1-p)^n=p\frac{1-p}1-(1-p)=1-p\\
  \therefore E(X)&=\frac{1-p}p\\
\end{align*}$$
</details>

<details><summary>**Proof.** $E(X)$ Poisson$(\lambda)$</summary>
$$\begin{align*}
  E(X)&=\sum_{n=0}^\infty np_X(n)=\sum_{n=1}^\infty np_X(n)=\sum_{n=1}^\infty n\frac{\lambda^n}{n!}e^{-\lambda}\\
  &=e^{-\lambda}\sum_{n=1}^\infty\frac{\lambda^n}{(n-1)!}=e^{-\lambda}\sum_{n=0}^\infty\frac{\lambda^{n+1}}{n!}=\lambda e^{-\lambda}\sum\frac{\lambda^n}{n!}\\
  &=\lambda e^{-\lambda}e^{-\lambda}=\lambda\\
\end{align*}$$

<details><summary>**Proof.** $V(X)$ Geometric$(p)$</summary>
find $E(X^2-X)=E(X(X-1))$
$$\begin{align*}
  E(X(X-1))&=\sum^\infty x(x-1)q^xp=p\sum x(x-1)q^x\\
  &=p\sum_{x=2}^\infty x(x-1)q^x=pq^2\sum_2 x(x-1)q^{x-2}\\
  &=pq^2\sum_2\frac{d^2}{dq^2}q^x=pq^2\frac{d^2}{dq^2}\sum_{x=2}^\infty q^x\\
  &=pq^2\frac{d^2}{dq^2}\left(\frac{q^2}{1-q}\right)\\
  \because\frac{q^2}{1-q}&=\frac{(1-q)^2+2q-1}{1-q}=1-q+\frac{2q}{1-q}-\frac1{1-q}\\
  \because\frac q{1-q}&=\frac{1-(1-q)}{1-q}=\frac1{1-q}-1\\
  \therefore\frac{q^2}{1-q}&=1-q+2\left(\frac1{1-q}-1\right)-\frac1{1-q}\\
  &=-1-q+\frac1{1-q}\\
  \therefore E(X(X-1))&=pq^2\frac{d^2}{dq^2}\left(-1-q+\frac1{1-q}\right)\\
  &=pq^22(1-q)^{-3}=2pq^2p^{-3}=2p^{-2}q^2\\
  &=E(X^2-X)
\end{align*}$$
find $V(X)=E(X^2+X-X)-\mu^2$
$$\begin{align*}

  &=2\frac{q^2}{p^2}+\frac qp-\frac{q^2}{p^2}=\frac{q^2}{p^2}+\frac qp\\
  &=\frac{q^2+qp}{p^2}=\frac{q^2+q(1-q)}{p^2}=\frac q{p^2}
\end{align*}$$
