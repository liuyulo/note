Places

  • Bilabial: Labials
    • two lips
  • Labiodental: Labials
    • both teeth and lips
  • Interdental
    • tip of tongue between teeth
  • Alveolar
    • tongue touches alveolar ridge
  • Alveopalatal
    • tongue touches behind alveolar ridge
  • Palatal
    • tongue touches hard palate
  • Velar
    • tongue touches velum (soft palate)
  • Glottal
    • produced with vocal cords/folds as primary articulators

Manner

  • Plosive/Stop: opposite to continuant
    • airflow is stopped
  • Nasal: from nose, opposite to oral
    • lower velum, air passes through nasal cavity
  • Fricative/Turbulence
    • makes friction
  • Affricate
    • stop + fricative
  • Strident/Sibilant: noisier fricative/affricate
    • /s/ /z/ /ʃ/ /ʒ/ /tʃ/ /dʒ/
  • Liquid
    • air passes more freely
  • Glides: semi-vowels
    • no obstruction of airflow

Diphthong

vowel + glide

  • major diphthongs: large, noticeable change
    • toy [tɔɪ], lie [laɪ], cow [kaʊ]
  • minor diphthongs: less noticeable
    • day [deɪ]

Speech Production

Assimilation

  • regressive affects the preceding word
  • progressive affects the following word

Voicing

consonants become voiceless before voiceless stops

  • have to [v] --> [t]
  • has to [z] --> [s]

Devoicing

vowels/voiced consonants become voiceless

  • plan [pl̥æn]
  • trick [tr̥ɪk]

Nasalisation

lower the velum in anticipation of a nasal stops

  • gone [gɑ̃n]

  • bond [bõnd] ### Flapping/Tapping > alveolar stop turns into a flap [ɾ]

  • butter /bʌtə/ --> /bʌɾə/

  • bedding /bedɪŋ/ --> /beɾɪŋ/

Dissimilation

Deletion

  • suppose [səpowz] --> [spowz]
  • fifths [fɪfθs] --> [fɪfs]

Epenthesis/Insertion

add a sound to make it easier to pronounce

Metathesis

segments are reordered to make it easier to articulate

  • comfortable -> comfterble

  • prescription -> perscription ## Aspiration > voiceless stops at the start of a stressed syllable

  • top [tʰɑp] peak [pʰik]

  • [tʰ] and [t] cannot exist in the same env

    • they are in complementary distribution
