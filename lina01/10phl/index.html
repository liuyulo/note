Segments

alternations between oral and nasal vowels is a by-product of the articulation of neighbouring sounds within the word

  • e.g. bean [ĩ], beak [i]

Phonemes and Allophones

  • phoneme: abstract unit of sound that changes meaning between sounds
    • allophones: variation of the same phoneme
    • e.g. [ĩ] and [i] are allophones
      [æ̃] is an allophone of /æ/
      [i] and [æ] are separate phonemes

Minimal Pairs

set of two words that differ by a single sound in the same position

  • the sound differences are constrastive

Syllables

Wd - σ (CVC, CV, VC) - onset (O) - rhyme (R) - nucleus (N) - coda (Co) ## Phonotactics (English) - if on onset has 3 consonants - 1st always [s] - 2nd awlays voiceless stop [ptk] - 3rd liquid or glide [lrwj]

Syllabification

Nucleus

every vowel segment is separate nucleus (σ -> R -> N) ### Onset the longest sequence of consonants to the left of each nucleus ### Coda any left-over consonants to the right

  • if a syllable has a coda, it’s a closed syllable, else open
