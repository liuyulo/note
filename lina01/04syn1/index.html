Syntatic Categories

  • categories
    • lexical/open
      • noun (n)
      • verb (v)
      • adjective (adj)
      • adverb (adv)
      • preposition (prep)
    • functional/closed
      • determiner (det)
      • negation (neg)
      • degree word (deg)
      • auxiliary verb (aux)
  • reasons for this categorisation: | | inventory | productivity | semantics | | ———- | ——— | ———— | ————————— | | lexical | unlimited | yes | has association of meanings | | functional | limited | no | contentless |
    • inventory: the numbers of items
    • productivity: the possibility of creating new words
    • semantics: the meaning behind the items

Identifying Lexical Categories

Nouns

  • derivational affixes: -ment, -tion, -ity, -ness
  • inflectioal affixes: -s (plural)

Morphological Distribution

use affixes to prove a word is noun: morphological distribution/evidence

  • eg developers
    • -er creates noun from verbs
    • -s plural marker and attaches to nouns

Syntactic Distribution

  • preceded by a determiner
    • eg: some children
  • subject/object of the sentence
    • eg: that tired student is applying to law school
  • sometimes come after adjectives
    • eg: two constructive comments
  • may be preceded by a preposition
    • eg: by noon, during class

Verbs

action verbs express action, state verbs express perception/state

Types

main verbs or auxilaries/helping verbs

  • main: express action or state
  • aux: support main and cannot occur independently
    • regular aux (we are doing, i have done, you do not know)
    • modal aux (can, could, will, must, etc)
  • order:
    • modal have be main

Morphological Distribution

derivational: -ize
inflectional: -ed, -ing, etc

Syntactic Distribution

  • preceded by an aux
    • he were playing
  • after the subject of the sentence
    • I did it
  • may come after negation
    • I did not do it
