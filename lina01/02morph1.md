# Introduction
- **phonemes**: sounds, building blocks of language
    - phonemes carry no meaning, *words* do
- **morphology**: division of linguistics that looks at words and their formation

# Words and Their Sturcture
- human have the ability to **segment sounds** into meaningful **words**
- **words**: element whose position is flexible relative to neighboring units
    - **smallest** *free form* that can stand on its own without having to be attached to any other element

example: <u>elephants are amazing</u>
- the intuition is that *elephants* is a word because it can **occur in isolation**, and **in different positions** in the sentence

## Morphemes
> words have an *internal structure* of smaller units of meaning called **morphemes**

- **simple** word: word with 1 morpheme
    - **complex**: more than 1 morpheme
- the *root* should have relatable meaning to the original word

## Allomorphs
morpheme that varies on pronunciation
- book**ed**, begg**ed**, stat**ed**
- **im**possible, **in**correct, **il**legal, **ir**regular

each of them is a different **allomorph**

## Free and Bound Morphemes
- **free**: consntitute words and can stand on their own (eg. *cry*)
- **bound**: must attach to another element (eg. *-ing*)

## Roots and Affixes
> complex words are made up of a *root* and *affix(es)*

- **root**: belongs to a *lexical category* (noun, verb, adjective, etc.)
    - most roots are **free roots** (couterexample: <span class="spoiler"> re**ceive**, per**ceive**, de**ceive** </span> )

- **affix**: always bound morphemes

## Base
<blockquote>

```rust
// 'word' and 'base' can contain affixes
let base = word - affix;
let first_base = root;

let second_base = first_base + affix
```

## Type of Affix
> affix is classified based on their **position** with respect to the base

- **prefix**: attached to the front
- **suffix**: attached to the end
- **infix**: attached inside (doesn't exist in English)
- **circumfix**: attached around
    - German verb root for "love": lieb
    - past participle: **ge**lieb**t**


## Derivation and Inflection
- **derivational** affix: changes in **meaning** and/or lexical category
    - eg. -ment, -ize, un-
- **inflection**: process that doesn't change meaning or category, only changes **grammatical information**
    - eg. -s, -ing, -est
