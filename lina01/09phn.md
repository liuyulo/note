## Places
- Bilabial: Labials
    - two lips
- Labiodental: Labials
    - both teeth and lips
- Interdental
    - tip of tongue between teeth    
- Alveolar
    - tongue touches *alveolar ridge*
- Alveopalatal
    - tongue touches behind alveolar ridge
- Palatal
    - tongue touches hard palate
- Velar
    - tongue touches *velum* (soft palate)
- Glottal
    - produced with vocal cords/folds as primary articulators

## Manner
- Plosive/Stop: opposite to continuant
    - airflow is stopped
- Nasal: from nose, opposite to oral
    - lower velum, air passes through nasal cavity
- Fricative/Turbulence
    - makes friction
- Affricate
    - stop + fricative
- Strident/Sibilant: noisier fricative/affricate
    - `/s/ /z/ /ʃ/ /ʒ/ /tʃ/ /dʒ/`
- Liquid
    - air passes more freely
- Glides: semi-vowels
    - no obstruction of airflow

## Features
- Tongue Height
- Tongue Frontness/Backness
- Tenseness/Laxness
- Roundness/Unroundness

## Diphthong
> vowel + glide

- *major* diphthongs: large, noticeable change
    - toy `[tɔɪ]`, lie `[laɪ]`, cow `[kaʊ]`
- *minor* diphthongs: less noticeable
    - day `[deɪ]`

# Speech Production
## Assimilation
- regressive affects the preceding word
- progressive affects the following word

### Voicing
> consonants become voiceless before voiceless stops

- have to `[v] --> [t]`
- has to `[z] --> [s]`

### Devoicing
> vowels/voiced consonants become voiceless

- p**l**an `[pl̥æn]`
- t**r**ick `[tr̥ɪk]`

### Nasalisation
> lower the velum in anticipation of a nasal stops

- g**o**ne `[gɑ̃n]`
- b**o**nd `[bõnd]`
### Flapping/Tapping
> alveolar stop turns into a flap `[ɾ]`

- bu**tt**er `/bʌtə/ --> /bʌɾə/`
- be**dd**ing `/bedɪŋ/ --> /beɾɪŋ/`

## Dissimilation
### Deletion
- suppose `[səpowz] --> [spowz]`
- fifths `[fɪfθs] --> [fɪfs]`

### Epenthesis/Insertion
> add a sound to make it easier to pronounce

## Metathesis
> segments are reordered to make it easier to articulate

- `comfortable -> comfterble`
- `prescription -> perscription`
## Aspiration
> voiceless stops at the start of a *stressed syllable*

- top `[tʰɑp]` peak `[pʰik]`
- `[tʰ]` and `[t]` cannot exist in the same env
    - they are in *complementary distribution*

## Canadian Rising
| Before **voiced** consonants | Before **voiceless** consonants |
| ---------------------------- | ------------------------------- |
| rise `[rajz]`                | rice `[rʌjs]`                   |
| ride `[rajd]`                | right `[rʌjt]`                  |
| tribe `[trajb]`              | tripe `[trʌjp]`                 |
| fly `[flaj]`                 | flight `[flʌjt]`                |
| wives `[wajvz]`              | wife `[wʌjf]`                   |

- same for `/ʌw/` in about, doubt, mouse and `/aw/` in lound, arouse, how

## Syllabic Consonants
> unstressed syllable gets ignored

- rhythm `/rɪðəm/ --> /rɪðm̩/`
- funnel `/fʌnəl/ --> /fʌnl̩/`
