# Segments
> alternations between oral and nasal vowels is a *by-product* of the articulation of neighbouring sounds within the word


- e.g. `bean [ĩ]`,  `beak [i]`

## Phonemes and Allophones
- *phoneme*: abstract unit of sound that changes meaning between sounds
    - *allophones*: variation of the same phoneme
    - e.g. `[ĩ]` and `[i]` are allophones<br>
    `[æ̃]` is an allophone of `/æ/`<br>
    `[i]` and `[æ]` are separate phonemes

## Minimal Pairs
> set of two words that *differ by a single sound* in the same position

**what**

***italic***

- the sound differences are *constrastive*
    - e.g. `bat`, `cat`
    - `b` and `c` are constrastive

## Phonetic and Phonomic Representations
> /phonemic/: less information<br>
> [phonetic]: contains aspiration, nasalisation, devoicing, etc

| Word | Phonemic | Phonetic  | Diff                        |
| ---- | -------- | --------- | --------------------------- |
| swam | `/swæm/` | `[swæ̃m]`  | nasalisation                |
| type | `/tajp/` | `[tʰʌjp]` | aspiration, canadian rising |

## Phonotactics (English)
- if on onset has 3 consonants
    - 1st always `[s]`
    - 2nd awlays voiceless stop `[ptk]`
    - 3rd liquid or glide `[lrwj]`

## Syllabification
### Nucleus
> every vowel segment is separate nucleus (`σ -> R -> N`)
### Onset
> the longest sequence of consonants to the left of each nucleus
### Coda
> any left-over consonants to the right

- if a syllable has a coda, it's a *closed syllable*, else open
