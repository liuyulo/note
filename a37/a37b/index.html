Darboux Sum: given a\lt b\quad n\in\Z^+ let any P=\{x_i\}\textsf{ st }a=x_0\lt x_1\lt\cdots\lt x_n=b

Darboux Definition of Integral

\begin{align*} U_f&=\sup\{U(f,P):P\textsf{ partition of }[a,b]\}\\ L_f&=\inf\{L(f,P):P\textsf{ partition of }[a,b]\}\\ \end{align*}

f is integrable on [a,b]\iff U_f=L_f

if \{a_n\} is bounded and monotonic, then \{a_n\} converges

a_1=\sqrt7\enspace a_{n+1}=\sqrt{2a_n+6}\enspace find limit of \{a_n\}

formal proof
  • wts \{a_n\} converges
    • wts \forall n\in\Z^+\enspace a_n\le5 i.e. bounded above
      • base n=1\implies a_n=\sqrt7\le\sqrt{25}=5 (√ is monotone)
      • IH: \forall n\in\Z^+\enspace a_n\le5\implies a_{n+1}\le5
        assume a_n\le4,then a_{n+1}=\underbrace{\sqrt{2a_n+6}\le\sqrt{2\cdot5+6}}_{\textsf{IH}}=4\le5 \therefore by induction \forall n\in\Z^+\enspace a_n\le5 i.e. bounded above
    • wts \forall n\in\Z^+\enspace a_n\lt a_{n+1} i.e. increases
      • base n=1\implies a_n=\sqrt7\enspace a_{n+1}=\sqrt{2\sqrt7+6} \begin{align*} \because 1/4&<7\tag{\sf draft}\\ \therefore 1/2&<\sqrt7\tag{\sf √ is monotone}\\ \implies1=7-6&<2\sqrt7\\ \implies7&<2\sqrt7+6\\ \implies\sqrt7&<\sqrt{2\sqrt7+6}\quad\quad\tag{\sf √ mono}\\ \end{align*}

    • \therefore\{a_n\} bounded above and increases \therefore converges
  • find limit \because converges \therefore\exists a\in\R st \displaystyle\lim_{n\to\infty}a_n=a \begin{align*} \because\lim_{n\to\infty}a_n&=\lim_{n\to\infty}a_{n+1}\\ \therefore a&=\lim_{n\to\infty}\sqrt{2a_n+6}\tag{\sf def}\\ \implies a^2&=\lim_{n\to\infty}(2a_n+6)=2a+6\\ \implies a^2-2a-6&=0\implies a=1\pm\sqrt7\\ \because 1-\sqrt7&<0\\ \therefore a&=1+\sqrt7\\ \end{align*} \therefore converges to 1+\sqrt7

Integral Test

use it when you want to kys

if a(x) is continuous, positive, decreasing for n\ge k, then
\displaystyle\sum_{n=k}^\infty a_n and \displaystyle\int_k^\infty a(x)\,dx
both diverges or converges
find \displaystyle\sum\frac n{e^n}
  • wts \sum_{n=2} a_n (the tail) converges
    • wts (x) positive \forall x\ge2 \begin{align*} \because x&\ge2\implies x\gt0\\ x&\ge2\implies e^{-x}\gt0\\ \therefore xe^{-1}&\gt0\text{ as their product} \end{align*}
    • wts a(x) decrease and continuous \forall x\ge2 \begin{align*} \because a'(x)&=\overbrace{e^{-x}}^+\overbrace{(1-x)}^-\lt0\enspace\forall x\ge2\\ \therefore a(x)&\text{ is diffable and decreasing }\forall x\ge2\\ \therefore a(x)&\text{ is cont }\forall x\ge2\tag{diffable $\implies$ cont}\\ \end{align*}
    • wts \displaystyle\int_2^\infty a(x)\,dx converges \begin{align*} \int_2^\infty a(x)\,dx&=\lim_{n\to\infty}\int_2^n a(x)\,dx\\ &=\lim_{n\to\infty}\int xe^{-x}\,dx\Big|_2^n\tag{FTOCI}\\ &=\lim_{n\to\infty}\left[-\frac{x+1}{e^x}\right]_2^n\tag{IBP}\\ &=\frac{2+1}{e^2}-\lim_{n\to\infty}\frac{n+1}{e^n}\\ &=\frac3{e^2}\enspace\therefore\text{ converges }\tag{L'Hôpital} \end{align*}
  • \therefore \sum_{n=2}a_n (tail) converges
  • \therefore\sum a_n converges (tail converges \implies series converges)
