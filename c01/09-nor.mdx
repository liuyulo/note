## Examples

<details class="example" open>
<summary>ab $|G|=pq$ is cyclic for distinct prime $p,q$</summary>
$\therefore$ by cauchy $\exists a,b\in G$ with $|a|=p$ and $|b|=q$\
$\because (ab)^{pq}=(a^p)^q(b^q)^p=e$ thus $|ab|$ divides $pq$\
$\therefore|ab|=1$ or $p$ or $q$ or $pq$\
BWOC assume $|ab|=1$, that is $ab=e$
- $\therefore a=b^{-1}$ thus $|a|=|b|$, contradicts that $p\ne q$

BWOC assume $|ab|=p$ (similar for $|ab|=q$)
- $\therefore e=(ab)^p=a^pb^p=b^p$, contradicts that $|b|=q$ (prime)

$\therefore|ab|=pq$, thus $G=\langle ab\rangle\quad\square$
</details>

<details class="example" open>
<summary>$G/N=(\mathbb Z_4\oplus\mathbb Z_{12})/\langle (2,2)\rangle$ not isomorphic to $\mathbb Z_8$ nor $\mathbb Z_2\oplus\mathbb Z_2\oplus\mathbb Z_2$</summary>
wts any element in $G/N$ has order at most $4$
- $\because$ for any $(a,b)N\in G/N$

$$
\begin{aligned}
4(a,b)&=(4a,4b)=(0,4b)\in\{(0,0),(0,4),(0,8)\}\sube N\\
\therefore((a,b)N)^4&=(a,b)^4N=N
\end{aligned}
$$

- $\therefore |(a,b)N|$ divides $4$, thus at most $4$
- $\therefore G/N\not\cong\mathbb Z_8$

wts $G/N$ has element of order $4$
- $\because (1,0)N$ has order $4$
- $\therefore G/N\not\cong\mathbb Z_2\oplus\mathbb Z_2\oplus\mathbb Z_2\quad\square$
</details>

<details class="example" open>
<summary>noab $|G|=8\implies G$ is not internal direct product of proper subgroup</summary>
$\because$ by Lagrange, proper subgroup must have order $2$ or $4$, with are both ab\
$\because$ ab group $\implies$ ab external direct prod $\implies$ ab internal direct prod

</details>

<details class="example" open>
<summary>$G$ not ab $\implies\operatorname{aut} G$ not cyclic</summary>
$\because G$ not ab\
$\therefore G/Z(G)$ not cyclic\
$\therefore\operatorname{inn}G$ not cyclic
$\therefore\operatorname{aut}G$ not cyclic $\quad\square$
</details>

<details class="example list-none" open>
<summary>let $H\le G$ with index $2$, wts $H$ has all odd-order elements of $G$</summary>

$\because|G:H|=2$\
if $a\in H$, then $aH=H=Ha$\
if $a\not\in H$, then $aH=Ha$ since $G=H\sqcup Ha=H\sqcup aH$\
$\therefore H\triangleleft G$ and $|G/H|=2$\
$\therefore (aH)^2=H$ for any $a\in G$\
$\therefore$ for any $a\in G$ with $a^{2n+1}=e$ for some $n\in\mathbb N$
- $H=eH=a^{2n+1}H=a(a^nH)^2=aH$
- $\therefore a\in H\quad\square$

</details>

## Normaliser

<details class="definition" open>
<summary>normaliser of $H\le G$</summary>

$$
N(H)=\{a\in G: aHa^{-1}=H\}
$$
</details>

## Internal Direct Product

<details class="definition" open>
<summary>internal direct product</summary>
- if $H,K\vartriangleleft G$, $H\cap K=\{e\}$
- then $G=H\times K=HK$ is the interal direct product of $H$ and $K$
</details>

<details class="theorem" open>
<summary>internal direct product is isomorphic to external direct product</summary>

$$
H_1\times\cdots\times H_n\cong H_1\oplus\cdots\oplus H_n
$$
</details>

## Factor Group

<details class="definition" open>
<summary>let $H\vartriangleleft G$</summary>
$$
G/H=\{aH:a\in G\}
$$

$G/H$ is a group under $(aH)(bH)=abH$

- $G$ cy $\implies G/H$ cy
- $G$ ab $\implies G/H$ ab
</details>

<details class="theorem" open>
<summary>$G/Z(G)$ cyclic $\implies G/Z=\{Z\}\implies G=Z$</summary>

<details class="list-none">
<summary>**Proof.** assume $G/Z=\langle gZ\rangle$ for some $g\in G$</summary>

let any $a\in G$

- $\therefore\exists i$ st $aZ=(gZ)^i=g^iZ$
- $\therefore a\in g^iZ$ thus $\exists z\in Z$ st $a=g^iz$
- $\therefore ga=gg^iz=zg^ig=ag$
- $\therefore a\in C(g)$

$\therefore g\in Z$ thus $gZ=Z$\
$\therefore G/Z=\langle gZ\rangle=\langle Z\rangle=\{Z\}$\
$\therefore G=Z$ thus ab $\quad\square$
</details>

collaries

- $G/Z$ cyclic $\implies G/Z$ trivial
- $G/H$ cyclic for some $H\le Z(G)\implies G$ abelian
- contrapositive
  - e.g. nonabelian $G$ and $p,q$ prime
  - $|G|=pq\implies Z(G)=\{e\}$

</details>

<details class="theorem" open>
<summary>$G/Z(G)\cong\operatorname{Inn}(G)$</summary>

<details class="list-none" >
<summary>**Proof.** let $Z=(G)$ and $T=gZ\mapsto \varphi_g$</summary>

wts well-defined, assume $aZ=bZ$, wts $\varphi_a=\varphi_b$

- $\therefore b^{-1}a\in Z$
- $\therefore$ for any $x\in G$

$$
axa^{-1}=b(b^{-1}ax)a^{-1}=b(xb^{-1}a)a^{-1}=bxb^{-1}
$$

- $\therefore \varphi_a=\varphi_b$

</details>
</details>

## Normal Subgroup

<details class="definition" open>
<summary>$H\le G$ is normal when, denoted as $H\vartriangleleft G$</summary>
for any $a\in G$

$$
aH=Ha
$$
</details>

<details class="theorem" open>
<summary>normal subgroup test</summary>
$H\vartriangleleft G$ iff

1. $H\le G$
2. $\forall a\in G\enspace aHa^{-1}\in H$
</details>

<details class="theorem" open>
<summary></summary>
- $H\le Z(G)\implies H\triangleleft Z(G)$
- $Z(G)\vartriangleleft G$
- $A_n\vartriangleleft S_n$
- $H\vartriangleleft G$ and $K\le G\implies HK\le G$
- $H,K\triangleleft G$ then
  - $HK\triangleleft G$
  - $H\cap K\triangleleft G$
- $G$ has unique subgroup $H$ of its order $\implies H\vartriangleleft G$
  - for any $a\in G$ the inner aut $\varphi_a:H\to aHa^{-1}$
  - $\therefore aHa^{-1}\le G$ and $|H|=|aHa^{-1}|$
- $SL(2,\mathbb R)\vartriangleleft GL(2,\mathbb R)$
</details>
