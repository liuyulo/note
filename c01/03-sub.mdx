<details class="definition" open>
<summary>order</summary>
- order of group $G$ is $|G|$: number of elements
- order of $a\in G$ is $|a|$: smallest $n$ st $a^n=e$ (or infinity)
</details>

<details class="definition" open>
<summary>subgroup $H\le G$</summary>
iff $H\sube G$ is a group with the operation of $G$

-  $H<G$ denotes proper subgroup
</details>

<details class="theorem" open>
<summary>subgroup test: set $H$ is a subgroup of $G$, $H\le G$ iff all </summary>
- $H$ non empty
- $H\sube G$
- $a,b\in H\implies ab^{-1}\in H$
</details>

## Center

<details class="definition" open>
<summary>center of group $G$ is $Z(G)\le G$</summary>
$$
Z(G)=\{a\in G:ab=ba\enspace\forall b\in G\}
$$

i.e. group of elements that commute with any element
</details>

<details class="definition" open>
<summary>centraliser of $a\in G$ is $C(a)\le G$</summary>
$$
C(a)=\{b\in G:ab=ba\}
$$

i.e. group of elements that commute with $a$
</details>

<details class="theorem" open>
<summary>$G$ abeliean $\iff G=Z(G)$</summary>

</details>

<details open class="example">
<summary>$\exists!a\in G$ st $|a|=2\implies a\in Z(G)$</summary>
<details class="list-none">
<summary>**Proof.** assume $\exists!a\in G$ st $|a|=2$</summary>

$\therefore a^2=e$ by def

let any $b\in G$

$$
\begin{aligned}
  \therefore&&(b^{-1}ab)^2&=b^{-1}abb^{-1}ab=b^{-1}a^2b=e\\
  \therefore&&|b^{-1}ab|&=2\\
  \therefore&&b^{-1}ab&=a\\
  \therefore&&ab&=b(b^{-1}ab)=ba
\end{aligned}
$$

$\therefore a\in Z(G)\quad\square$
</details>
</details>

<details class="example" open>
<summary>given $\exists k\in\mathbb N$ st $\forall x\in G\enspace x^{2k+1}=e$, wts $\forall a,b\in G\enspace a^2=b^2\implies a=b$</summary>

<details>
<summary>**Proof.** let any $a,b\in G$</summary>
let any $a,b\in G$
$$
\begin{aligned}
  \because&&a^2&=b^2\\
  \therefore&&e&=a^2a^{-2}=b^2a^{-2}\\
  \therefore&&a&=ea=(ba^{-1})^{2k+1}a=(ba^{-1})^{2k}ba^{-1}a=(b^2a^{-2})^kb=b\quad\square
\end{aligned}
$$
</details>

</details>

<details class="example" open>
<summary>let $G=SL(2,\mathbb R)$ wts $Z(G)=\{aI:a\in\mathbb R\}$</summary>
<details>
<summary>**Proof.** wts $\sube$, let any $A=\begin{pmatrix}a&b\\c&d\\\end{pmatrix}\in Z(G)$</summary>



$$
\begin{aligned}
  \because&&\begin{pmatrix}a&0\\c&0\\\end{pmatrix}&=\begin{pmatrix}a&b\\c&d\\\end{pmatrix}\begin{pmatrix}1&0\\0&0\\\end{pmatrix}=\begin{pmatrix}1&0\\0&0\\\end{pmatrix}\begin{pmatrix}a&b\\c&d\\\end{pmatrix}=\begin{pmatrix}a&b\\0&0\\\end{pmatrix}\\
  \therefore&&b&=0=c\\
  \because&&\begin{pmatrix}b&a\\d&c\\\end{pmatrix}&=\begin{pmatrix}a&b\\c&d\\\end{pmatrix}\begin{pmatrix}0&1\\1&0\\\end{pmatrix}=\begin{pmatrix}0&1\\1&0\\\end{pmatrix}\begin{pmatrix}a&b\\c&d\\\end{pmatrix}=\begin{pmatrix}c&d\\a&b\\\end{pmatrix}\\
  \therefore&&a&=d\\
  \therefore&&\begin{pmatrix}a&b\\c&d\\\end{pmatrix}&=\begin{pmatrix}a&0\\0&a\\\end{pmatrix}=aI\in\{aI:a\in\mathbb R\}
\end{aligned}
$$

wts $\supe$, let any $aI\in\{aI:a\in\mathbb R\}$

- let any $B\in SR(2,\mathbb R)$
- $\therefore aIB=aB=BaI$
- $\therefore aI\in Z(G)$

</details>
</details>

## Subgroup Lattice
<details class="definition" open>
<summary>subgroup lattice of group $G$</summary>
- undirected graph
  - each vertex is a subgroup of $G$
  - each edge indicates bottom is proper subgroup of top
- top most vertex is $G$, bottom most is $\{e\}$
- between $G$ and $\{e\}$ has all subgroups of $G$
</details>
