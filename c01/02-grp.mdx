## Properties of Groups

<details class="theorem" open>
<summary>unique identity</summary>
<details>
<summary>**Proof.** wts $e,e'\in G$ identity $\implies e=e'$</summary>

- $\therefore \forall a\in G\enspace ae=a$ and $\forall a\in G\enspace a=e'a$
- in particular, $e'e=e'$ and $e=e'e$
- $\therefore e'=e'e=e\quad\square$

</details>
</details>

<details class="theorem" open>
<summary>can right/left cancel</summary>
<details>
<summary>**Proof.** wts $ba=ca\implies b=c$</summary>
let $a'$ be inverse of $a$

$$
\begin{aligned}
  \because&& ba&=ca\\
  \therefore&& (ba)a'&=(ca)a'\\
  \therefore&& b(aa')&=c(aa')\\
  \therefore&& be&=ce\\
  \therefore&& b&=c\quad\square
\end{aligned}
$$
</details>
</details>

<details class="theorem" open>
<summary>unique inverse, denote as $a^{-1}$</summary>
<details>
<summary>**Proof.** wts $b,c$ inverse of $a\implies b=c$</summary>
$$
\begin{aligned}
  \because&& ab&=e=ac\\
  \therefore&&b&=c\\
\end{aligned}
$$
</details>
</details>

<details class="definition" open>
<summary>for any $a\in G$ and $n\in\mathbb Z^+$</summary>
- $a^0=e$
- $a^{-1}$ denotes inverse
- $a^n$ denotes $aa\cdots a$ (by $n$)
- $a^{-n}$ denotes $(a^{-1})^n$
</details>

<details class="definition" open>
<summary>for additive group</summary>
- $e=0$
- $ab=a+b$
- $a^{-1}=-a$
- $a^n=na$
- $ab^{-1}=a-b$
</details>

<details class="theorem" open>
<summary></summary>
- $(ab)^n=ababab\cdots ab$
- $(ab)^{-1}=b^{-1}a^{-1}$
</details>

<details class="example">
<summary>$ab=ca\implies b=c$ implies abelian</summary>
let any $a,b\in G$

- let $c=aba^{-1}$

$$
\begin{aligned}
  \therefore&& ab&=ab(a^{-1}a)=(aba^{-1})a=ca=ba\\
\end{aligned}
$$

- $\therefore$ abelian
</details>
<details class="example">
<summary>$\forall a,b\in G\enspace(ab)^{-1}=a^{-1}b^{-1}\iff$ abelian</summary>
- let any $a,b\in G$, assume $(ab)^{-1}=a^{-1}b^{-1}$

$$
\begin{aligned}
  \therefore&& ab&=((ab)^{-1})^{-1}\tag{\sf unique inverse}\\
  &&&=(a^{-1}b^{-1})^{-1}\\
  &&&=(b^{-1})^{-1}(a^{-1})^{-1}\\
  &&&=ba
\end{aligned}
$$

- assume abelian

$$
\begin{aligned}
\therefore&&(ab)^{-1}&=b^{-1}a^{-1}=a^{-1}b^{-1}\quad\square\\
\end{aligned}
$$
</details>
<details class="example">
<summary>$\forall a,b\in G\enspace(ab)^2=a^2b^2\implies$ abelian</summary>

let any $a,b\in G$, assume $(ab)^2=a^2b^2$
$$
\begin{aligned}
  \therefore ab&=a^{-1}a^2b^2b^{-1}=a^{-1}(ab)^2b^{-1}\\
  &=a^{-1}ababb^{-1}=ba\quad\square
\end{aligned}
$$
</details>
<details class="example">
<summary>$\forall a\in G\enspace a^2=e\implies$ abelian</summary>
$$
\begin{aligned}
  ab&=a^{-1}a^2b^2b^{-1}\\
  &=a^{-1}eeb^{-1}\\
  &=a^{-1}eb^{-1}\\
  &=a^{-1}(ab)^2b^{-1}\\
  &=a^{-1}ababb^{-1}\\
  &=ba\quad\square
\end{aligned}
$$
</details>

<details class="example list-none">
<summary>$|G|=3\implies$ abelian</summary>
let $G=\{e,a,b\}$ where $e\ne a\ne b$

wts $ab=e$

- BWOC assume $ab=a$
  - $\therefore b=a^{-1}ab=a^{-1}a=e$, contradicts that $b\ne e$
- BWOC assume $ab=b$
  - $\therefore a=abb^{-1}=bb^{-1}=e$, contradicts that $a\ne e$

$\therefore ab=e$

$\therefore ab=e=babb^{-1}=ba$

$\therefore$ cayley table is symmetric, thus abelian
</details>

<details class="example list-none">
<summary>$A$ has closed asso operator, right iden, right inv $\implies A$ is group</summary>
<details>
<summary>**Proof.** wts $\forall a\in A\enspace a^2=a\implies a=e$</summary>
let any $a\in A$ st $a^2=a$

- $\therefore\exists b\in A$ st $ab=e$
- $\therefore a=a(ab)=(aa)b=ab=e$ as wanted
</details>

let any $a\in A$

- $\therefore\exists b\in A$ st $ab=e$
- $\therefore (ba)^2=(ba)(ba)=b(ab)a=ba$
- $\therefore ba=e=ab$ (1)
- $\therefore ea=(ab)a=a(ba)=ae=a$ (2)

$\therefore$ closed, assoc, iden, inv

$\therefore$ is group $\quad\square$
</details>

## Group Definition

<details class="definition" open>
<summary>let set $G$ with binary operation $G\times G\to G;(a,b)\mapsto ab$</summary>
$G$ is a group iff

|||
|-:|-|
| closure | $a,b\in G\implies ab\in G$
| associativity | $\forall a,b,c\in G\quad (ab)c=a(bc)$
  identity | $\exists e\in G\enspace\forall a\in G\quad ae=ea=a$
  inverse | $\forall a\in G\enspace\exists b\in G\quad ab=ba=e$
</details>

<details class="definition" open>
<summary>abelian</summary>
group $G$ is abelian iff $\forall a,b\in G\enspace ab=ba$
</details>

## Equivalence

<details class="definition" open>
<summary>an equivalence relation on set $S$ is $R\sube S\times S$ such that</summary>
for any $a,b,c\in S$
- $(a,a)\in R$ (reflexive)
- $(a,b)\in R\implies(b,a)\in R$ (symmetric)
- $(a,b)\in R$ and $(b,c)\in R\implies(a,c)\in R$ (transitive)

denote $(a,b)\in R$ as $a\sim b$
</details>

<details class="definition" open>
<summary>equivalence class</summary>
for $a\in S$, the **equivalence class** of $S$ containing $a$ is 
$$
[a]=\{b\in S:b\sim a\}
$$
</details>

<details class="theorem" open>
<summary>partitions</summary>
the collection of $[a]$ for any $a\in S$ forms a partition of $S$
</details>

<details class="example list-none" open>
<summary>$|G|=2k\implies\exists a\ne e\in G$ st $a^2=e$</summary>

let $A=\{a\in G:a=a^{-1}\}$ and $B=\{b\in G:b\ne b^{-1}\}$

$\because$ for any $b\ne b^{-1}\in G$, we have $b\in B\iff b^{-1}\in B$\
$\therefore|B|$ is even\
$\because A$ and $B$ partition $G$ and $|G|$ even\
$\therefore|A|=|G|-|B|$ is even\
$\because e\in A$ since $e=e^{-1}$\
$\therefore|A|\ge1$, thus $|A|\ge2$ since $|A|$ even\
$\therefore\exists a\ne e\in A$, thus $a=a^{-1}$, thus $a^2=e\quad\square$

</details>
