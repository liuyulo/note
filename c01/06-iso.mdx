<details class="theorem" open>
<summary>Cayley's theorem</summary>
every group is isomorphic to a permutation group

<details>
<summary>**Proof.** let any group $G$</summary>

for any $g\in G$, define permutation $T_g:G\to G$ where $T_g(a)=ga$

- $\therefore T_g$ is permutation i.e. bijective endofunction

let $G'=\{T_g:g\in G\}$

- $\therefore G'$ is group (of permutations) under function composition

let $\psi:G\to G'$ where $\psi(a)=T_a$

- $\therefore\psi$ is isomorphism

</details>
</details>

## Automorphism & Inner

<details class="definition" open>
<summary>automorphism is isomorphism & endomorphism</summary>
$\operatorname{Aut}(G)$ is the group of automorphisms in $G$
</details>

<details class="definition" open>
<summary>inner automorphism is $\varphi_a:G\to G$ for any $a\in G$</summary>
$$
\varphi_a(g)=aga^{-1}
$$

$\operatorname{Inn}(G)=\{\varphi_a:a\in G\}$ is a group
</details>

<details class="example" open>
<summary>any aut $\varphi:\mathbb Q\to\mathbb Q$ (under add) has form $\varphi(x)=x\varphi(1)$</summary>
let any $x=n/m\in\mathbb Q$

$$
\begin{aligned}
  \varphi(x)&=\varphi(n/m)=\varphi\left(\sum^n\frac1m\right)=\sum^n\varphi\left(1/m\right)=n\varphi(1/m)\\
  &=n\frac mm\varphi(1/m)=\frac nmm\phi(1/m)=\frac nm\sum^m\varphi(1/m)\\
  &=\frac nm\varphi\left(\sum^m\frac1m\right)=\frac nm\varphi(1)=x\varphi(1)\quad\square
\end{aligned}
$$
</details>

<details class="example">
<summary>$G\cong H\implies\operatorname{Aut}(G)\cong\operatorname{Aut}(H)$</summary>

let $\varphi:G\to H$ be an isomorphism

let $\psi:\operatorname{Aut}(G)\to\operatorname{Aut}(H)$ 
 -  $\psi(\alpha)=\varphi\alpha\varphi^{-1}$ for any $\alpha\in\operatorname{Aut}(G)$
<details>
<summary>**Proof.** $\psi$ hom</summary> 
let any $\alpha_1,\alpha_2\in\operatorname{Aut}(G)$

$$
\begin{aligned}
  \psi(\alpha_1\alpha_2)&=\varphi(\alpha_1\alpha_2)\varphi^{-1}\\
  &=(\varphi\alpha_1\varphi^{-1})(\varphi\alpha_2\varphi^{-1})\\
  &=\psi(\alpha_1)\psi(\alpha_2)
\end{aligned}
$$
</details>

<details>
<summary>**Proof.** $\psi$ bij</summary>
let $\mu:\operatorname{Aut}(H)\to\operatorname{Aut}(G)$
  - $\mu(\beta)=\varphi^{-1}\beta\varphi$

let any $\alpha\in\operatorname{Aut}(G)$

$$
(\mu\psi)(\alpha)=\mu(\varphi\alpha\varphi^{-1})=\varphi^{-1}\varphi\alpha\varphi^{-1}\varphi=\alpha
$$

$\therefore\mu\psi=1_{\operatorname{Aut}(G)}$, thus bijective
</details>
</details>

## Properties

<details class="theorem" open>
<summary>properties of **hom**omorphism on elements</summary>

|perserves| $\varphi:G\to G'$ |
|-|-|
| identity | $\varphi(e)=e$
| exponent | $\varphi(a^n)=\varphi(a)^n$
| commute | $ab=ba\iff\varphi(ab)=\varphi(ba)$
</details>
<details class="theorem" open>
<summary>properties of **hom**omorphism on groups</summary>
for $\varphi:G\to G'$
- for any set $A\sube G$, define $\varphi(A)=\{\varphi(a):a\in A\}\sube \varphi(G)$
- $\varphi(G)=G'$

| perserves | $\varphi:G\to G'$
|-|-|
| subgroup | $H\le G\iff\varphi(H)\le \varphi(G)$
| cyclic | $G=\langle a\rangle\iff \varphi(G)=\langle\varphi(a)\rangle$
| abelian | $G$ abelian $\iff \varphi(G)$ abelian
</details>

<details class="theorem" open>
<summary>properties of **iso**morphism on elements</summary>

- $\varphi(Z(G))=Z(\varphi(G))$

for any $a\in G$

- $|a|=|\varphi(a)|$
- for any $n\in\mathbb N$
  - $|\{b:b^n=a\}|=|\{b:b^n=\varphi(a)\}|$
- for finite $G$
  - $G$ and $\varphi(G)$ have the same number of elements of any order

</details>

## Isomorphic Groups

<details class="theorem" open>
<summary>$(\mathbb Q,+)$ is not isomorphic to $(\mathbb Q^*,\cdot)$</summary>
<details>
<summary>**Proof.** BWOC assume $\varphi:\mathbb Q\to\mathbb Q^*$ is isomorphism</summary>

$\therefore\exists a\in \mathbb Q\enspace\varphi(a)=-1$

$ \therefore -1=\varphi(a)=\varphi(1/2a+1/2a)=\varphi(1/2a)\varphi(1/2a)=\varphi(1/2a)^2\ge0$

$\therefore$ contradicts that $-1<0\quad\square$
</details>
</details>

<details class="theorem" open>
<summary>any finite cyclic group is isomorphic to $(\mathbb Z_n,+_n)$</summary>
- **Proof.** same
</details>

<details class="theorem" open>
<summary>any inifinite cyclic group is isomorphic to $(\mathbb Z,+)$</summary>
- **Proof.** let $\varphi:\mathbb Z\to\langle a\rangle$ where $\varphi(n)=a^n$
</details>

## Isomorphism

<details class="definition" open>
<summary>a homomorphism from group $G$ to $\overline G$ is $\varphi:G\to\overline G$ such that</summary>

for any $a,b\in G$

$$
\varphi(ab)=\varphi(a)\varphi(b)
$$

<details>
<summary>i.e. this digram commutes</summary>

for group $(G,\cdot)$ and $(\overline G,\odot)$ with homomorphism $\varphi:G\to\overline G$

<details class="definition" open>
<summary>isomorphism</summary>
isomorphism is a bijective homomorphism

- $G\cong G'$ iff $\exists$ iso $\varphi:G\to G'$

wts $G\cong G'$

1. let $\varphi:G\to G'$ be a map
4. wts $\forall a,b\in G\enspace\varphi(ab)=\varphi(a)\varphi(b)$ (hom)
2. wts $\forall a,b\in G\enspace\varphi(a)=\varphi(b)\implies a=b$ (inj)
3. wts $\forall a'\in G'\enspace\exists a\in G\enspace\varphi(a)=a'$ (surj)
</details>

<details class="definition" open>
<summary>more morphisms</summary>
- endomorphism is $\varphi:G\to G$
- automorphism is endomorphism & isomorphism
</details>
