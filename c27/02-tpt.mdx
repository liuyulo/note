## Interior and Closure

<details class="definition" open>
<summary>interior</summary>

for space $(X,\mathcal T)$, the **interior** of $A\sube X$ is

$$
A^\circ=\bigcup\{U\in\mathcal T:U\sube A\}
$$

- that is, union of open sets contained in $A$


<div class="flex justify-center">
$a\in A^\circ\iff a$ has a neighborhood contained in $A$
</div>
</details>

<details class="example" open>
<summary>interior commutes with intersection</summary>

let any $A,B\subseteq X$

$$
(A\cap B)^\circ=A^\circ\cap B^\circ
$$

<details class="list-none">
<summary>**Proof.**</summary>

$(\subseteq)$ let any $x\in (A\cap B)^\circ$

$\therefore\exists x\in U\subseteq A\cap B$

$\therefore x\in U\subseteq A$ and $x\in U\subseteq B$

$\therefore x\in A^\circ$ and $x\in B^\circ$, thus $x\in A^\circ\cap B^\circ$

$(\impliedby )$ let any $x\in A^\circ\cap B^\circ$

$\therefore\exists x\in U_A\subseteq A$ and $\exists x\in U_B\subseteq B$

$\therefore x\in U_A\cap U_B\subseteq A\cap B$ where $U_A\cap U_B$ open

$\therefore  x\in (A\cap B)^\circ\enspace\square$
</details>

notice 
- $A^\circ\cup B^\circ\subseteq(A\cup B)^\circ$
- $[0,1]^\circ\cup[1,2]^\circ=(0,1)\cup(1,2)\subset(0,2)=[0,2]^\circ$
</details>


<details class="definition" open>
<summary>closure</summary>
for space $(X,\mathcal T)$, the **closure** of $A\sube X$ is

$$
\bar A=\bigcap\{U^c\supe A:U\in\mathcal T\}
$$

- that is, intersection of closed sets containing $A$

<div class="flex justify-center">
$a\in \overline A\iff$ any neighborhood of $a$ intersects with $A$\
$a\in \overline A\iff$ any closed set containing $A$ contains $a$
</div>

</details>

<details class="theorem" open>
<summary>interior vs closure</summary>

for any space $(X,\mathcal T)$ and $A\sube X$

$$
A^\circ\sube A\sube\bar A
$$

- $A^\circ=A\iff A$ is open
- $A=\bar A\iff A$ is closed

<details class="list-none">
<summary>**Proof.**</summary>
wts $A^\circ=A\iff A\in\mathcal T$

- $(\implies)A^\circ=A$ is union of open sets, thus open
- $(\impliedby)$ notice $A^\circ\sube A$ by definition
  - let any $a\in A$
  - $\therefore A$ is a nei of $a$ contained in $A$
  - $\therefore a\in A^\circ\quad\square$

wts $A=\bar A\iff A^c\in\mathcal T$ 

- $(\implies)A=\bar A$ is intersection of closed sets, thus closed
- $(\impliedby)$ notice $A\sube\bar A$ by definition
- BWOC assume $\exists a\in\bar A-A$
- $\therefore a\in A^c$  where $A^c$ is nei of $a$
- $\therefore$ contradicts that $A^c$ intersects $A$
</details>
</details>

## Boundary and Accumulation

<details class="definition" open>
<summary>boundary</summary>
for space $(X,\mathcal T)$, the **boundary** of $A\sube X$ is

$$
\partial A=\overline A\cap\overline{A^c}
$$

or equivalently
<div class="flex justify-center">
$a\in\partial A\iff$ any neighborhood of $a$ intersects with $A$ and $A^c$
</div>

<details class="list-none">
<summary>**Proof.**</summary>
for any $x\in X$

$$
\begin{align*}
    x\in\overline A\cap\overline{A^c}\iff&\forall U\in\mathcal T\enspace A\sube U^c\implies x\in U^c\tag{$x\in\overline A$}\\
    &\forall U\in\mathcal T\enspace A^c\sube U^c\implies x\in U^c\tag{$x\in\overline{A^c}$}\\
    \iff&\forall U\in\mathcal T\enspace A\sube U^c\text{ or }A^c\sube U^c\implies x\notin U\\
    \iff&\forall U\in\mathcal T\enspace x\in U\implies A\not\sube U^c\text{ and }A^c\not\sube U^c\\
    \iff&\forall U\in\mathcal T\enspace x\in U\implies A\cap U\ne\varnothing\text{ and }A^c\cap U\ne\varnothing\\
    \iff&\text{any neighborhood of }x\text{ intersects }A\text{ and }A^c\enspace\square
\end{align*}
$$
</details>
</details>

<details class="example" open>
<summary>interior and boundary partitions closure</summary>

for $A\sube X$
$$
\overline A=A^\circ\sqcup\partial A
$$

<details class="list-none">
<summary>**Proof.**</summary>
wts disjoint, BWOC assume $\exists a\in A^\circ\cap\partial A$

- $\because A^\circ$ is a neighborhood of $a$ and $a$ is a boundary point of $A$
- $\therefore A^c\cap A^\circ\ne\varnothing$, contradicts that $A^\circ\sube A$

$(\implies)$ let any $a\in\bar A-\partial A$

- $\therefore a$ has some nei $U$ that disjoints $A$ or $A^c$
- $\because$ any nei of $a$ intersects $A$
- $\therefore U$ disjoints $A^c$, thus $a\in U\sube A$
- $\therefore a\in A^\circ$

$(\impliedby)$

- notice $\partial A\sube\bar A$ by definition
- let any $a\in A^\circ$
  - $\therefore a\in A$
  - $\therefore$ any nei of $a$ intersects with $A$ (namely, on $a$)
</details>
</details>

<details class="example" open>
<summary>empty boundary iff clopen</summary>
for $A\sube X$

$$
\partial A=\varnothing\iff A,A^c\in\mathcal T
$$

<details class="list-none">
<summary>**Proof.**</summary>
$(\implies)$ assume $\partial A=\varnothing$

$$
\begin{align*}
  \therefore&&\bar A&=A^\circ\cup\partial A=A^\circ\\
  \because&& A^\circ&\sube A\sube\bar A\\
  \therefore&&A^\circ&=A=\bar A\\
  \therefore&& A,A^c&\in\mathcal T
\end{align*}
$$

$(\impliedby)$ assume $A,A^c\in\mathcal T$

$$
\begin{align*}
  \therefore&& A&=\overline A=A^\circ\sqcup\partial A=A\sqcup\partial A\\
  \therefore&&\partial A&=\varnothing\quad\square
\end{align*}
$$
</details>
</details>

<details class="definition" open>
<summary>accumulation point</summary>

for space $(X,\mathcal T)$, and $A\sube X$

<div class="flex justify-center">

$x\in X$ is a **accumulation** point of $A\iff U-\{x\}$ intersects $A$ for any neighborhood $U$ of $x$
</div>

- $A'$ denotes the set of accumulation points of $A$
</details>

<details class="theorem" open>
<summary>closure is union with accumulation points</summary>

for space $(X,\mathcal T)$ and $A\sube X$

$$
\overline A=A\cup A'
$$

<details class="list-none">
<summary>**Proof.**</summary>
$(\implies)$ let any $a\in \bar A-A$

- let any neighborhood $U$ of $a$
- $\therefore U$ intersects $A$
- $\because a\notin A$
- $\therefore U-\{a\}$ intersects $A$
- $\therefore a\in A'$

$(\impliedby)$ notice $A\sube\bar A$

- let any $a\in A'$ and any neighborhood $U$ of $a$
- $\therefore U-\{a\}$ intersects $A$
- $\therefore U$ intersects $A$
- $\therefore a\in\bar A\quad\square$
</details>

**Corollary.** $A$ is closed $\iff A'\sube A$
</details>

<details class="definition" open>
<summary>more accumulations</summary>

for any $A;x\subseteq X$

|||
|-:|:-|
| $x$ is **accumulation** point of $A$ | $\vert U^*\cap A\vert\gt0$ for any nei $U$ of $x$|

| $x$ is **complete-accumulation** point of $A$ | $\vert U\cap A\vert=\vert A\vert$ for any nei $U$ of $x$|

</details>
