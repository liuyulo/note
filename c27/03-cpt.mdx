## Compactness (Open Cover)

<details class="definition" open>
<summary>open cover and compactness</summary>

for space $(X,\mathcal T)$

- collection $\mathcal{C} \subseteq\mathcal{T} $ is **open cover** of $X$ iff

$$
X\subseteq\bigcup\mathcal{C} 
$$

space $X$ is **compact** iff

<div class="flex justify-center">

every open cover has finite subcover
</div>
</details>


<details class="theorem" open>
<summary>closed subspace of compact space is compact</summary>

for compact space $X$

<div class="flex justify-center">

subspace $A\sube X$ is closed $\implies A$ is compact
</div>

<details class="list-none">
<summary>**Proof.**</summary>
let any open cover $\mathcal C_A=\{U_\alpha\cap A\}$ of $A$ where $ U_\alpha\in\mathcal T_X$

- let $\mathcal C_X=\{U_\alpha\}\cup\{X-A\}$
- $\therefore\mathcal C_X$ is open cover of $X$, thus has some finite subcover $\mathcal C_0$
- $\therefore\{U\cap A:U\in\mathcal C_0\}$ is finite open cover of $A$
- $\therefore A$ compact $\quad\square$
</details>
</details>

<details class="theorem" open>
<summary>compact subspace of Hausdorff space is closed</summary>
for any $T_2$ space $X$

<div class="flex justify-center">
subspace $A\sube X$ is compact $\implies A$ is closed
</div>

<details class="list-none">
<summary>**Proof.**</summary>
wts $X-A\sube(X-A)^\circ$

- let any $x\in X-A$
- for any $a\in A$, let $U_a,V_a$ be disjoint neighborhoods of $x$ and $a$ respectively
- $\therefore\{V_a\}_{a\in A}$ is cover of $A$, open in $X$, has some finite cover of $A$, say

$$
\{V_{a_1},\cdots,V_{a_n}\}\sube\{V_a\}_{a\in A}
$$

- $\therefore$ let $V=\bigcup_iV_{a_i}$, open in $X$ and contains $A$
- $\phantom\therefore$ let $U=\bigcap_iU_{a_i}$, open in $X$ and disjoints $V$
- $\therefore x\in U\sube X-A$, thus $x\in(X-A)^\circ$

$\therefore X-A$ is open, thus $A$ is closed $\quad\square$
</details>
</details>

<details class="example" open>
<summary></summary>

let decreasing $K_1\supseteq K_2\supseteq\cdots$ of nonempty compact sets


<div class="flex justify-center">

$\bigcap_iK_i$ nonempty
</div>

<details class="list-none">
<summary>**Proof.**</summary>

BWOC assume $K=\bigcap_iK_i$ empty

let $U_i=K_i^c$ be open, thus $U_1\subseteq U_2\subseteq\cdots$

$\therefore K_{n_k}\subseteq K_1\subseteq U_{n_k}$ where $K_{n_k}$ nonempty

$\therefore$ contradicts that $K_{n_k}$ disjoints $U_{n_k}\enspace\square$
</details>

## Limit Point Compactness

<details class="definition" open>
<summary>limit point compactness</summary>
space $X$ is **limit point compact** iff

<div class="flex justify-center">
any infinite subset of $X$ has a accumulation point
</div>
</details>

<details class="theorem" open>
<summary>compact implies limit point compact</summary>

<details class="list-none">
<summary>**Proof.**</summary>
assume space $X$ is compact, let any $A\sube X$

- <span title="by way of contraposition">BWOP</span> assume $A$ has no limit point, that is, $A'=\varnothing$

- $\therefore$ for any $a\in A$, there is some neighborhood $U_a$ of $a$ that only intersects $A$ at $a$ (*)
- let $\mathcal C=\{U_a\}_{a\in A}$ by open cover of $A$
- $\because A$ is closed, since $A'\sube A$
- $\therefore A$ is compact, since $X$ is compact
- $\therefore\mathcal C$ has some finite subcover $\mathcal C_0$ that covers $A$
- $\because$ each $U\in\mathcal C_0\sube\mathcal C$ only has one point in $A$ (by *)
- $\therefore A$ is finite as $\mathcal C_0$ is finite $\quad\square$
</details>
</details>

## Compactness on Reals

<details class="theorem" open>
<summary>Heine-Borel</summary>

for subspace $A\subseteq\mathbb{R}$

<div class="flex justify-center">

$A$ compact $\iff A$ closed and bounded
</div>

<details class="list-none">
<summary>**Proof.**</summary>

$(\implies )$ assume $A$ compact

<details>
<summary>wts $A$ bounded</summary>

let open cover $\mathcal{C} $ of $A$ be
  $$
  \mathcal{C} =\{(-n,n):n\in\mathbb{Z}^+\}
  $$
$\therefore\mathcal{C} $ has some finite subcover $\mathcal{C} _0=\{(-n_i,n_i)\}_{i=1}^N$

$\therefore A\subseteq\bigcup\mathcal{C} _0=(-n_N,n_N)$ is bounded


</details>


<details>
<summary>wts $A$ closed, that is, $A^c\subseteq(\mathbb{R}-A)^\circ$</summary>

tl;dr $\mathbb{R}$ is T2

- namely, any $x\in A^c$ and any $a\in A$ are separated by

$$
  U_a=N(x,|x-a|/2)\quad V_a=N(a,|x-a|/2)
$$

</details>

$(\impliedby )$ assume $A$ closed and bounded (WLOG assume $A$ infinite)


let $A$ bounded by $b\in\mathbb{R}^+$

<details>
<summary>wts $I=[-b,b]$ compact</summary>

let any open cover $\mathcal{C} $ of $I=[-b,b]$

let $B\subseteq I$ where

<div class="flex justify-center">

$x\in B\iff\mathcal{C} $ has some finite cover that covers $[-b,x]$
</div>

- notice $B$ is interval, that is, $x\in B\implies [a,x]\subseteq B$

let $\beta=\sup B$<span class="float-right">(completenss: $-b\in B$ and $B\subseteq\mathbb{R}$ bounded)</span>

$\therefore -b\le\beta\le b$ since $-b\in A\le b$

$\therefore\mathcal{C} $ covers $\beta$, thus $\exists U\in\mathcal{C} $ st $\beta\in N(\beta,\varepsilon)\subseteq U$ for some $\varepsilon\gt0$

wts $\beta\in B$

- $\because\exists q\in\mathbb{R}$ st $\beta-\varepsilon\lt q\lt \beta$<span class="float-right">(density)</span>
- $\because q\in B$<span class="float-right">($B$ is interval and $\beta$ is supremum)</span>

- $\therefore\exists\mathcal{C} _0\in[\mathcal{C} ]^{\lt\aleph_0}$ that covers $[-b,q]$
- $\therefore\mathcal{C} _0\cup\{U\}\in[\mathcal{C} ]^{\lt\aleph_0}$ covers $[-b,\beta+\varepsilon)$, thus covers $[-b,\beta]$

wts $\beta=b$, BWOC assume $\beta\lt b$

- $\therefore\exists q\in\mathbb{R}$ st $-b\le\beta\lt q\lt \min\{\beta+\varepsilon,b\}$<span class="float-right">(density)</span>
- $\therefore \mathcal{C} _0\cup\{U\}$ covers $[-b,q]\subseteq[-b,b]$, thus $q\in B$ where $q\gt\beta$
- $\therefore$ contradicts that $\beta$ is supremum

$\therefore\mathcal{C} _0\cup\{U\}$ is finite subcover that covers $[-b,b]$
</details>

<details>
<summary>wts $A\subseteq I$ is compact</summary>
(tl;dr because $A$ is closed)

- let any open cover $\mathcal{C} _A$ of $A$
- $\therefore\mathcal{C}_I=\mathcal{C}_A\cup\{A^c\}$ is open cover of $I$
- $\therefore\exists\mathcal{C}_0\in[\mathcal{C}_I]^{\lt\aleph_0}$ that covers $I$, thus covers $A\enspace\square$
</details>

</details>

</details>


<details class="theorem" open>
<summary>Bolzano-Weierstrass</summary>

for subspace $A\subseteq\mathbb{R}$

<div class="flex justify-center">

$A$ is infinite and bounded $\implies A$ has some accumulation point
</div>

<details class="list-none">
<summary>**Proof.**</summary>

BWOC assume $A'=\varnothing$

- $\therefore A$ closed, since $A'\subseteq A$
- $\therefore A$ compact (HB)
- $\because\forall a\in A\quad\exists N^*(a,\varepsilon_a)$ that disjoints $A$ (that is, not accumulation point)
- $\therefore$ every point in $A$ is isolated (since $N(a,\varepsilon_a)$ only intersects $A$ at $a$)
- $\therefore$ the open cover $\mathcal{C} =\{N(a,\varepsilon_a):a\in A\}$ has no finite subcover (since $A$ infinite)
- $\therefore$ contradicts that $A$ compact $\enspace\square$
</details>
</details>

### Compactness vs Interesction Property

<details class="definition" open>
<summary>wat</summary>

for set $I$

$$
[I]^{\lt\aleph_0}=\{I_0\subseteq I:|I_0|\lt\aleph_0\}
$$
- that is, system of finte subsets of $I$
</details>

<details class="definition" open>
<summary>finite intersection property</summary>

for system $\mathcal{S} $

<div class="flex justify-center">

$\mathcal{S} $ **FIP** $\iff\forall\mathcal{S} _0\in[\mathcal{S} ]^{\lt\aleph_0}\quad\bigcap\mathcal{S} _0\ne\varnothing$
</div>

- that is, every finite subset has nonempty intersection
</details>

<details class="theorem" open>
<summary>compactness (finitely satisfiable implies satisfiable)</summary>

for space $X$

<div class="flex justify-center">

$X$ compact $\iff$ any FIP collection of closed sets has nonempty intersection
</div>

<details class="list-none">
<summary>**Proof.** contrapositive</summary>

$(\implies )$ assume $\exists\mathcal{K} =\{K_\alpha\}_{\alpha\in I}$ st FIP but $\bigcap\mathcal{K} =\varnothing$

- let $\mathcal{C} =\{U_\alpha\}$ where $U_\alpha=K_\alpha^c$
- $\therefore\mathcal{C} $ is open cover of $X$, since $\bigcup\mathcal{C} =(\bigcap\mathcal{K} )^c=X$
- let any finite subcollection $I_0\in[I]^{\lt\aleph_0}$
- $\therefore\bigcap_{\alpha\in I_0}K_\alpha\ne\varnothing$ (FIP)
- $\therefore\bigcup_{\alpha\in I_0}U_\alpha=(\bigcap_{\alpha\in I_0}K_\alpha)^c\subset X$, thus is not cover of $X$
- $\therefore\mathcal{C} $ has no finite subcover, thus $X$ not compact

$(\impliedby )$ assume $X$ not compact

- $\therefore X$ has some open cover $\mathcal{C} =\{U_\alpha\}_{\alpha\in I}$ with no finite subcover
- let $\mathcal{K} =\{K_\alpha\}_{\alpha\in I}$ where $K_\alpha=U_\alpha^c$
- $\because\mathcal{C} $ has no finite subcover
- $\therefore$ for any $I_0\in[I]^{\lt\aleph_0}\quad\bigcup_{\alpha\in I_0}U_\alpha\subset X$, thus

$$
\bigcap_{\alpha\in I_0}K_\alpha=\bigcap_{\alpha\in I_0}U_\alpha^c=\left(\bigcup_{\alpha\in I_0}U_\alpha\right)^c\ne\varnothing
$$

- $\therefore\mathcal{K} $ FIP, but $\bigcap\mathcal{K} =(\bigcup\mathcal{C} )^c=\varnothing\enspace\square$
</details>

**Corollary.** for any FIP collection $\{K_a\subseteq\mathbb{R}\}$ of compact sets

<div class="flex justify-center">

$\bigcap_\alpha K_\alpha$ is also compact
</div>
</details>

<details class="example" open>
<summary>reals no</summary>

for $\mathbb{R}$, let $\mathcal{K} =\{[n,\infty)\}_{n\in\mathbb{N}}$

- $\mathcal{K} $ is FIP collection of closed sets, but has empty intersection
</details>
