## Topological Spaces

<details class="definition" id="top" open>
<summary>topology</summary>

a **topology** on a set $X$ is the subset $\mathcal T\sube\mathcal P(X)$  such that

- $\mathcal T$ has $\varnothing$ and $X$
- $\mathcal T$ is closed under *any* union of subcollections
- $\mathcal T$ is closed under *finite* intersection

</details>

<details class="definition" open>
<summary>names</summary>

- $(X,\mathcal T)$ is a **topological space**
- $U\sube X$ is **open** $\iff U\in\mathcal T$
- open set $U$ is a **neighborhood** of $x\in X\iff x\in U$
</details>

<details class="example" id="tops" open>
<summary>topologies</summary>

- $\{X,\varnothing\}$ is the **trivial** topology on set $X$
- $\mathcal P(X)$ is the **discrete** topololy on set $X$
</details>

<details class="definition" open>
<summary></summary>
for topology $\mathcal T$ and $\mathcal T'$ on set $X$

- $\mathcal T$ is **smaller** than $\mathcal T'\iff \mathcal T\sube\mathcal T'$
- $\mathcal T$ is **larger** than $\mathcal T'\iff\mathcal T\supe\mathcal T'$
- $\mathcal T$ and $\mathcal T'$ **comparable** $\iff$ smaller or larger
</details>

### Closed Sets

<details class="definition" open>
<summary>closed set</summary>

for space $(X,\mathcal T)$

<div class="flex justify-center">
set $A\sube X$ is **closed** $\iff A^c=X-A$ is open
</div>
</details>

<details class="theorem" open>
<summary>closed sets in spaces</summary>
for space $(X,\mathcal T)$

- $\varnothing$ and $X$ are closed
- any intersection of closed sets are closed
- any finite union of closed sets are closed
</details>

### Basis

<details class="definition" open>
<summary>basis</summary>

for set $X$, set $\mathcal B$ is **basis** of $X$ iff all

- every point in $X$ is contained in some basis element


$$
\forall x\in X\quad\exists B\in\mathcal B\quad x\in B
$$

- every pt in some intersection of basis elements is contained in some basis element

$$
\forall B_1,B_2\in\mathcal B\quad\forall x\in B_1\cap B_2\quad\exists B_3\in\mathcal B\quad x\in B_3\sub B_1\cap B_2
$$

the topology **generated** by basis $\mathcal B$ on set $X$ is


$$
\mathcal T=\left\{\bigcup\mathcal A:\mathcal A\sube\mathcal B\right\}
$$

- that is, unions of some basis elements

</details>

<details class="example" id="bexample" open>
<summary></summary>
- $\mathcal B=\{\{a\}:a\in X\}$ generates the discrete topology on set $X$
</details>

### Subspace

<details class="definition" open>
<summary>subspace</summary>
for space $(X,\mathcal T_X)$ and $A\sube X$
$$
\mathcal T_A=\{A\cap U:U\in\mathcal T_A\}
$$

$(A,\mathcal T_A)$ is a **subspace** of $X$

</details>

<details class="theorem" open>
<summary>space basis to subspace basis</summary>
for basis $\mathcal B_X$ of space $X$ 

$$
\mathcal B_A=\{A\cap B:B\in\mathcal B_X\}
$$

is a basis for $A\sube X$
</details>

## Metric Topology

<details class="definition" open>
<summary>metric</summary>
a **metric** on set $X$ is $d:X^2\to\mathbb R$ where

$$
\begin{align*}
  \forall x\forall y&& d(x,y)&=0\iff x=y\\
  \forall x\forall y&& d(x,y)&=d(y,x)\gt0\\
  \forall x\forall y\forall z&& d(x,z)&\le d(x,y)+d(y,z)
\end{align*}
$$

for $\varepsilon\gt0$, the **ε-ball** centered at $x\in X$ is

$$
B(x,\varepsilon)=\{y\in X:d(x,y)\lt\varepsilon\}
$$

</details>

<details class="definition" id="mtop" open>
<summary>metric topology</summary>

for metric $d:X^2\to\mathbb R$, the **metric topology** induced by $d$ is the topology generated by

$$
\mathcal B=\{B(x,\varepsilon):x\in X\text{ and }\varepsilon\gt0\}
$$

- euclidean distance `=>` euclidean topology
- `d(x,x) = 0, d(x,y) = 1 =>` discreate topology

</details>
