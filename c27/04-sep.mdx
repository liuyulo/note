## The Separation Axioms

<details class="definition" open>
<summary>separate</summary>

for space $X$

- $a,b\in X$ are **separated** $\iff$ each lies in some nei that contain't the other point
- $a,b\in X$ are **separated by neighborhood** $\iff$ each lies in some disjoint neis

- similar definition for 
  - $a\in X$ and $B\sube X$ separated by neighborhood
  - $A,B\sube X$ separated by neighborhood
</details>

<details class="definition" open>
<summary>$T_1$ points are separated</summary>
<div class="flex justify-center">

space $X$ is $T_1\iff$ distinct points are separated
</div>

or equivalently

1. every singleton set is closed
1. every finite set is closed
1. $x\in X$ is limit point of $A\subseteq X\iff x$ is accumulation point of $X$

<details class="list-none">
<summary>**Proof.** $T_1\implies 1$</summary>

let any distinct $a,b\in X$

- $\therefore a$ has some neighborhood $U$ such that $b\notin U$
- $\therefore a\notin\overline{\{b\}}$
- $\therefore\overline{\{b\}}=\{b\}$, thus $\{b\}$ is closed
</details>

<details class="list-none">
<summary>**Proof.** $T_1\implies3$</summary>

$(\implies )$ let any limit point $x$ of $A$ and any neighborhood $U$ of $x$

- $\therefore|U\cap A|\ge\aleph_0$
- $\therefore U$ intersects $A$ at some point other than $x$
- $\therefore x$ is accumulation point of $A$

$(\impliedby )$ let any accumulation point $x$ of $A$ 

- BWOC assume $x$ has some neighborhood $U$ st $|U\cap A|$ is finite
- let $A_0=\{a_1,\cdots,a_n\}=(U\cap A)-\{x\}\subseteq U\cap A$
- $\because A_0$ is closed and $x\notin A_0$<span style="float: right;">(T1)</span>
- $\therefore$ let $N=A^c_0$ be open and contains $x$
- $\therefore N$ is neighborhood of $x$, but $N^*$ disjonts $A$, contradcts that $x$ is acc $\enspace\square$


</details>
