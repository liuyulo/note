## Completeness
<details class="theorem" open>
<summary>real numbers are complete</summary>
for any nonempty $A\sube\mathbb R$
<div class="flex justify-center">
$A$ is bounded $\implies A$ has supremum and infimum
</div>
</details>

<details class="theorem" open>
<summary>archimedean property</summary>
$$
\forall a,b\in\mathbb R^+\enspace\exists n\in\mathbb n\enspace a\lt nb
$$

<details class="list-none">
<summary>**Proof.**</summary>

BWOC assume $\exists a,b\in\mathbb R\enspace\forall n\in\mathbb N\enspace na\le b$

- $\therefore b$ is upper bound of $S=\{na:n\in\mathbb N\}$
- $\therefore\exists\beta=\sup S\in\mathbb R$ 
- $\because\beta-a\lt\beta$
- $\therefore\beta-a$ is not ubd of $S$
- $\therefore\exists n'\in\mathbb N\enspace\beta-a\lt n'a$
- $\therefore\beta\lt(n'+1)a$, contradicts that $\beta$ is ubd of $S\quad\square$
</details>
</details>

<details class="theorem" open>
<summary>density</summary>
$$
\forall c,d\in\mathbb R\enspace\exists q\in\mathbb Q\enspace(c\lt d\implies c\lt q\lt d)
$$

<details class="list-none">
<summary>**Proof.**</summary>
let any $c\lt d$
- let $\lambda=d-c\gt0$
- $\therefore\exists N\in\mathbb N\enspace N\lambda\gt1$ (arch)
- $\phantom\therefore\exists P\in\mathbb N\enspace\enspace P\gt Nc$
- $\phantom\therefore\exists Q\in\mathbb N\enspace\enspace Q\gt -Nc$
- $\therefore -Q\lt Nc\lt P$
- $\therefore\exists M\enspace -Q\le M-1\le Nc\lt M\le P$
- $\therefore c\lt M/N$ and $\therefore M\le Nc+1$
- $\therefore M/N\le c+1/N\lt c+\lambda=d$
- $\therefore c\lt M/N\lt d\quad\square$
</details>
</details>


<details class="theorem" open>
<summary>triangle inequality</summary>

$$
  |a|-|b|\le|a+b|\le|a|+|b|\\
$$

<details>
<summary>**Proof.**</summary>

$$
\begin{align*}
  \because |a+b|&\le|a|+|b|\\
  \therefore|a|&=|a+b-b|\le|a+b|+|-b|=|a+b|+|b|\\
  \therefore|a+b|&\le|a|-|b|
\end{align*}
$$
</details>
</details>

## Extremum

<details class="definition" open>
<summary>supremum & infimum</summary>

for nonempty $A$ 

- $\alpha$ is **infimum** (denoted $\inf A$) iff
  - $a\ge\alpha$ for any $a\in A$ (is lower bound)
  - $\alpha\ge\alpha'$ for any lower bound $\alpha'$ (is greatest)
- $\beta$ is **supremum** (denoted $\sup A$) iff
  - $a\le\beta$ for any $a\in A$ (is upper bound)
  - $\beta'\le\beta$ for any upper bound $\beta'$ (is least)
</details>

<details class="definition" open>
<summary>sup and inf</summary>
for nonempty bounded $A$ and $\alpha,\beta$

$$
\begin{align*}
  \inf A=\alpha&\iff\forall\varepsilon\gt0\enspace\exists x\in A\enspace\alpha\le x\lt\alpha+\varepsilon\\
  \sup A=\beta&\iff\forall\varepsilon\gt0\enspace\exists x\in A\enspace\beta-\varepsilon\lt x\le\beta\\
\end{align*}
$$
</details>



<details class="theorem" open>
<summary>extremum of subset</summary>
for any nonempty $A\sube B\sube\mathbb R$

$$
\inf B\le\inf A\le\sup A\le\sup B
$$
</details>

<details class="example" open>
<summary>square root is real</summary>

$$
\exists x\in R\enspace x^2=2
$$

<details class="list-none">
<summary>**Proof.**</summary>

let $A=\{x\in[0,2]:x^2\lt 2\}$

- $\therefore 0\in A$ since $0^2=0\le 2$ and $0\in[0,2]$
- $\therefore A$ nonempty
- $\because x\le 2$ for any $x\in A$ by definition
- $\therefore A$ nonempty and bounded above
- $\therefore \exists\alpha\in\mathbb R$ such that $\alpha=\sup A$

BWOC assume $\alpha^2\lt 2$, wts $\alpha$ is not lower bound

<details>
<summary>aside</summary>

want $(\alpha+\varepsilon)^2\lt 2$

$\therefore$ want

$$
  (\alpha+\varepsilon)^2=\alpha^2+2\alpha\varepsilon+\varepsilon^2\lt\alpha^2+2\alpha\varepsilon+\varepsilon=\alpha^2+(2\alpha+1)\varepsilon\lt2\\
$$

$\therefore$ want

$$
0\lt\varepsilon\lt\frac{2-\alpha^2}{2\alpha+1}
$$

</details>

- $\because 1\le\alpha$ since $1\in A$, and $2-\alpha^2\gt0$
- $\therefore (2-\alpha^2)/(2\alpha+1)\gt0$
- $\therefore$ by density, $\exists\varepsilon\in\mathbb R$ st

$$
0\lt\varepsilon\lt\min\left\{1,\frac{2-\alpha^2}{2\alpha+1}\right\}
$$


- $\therefore$ we have $\alpha\lt\alpha+\varepsilon$ and

$$
\begin{align*}
  (\alpha+\varepsilon)^2&=\alpha^2+2\alpha\varepsilon+\varepsilon^2\le\alpha^2+2\alpha\varepsilon+\varepsilon\tag{$0\lt\varepsilon\lt1$}\\
  &=\alpha^2+(2\alpha+1)\varepsilon\lt\alpha^2+2-\alpha^2=2
\end{align*}
$$

- $\because 1\le\alpha\lt\alpha+\varepsilon\lt(\alpha+\varepsilon)^2\lt 2$ since $1\in A$
- $\therefore\alpha+\varepsilon\in[0,2]$, thus $\alpha+\varepsilon\in A$, contradicts that $\alpha$ is upper bound

BWOC assume $\alpha^2\gt2$, wts not least

<details>
<summary>aside</summary>

want

$$
\exists\varepsilon\gt0\enspace\forall x\in A\enspace x\le\alpha-\varepsilon\lt\alpha
$$

$\therefore$ want

$$
x^2\lt 2\lt(\alpha-\varepsilon)^2
$$

$\therefore$ want

$$
(\alpha-\varepsilon)^2=\alpha^2-2\alpha\varepsilon+\varepsilon^2\gt\alpha^2-2\alpha\varepsilon\gt2
$$

$\therefore$ want

$$
\begin{align*}
  &&\alpha^2&\gt 2+2\alpha\varepsilon\\
\therefore&&\alpha^2-2&\gt2\alpha\varepsilon\\
\therefore&&\varepsilon&\lt\frac{\alpha^2-2}{2\alpha}
\end{align*}
$$
</details>

- $\because 1\le\alpha$ since $1\in A$ 

- $\therefore$ let $\varepsilon\in\mathbb R$ be


$$
\varepsilon=\frac{\alpha^2-2}{2\alpha}
$$

- $\therefore$ for any $x\in A$

$$
\begin{align*}
  x^2&\lt 2=\alpha^2-2\alpha\frac{\alpha^2-2}{2\alpha}=\alpha^2-2\alpha\varepsilon\le\alpha^2-2\alpha\varepsilon+\varepsilon^2=(\alpha-\varepsilon)^2\\
  \therefore x&\le\alpha-\varepsilon\lt\alpha
\end{align*}
$$

- $\therefore\alpha-\varepsilon$ is upper bound, contradicts that $\alpha$ is supremum $\quad\square$
</details>
</details>

<details class="example" open>
<summary>inf with positive scalar</summary>

for nonempty $A\sube\mathbb R$ and $k\in\mathbb R^+$

$$
k\inf A=\inf(kA)
$$

<details class="list-none">
<summary>**Proof.**</summary>
let $\alpha=\inf A$

wts $k\alpha$ is lower bound of $kA$

- let any $ka\in kA$, thus $\alpha\le a$
- $\therefore k\alpha\lt ka$ as wanted

wts $k\alpha$ is infimum, BWOC assume $\exists\alpha'\in\mathbb R\enspace\forall ka\in kA\enspace (k\alpha\lt\alpha'\le ka)$ 

- $\therefore$ for any $a\in A$
$$
\alpha=\frac{k\alpha}k\lt\frac{\alpha'}k\le\frac{ka}k=a\in A
$$
- $\therefore\alpha'/k$ is lower bound of $A$, contradicts that $\alpha$ is infimum $\quad\square$
</details>
</details>


<details class="example" open>
<summary>bounded function</summary>
for nonempty $A$ and bounded $f,g:A\to\mathbb R$

$$
\sup(f+g)[A]\le\sup f[A]+\sup g[A]
$$

<details class="list-none">
<summary>**Proof.**</summary>
let $\beta_f=\sup f[A]$ and $\beta_g=\sup g[A]$

$\therefore$ for any $x\in A$

$$
\begin{align*}
  &&f(x)&\le\beta_f\quad g(x)\le \beta_g\\
  \therefore&&f(x)+g(x)&\le\beta_f+\beta_g
\end{align*}
$$

- $\therefore\beta_f+\beta_g$ is upper bound of $(f+g)[A]\quad\square$
</details>
</details>

<details class="example" open>
<summary>infinite union of closed intervals is not closed</summary>

$$
\bigcup_{n=1}^\infty[1/n,1]=(0,1]
$$

<details class="list-none">
<summary>**Proof.**</summary>
let $U$ be the union


$(\implies)$ let any $x\in U$

- $\therefore\exists n\in\mathbb Z^+\quad x\in[1/n,1]$
- $\therefore 0\lt1/n\le x\le 1$, thus $x\in(0,1]$


$(\impliedby)$ let any $x\in(0,1]$

- $\because (1/n)\to0$ and $x\gt0$
- $\therefore\exists n$ st $1/n=|1/n-0|\lt x\le 1$
- $\therefore 1\in(1/n,1]\sube U\quad\square$
</details>
</details>

<details class="example" open>
<summary>find extrema</summary>
let $f(x)=x^2+x-1=(x-\alpha_A)(x-\alpha_B)$ where

$$
\alpha_A=\frac{\sqrt5-1}2\gt0\qquad\alpha_B=-\frac{\sqrt5+1}2\lt0
$$

let $A=\{x\in[0,\infty):x^2+x-1\gt0\}$

<details>
<summary>wts $\inf A=\alpha_A$ and $A$ has no supremum</summary>

for any $x\in\mathbb R$ (draw graph)

$$
\begin{align*}
  \because&&f(x)\lt0&\iff x\in(-\infty,\alpha_B)\cup(\alpha_A,\infty)\\
  \therefore&&x\in A&\iff x\in(\alpha_A,\infty)
\end{align*}
$$

$\therefore \inf A=\inf(\alpha_A,\infty)=\alpha_A$ and $A$ has no supremum
</details>

let $B=\{x\in\mathbb Q\cap(-\infty,0]:f(x)\le0\}$

<details>
<summary>wts $\inf B=\alpha_B$ and $\sup B=0$</summary>

for any $x\in\mathbb R$

$$
\begin{align*}
  \because&& f(x)\le0&\iff x\in[\alpha_B,\alpha_A]\\
  \therefore&&x\in B&\iff x\in\mathbb Q\cap[\alpha_B,0]
\end{align*}
$$

wts $\inf B=\alpha_B$

- let any $\varepsilon\gt0$
- $\therefore\exists q\in\mathbb Q$ st $\alpha_B\lt q\lt\min\{\alpha_B+\varepsilon,0\}$ (density)
- $\therefore q\in B$

- $\therefore\inf B=\alpha_B$

wts $\sup B=0$

- $\because 0\in B\lt 0$
- $\therefore \max B=0$, thus $0$ is supremum
</details>
</details>
