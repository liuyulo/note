## Sets and Dependent Types

<details class="definition" open>
<summary>almost containment</summary>
$A\sube^*B$ means $A\setminus B$ is finite

- i.e. $A$ has finitely many elements not in $B$

**Corollary.** $A$ is finite $\implies A\sube^*B$ for any $B$
</details>

<details class="definition" open>
<summary>almost disjoint</summary>
$A$ **almost disjoints** $B$ iff $A\cap B$ is finite
- i.e. the "disjoint" property holds for finitely many elements
</details>

<details class="definition" open>
<summary>union and intersection</summary>
let set $I$ be an index

let indexed family $\mathcal F=\{A_\alpha:\alpha\in I\}$

let intersection and union

$$
\begin{align*}
  \bigcup_{\alpha\in I}A_\alpha&=\{a:\exists \alpha\in I\enspace a\in A_\alpha\}\\
  \bigcap_{\alpha\in I}A_\alpha&=\{a:\forall\alpha\in I\enspace a\in A_\alpha\}
\end{align*}
$$

</details>

<details class="definition" open>
<summary>functions</summary>
let set $A,B$
$$
B^A=\{f\mid f:A\to B\}
$$

- $f:A\hookrightarrow B$ is **injective**
- $f:A\twoheadrightarrow B$ is **surjective**
- $f:A\stackrel\sim\to B$ is **bijective**
</details>

<details class="definition" open>
<summary>cartesian product (sequence)</summary>
for family $\mathcal F=\{A_\alpha\}_{\alpha\in I}$, let union $A=\bigcup_\alpha A_\alpha$

$$
\prod_{\alpha\in I} A_\alpha=\{f\in A^I\mid \forall\alpha\in I\enspace f(\alpha)\in A_\alpha\}
$$
</details>

## Cardinality

<details class="definition" open>
<summary>equinumerous</summary>
for set $A$ and $B$
<div class="flex justify-center">
$A\sim B\iff\exists f: A\stackrel\sim\to B$
</div>
</details>


<details class="definition" open>
<summary>aleph 0</summary>
let $\omega\sim \mathbb N$

<div class="flex justify-center">
$\aleph_0$ denotes the cardinality of $\omega$
</div>
</details>


<details class="definition" open>
<summary>relation on set</summary>
for set $A$

<div class="flex justify-center">
a **relation** on $A$ is $R\sube A\times A$
</div>
</details>

<details class="definition" open>
<summary>equivalence relation</summary>
relation $R$ is **equivalence relation** iff

- symmetric $aRb\implies bRa$
- transitive $aRb\implies bRc\implies aRc$
- reflexive $aRa$
</details>

<details class="definition" open>
<summary>partition</summary>
for set $A$ and powerset $\mathcal P(A)$, a **partition** $P\in\mathcal P(A)$ iff

$$
\bigsqcup_{A_\alpha\in P} A_\alpha=A
$$

</details>

<details class="theorem" open>
<summary>Schroder Berstein</summary>
for set $A$ and $B$

$$
\exists f:A\hookrightarrow B\text{ and }\exists g:B\hookrightarrow A\implies A\sim B
$$
</details>

<details class="definition" open>
<summary>cardinality</summary>
$$
|A|=|B|\iff A\sim B
$$
</details>

<details class="example" open>
<summary>russell's paradox</summary>
for any nonempty $A$

$$
|A|\lt|\mathcal P(A)|
$$

<details class="list-none">
<summary>**Proof.**</summary>

$\because a\mapsto\{a\}$ is injective 

$\therefore|A|\le|\mathcal P(A)|$

BWOC assume $\exists \varphi:A\stackrel\sim\to\mathcal P(A)$

- $\therefore\mathcal P(A)=\{\varphi(a)\}_{a\in A}$
- let $A'=\{a\in A:a\notin\varphi(a)\}$
- $\therefore\exists a'\in A$ st $\varphi(a')=A'$ as $\varphi$ surj
- ...
- $\therefore\bot$
</details>

<details class="example" open>
<summary>set difference equinumerous `=>` equinumerous</summary>
for any nonempty sets $A,B$

$$
(A-B)\sim(B-A)\implies |A|=|B|
$$

<details class="list-none">
<summary>**Proof.**</summary>
let $f:A-B\stackrel\sim\to B-A$

let $\varphi:A\to B$ be

- $\varphi(a)=a$ for any $a\in A\cap B$
- $\varphi(a)=f(a)$ otherwise

$\because A=(A-B)\sqcup(A\cap B)$, similar for $B$

$\therefore \varphi:A\stackrel\sim\to B$
</details>
</details>
