<details class="definition" open>
<summary>continuity</summary>

function $f:A\to B$ continuous iff

$$
\forall U\in\mathcal{T}_B\quad f^{-1}[U]\in\mathcal{T}_A
$$

on metric space, $f:A\to B$ continuous iff


$$
\forall x\in A\quad \forall \varepsilon\gt0\quad\exists\delta\gt0\quad f[B(x,\varepsilon)]\subseteq B(f(x),\varepsilon)
$$

<details class="list-none">
<summary>**Proof.** pullback iff ε-δ on metric spaces</summary>

$(\implies )$ let any $x\in A$, any $\varepsilon\gt0$

$\because f^{-1}[B(f(x),\varepsilon)]\in\mathcal{T}_A$ is neighbourhood of $x$

$\therefore\exists\delta\gt0\quad B(x,\delta)\subseteq f^{-1}[B(f(x),\varepsilon)]$

$\therefore f[B(x,\delta)]\subseteq B(f(x),\varepsilon)$ as wanted

$(\impliedby )$ let any $U\in\mathcal{T}_B$, wts $f^{-1}[U]\subset f^{-1}[U]^\circ$

let any $x\in f^{-1}[U]$

$\therefore f(x)\in U$

$\therefore\exists\varepsilon\gt0\quad B(f(x),\varepsilon)\subseteq U$

$\therefore\exists \delta\gt0\quad f[B(x,\varepsilon)]\subseteq B(f(x),\varepsilon)\subseteq U$

$\therefore x\in B(x,\delta)\subseteq f^{-1}[U]$, thus $x\in f^{-1}[U]^\circ$

$\therefore f^{-1}[U]\in\mathcal{T}_A\enspace\square$
</details>

</details>

<details class="theorem" open>
<summary>continuous map perserves compactness</summary>

for continuous $f:A\to B$

<div class="flex justify-center">

$K\subseteq A$ compact $\implies f[K]\subseteq B$ compact
</div>

<details class="list-none">
<summary>**Proof.**</summary>

let any open cover $\mathcal{C}$ of $f[K]$

let $\mathcal{D}=\{f^{-1}[U]:U\in\mathcal{C}\}$, thus is open cover of $K$ where<span class="float-right">($f$ cont)</span>

$$
\bigcup\mathcal{D}=\bigcup_{U\in\mathcal{C}}f^{-1}[U]=f^{-1}\left[\bigcup\mathcal{C}\right]\supseteq f^{-1}[f[K]]\supseteq K
$$

$\therefore\exists\mathcal{D}_0\in[\mathcal{D}]^{\lt\aleph_0}$ covers $K$<span class="float-right">($K$ compact)</span>

let $\mathcal{C}_0=\{f[U]:U\in\mathcal{D}_0\}\in[\mathcal{C}]^{\lt\aleph_0}$, thus is finite subcover of $f[K]$ where

$$
\bigcup\mathcal{C}_0=\bigcup_{U\in\mathcal{D}_0}f[U]=f\left[\bigcup\mathcal{D}_0\right]\supseteq f[K]
$$

$\therefore f[K]$ compact $\enspace\square$
</details>

</details>

<details class="theorem" open>
<summary>(metric space) map continuous iff map perserves limit</summary>

for metric space $A,B$

<div class="flex justify-center">

$f:A\to B$ continuous $\iff (a_n\to a\implies f(a_n)\to f(a))$ 
</div>

for any convergent sequence $(a_n)\subseteq A$

<details class="list-none">
<summary>**Proof.**</summary>

$(\implies )$ assume $f:A\to B$ continuous, let any $a_n\to a$ in $A$

let any $\varepsilon\gt0$

$\therefore\exists\delta\gt0\quad f[B(a,\delta)]\subseteq B(f(a),\varepsilon)$ <span class="float-right">($f$ cont)</span>

$\therefore\exists N\quad\forall n\gt N\quad a_n\in B(a,\delta)$<span class="float-right">($a_n\to a$)</span>

$\therefore\forall n\gt N\quad f(a_n)\in B(f(a),\varepsilon)$

$\therefore f(a_n)\to f(a)$ as wanted

$(\impliedby )$ assume $f:A\to B$ not continuous

$\therefore\exists a\in A\quad\exists\varepsilon\gt0$ such that

$$
\forall\delta\gt0\quad\exists x\in B(a,\delta)\quad f(x)\notin B(f(a),\varepsilon)
$$

$\therefore$ for any $n\in\mathbb{N}$, choose some $a_n\in B(a,1/n)$ such that $f(a_n)\notin B(f(a),\varepsilon)$

$\therefore a_n\to a$<span class="float-right">($|a_n-a|\lt1/n$ )</span>

$\because$ for any $N\in\mathbb{N}$

$$
f(a_N)\notin B(f(a),\varepsilon)
$$

$\therefore f(a_n)\not\to f(a)\enspace\square$
</details>

</details>

## Uniform Continuity

<details class="definition" open>
<summary>uniformly continuous</summary>

function $f:A\to\mathbb R$ **uniformly continuous** iff

$$
\forall\varepsilon\gt0\enspace\exists\delta\gt0\enspace\forall x,y\in D\enspace|x-y|\lt\delta\implies|f(x)-f(y)|\lt\varepsilon
$$
</details>

<details class="example" open>
<summary>$x\mapsto x^2$ is NOT uniformly continuous</summary>

<details class="list-none">
<summary>**Proof.**</summary>
let $\varepsilon=1\gt0$

let any $\delta\gt0$

let $x=1/\delta$ and $y=x+\delta/2$

- $\therefore|x-y|=\delta/2\lt\delta$
- but $|x^2-y^2|=|x-y||x+y|=\delta/2(2/\delta+\delta/2)=1+\delta^2/4\ge\varepsilon\quad\square$
</details>
</details>

<details class="theorem" open>
<summary>continuous function on closed interval is uniformly continuous</summary>
for any $f:D\to\mathbb R$ 
<div class="flex justify-center">
$f$ continuous on $[a,b]\implies f$ uniformly continuous on $[a,b]$
</div>

<details class="list-none">
<summary>**Proof.**</summary>
let $A=\{x\in[a,b]:f\text{ is unicont on }[a,x]\}$

- $\because f$ is continous at $a$ and $x\le b$ for any $x\in A$
- $\therefore a\in A$ and $b$ is upper bound of $A$
- $\therefore\exists\beta\in\mathbb R$ st $\beta=\sup A\le b$ (completeness)

wts $f$ unicont at $[a,\beta+\delta)$
- let any $\varepsilon\gt0$
  - $\because f$ continuous at $\beta$
  - $\therefore\exists\delta\gt0\enspace\forall x\in (\beta-\delta,\beta+\delta)$, we have $|f(x)-f(\beta)|\lt\varepsilon/2$

- let any $x,y\in(\beta-\delta,\beta+\delta)$
  $$
  \therefore|f(x)-f(y)|\le|f(x)-f(\beta)|+|f(\beta)+f(y)|\lt\varepsilon
  $$

- $\therefore f$ unicont at $(\beta-\delta,\beta+\delta)$
- $\because f$ unicont at $[a,\beta-\delta)$ since $\beta-\delta\lt\beta=\sup A$
- $\therefore f$ unicont at $[a,\beta+\delta)$

wts $\beta=b$, BWOC assume $\beta\lt b$

- $\therefore\exists q\in[a,b]$ st $\beta\lt q\lt\beta+\delta$ (density)
- $\therefore f$ unicont at $[a,q]\sube[a,\beta+\delta)$, thus $q\in A$, contradicts that $\beta$ is upper bound

$\therefore\beta=b$

$\therefore f$ unicont at $[a,b]\sube[a,\beta+\delta)\quad\square$
</details>
</details>

<details class="example" open>
<summary>extend uniform continuity</summary>
for any $f:D\to\mathbb R$  and $a\gt 0$

<div class="flex justify-center">
$f$ cont on $[0,\infty)$ and unicont on $[a,\infty)\implies f$ unicont on $[0,\infty)$
</div>

<details class="list-none">
<summary>**Proof.**</summary>

notice $f$ is unicont on $[0,a]$

let any $\varepsilon\gt0$

- $\therefore\exists\delta_1\gt0\quad\forall x,y\in[0,a]\quad|x-y|\lt\delta_1\implies|f(x)-f(y)|\lt\varepsilon/2$
- $\phantom\therefore\exists\delta_2\gt0\quad\forall x,y\in[a,\infty)\quad|x-y|\lt\delta_2\implies|f(x)-f(y)|\lt\varepsilon/2$

let $\delta=\min\{\delta_1,\delta_2\}\gt0$

let any $x,y\in[0,\infty)$

- case $x,y\in[0,a]$ or $x,y\in[a,\infty)$
  - $\therefore$ done, since $f$ unicont at $[0,a]$ and $[a,\infty)$

- case otherwise, WLOG assume $0\le x\lt a\lt y$

  - assume $|x-y|\lt\delta\le\delta_1,\delta_2$
  $$
  \begin{align*}
    \therefore&&|x-a|&=a-x\lt y-x=|x-y|\lt\delta_1\\
    &&|a-y|&=y-a\lt y-x=|x-y|\lt\delta_2\\
    \therefore&&|f(x)-f(y)|&\le|f(x)-f(a)|+|f(a)-f(y)|\lt\varepsilon/2+\varepsilon/2=\varepsilon\quad\square
  \end{align*}
  $$
</details>
</details>
