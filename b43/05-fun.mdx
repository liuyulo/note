## Pointwise Convergence

<details class="definition" open>
<summary>pointwise convergence</summary>

real valued sequence $(f_n:A\to \mathbb{R})$ **pointwise converges** to $f$ on $A$ iff

$$
\forall a\in A\quad\forall\varepsilon\gt0\quad\exists N\in\mathbb{N}\quad\forall n\ge N\quad |f_n(a)-f(a)|\lt\varepsilon
$$

</details>

<details class="example" open>
<summary>$f_n(x)$ bounded, but limit unbonded</summary>

on $(0,1)$

$$
\frac n{nx+1}\to \frac1x
$$

<details class="list-none">
<summary>**Proof.**</summary>

let any $x\in(0,1)$, any $\varepsilon\gt0$

$$
\because\left|\frac n{nx+1}-\frac 1x\right|=\frac1{x(nx+1)}\gt\frac1{nx^2}
$$

thus choose (large) $N$ such that $1/N\lt\varepsilon x^2$<span class="float-right">(arch)</span>

$\therefore$ for any $n\ge N$

$$
\left|\frac n{nx+1}-\frac 1x\right|\lt\frac 1{nx^2}\lt\frac 1{Nx^2}\lt\varepsilon\enspace\square
$$
</details>

1. notice $|n/(nx+1)|$ is bounded by $n$, but $1/x$ is unbounded
</details>

## Uniform Convergence

<details class="definition" open>
<summary>uniform convergence</summary>

$(f_n:A\to \mathbb{R})$ **uniformly** convergence to $f$ on $A$, denoted $f_n\rightrightarrows f$ iff

$$
\forall\varepsilon\gt0\quad\exists N\in\mathbb{N}\quad\forall n\ge N\quad\forall x\in A\quad |f_n(x)-f(x)|\lt\varepsilon
$$

</details>

<details class="theorem" open>
<summary>uconv perserves continuity</summary>

for $f_n$ continuous

<div class="flex justify-center">

$f_n\rightrightarrows f\implies f$ continuous
</div>

<details class="list-none">
<summary>**Proof.**</summary>

let any $x\in A$, any $\varepsilon\gt0$

$\therefore\exists N\quad\forall y\in A\quad |f_N(y)-f(y)|\lt\varepsilon/3$<span class="float-right">($f_n\rightrightarrows f$)</span>

$\therefore\exists\delta\gt0\quad f_N[B(x,\delta)]\subseteq B(f_N(x),\varepsilon/3)$<span class="float-right">($f_N$ continuous)</span>

let any $y\in B(x,\delta)$, thus

$$
\begin{align*}
  |f(x)-f(y)|&\le|f(x)-f_N(x)|+|f_N(x)-f_N(y)|+|f_N(y)-f(y)|\\
  &\lt\varepsilon/3+\varepsilon/3+\varepsilon/3=\varepsilon\enspace\square
\end{align*}
$$
</details>

</details>

<details class="theorem" open>
<summary>uconv perserves bounded</summary>

for $f_n$ bounded

<div class="flex justify-center">

$f_n\rightrightarrows f\implies f$ bounded and $f_n$ uniformly bounded
</div>

<details class="list-none">
<summary>**Proof.**</summary>

for any $n$, let $M_n$ be bound of $f_n$

$\therefore\exists N\forall x$

$$
|f(x)|\lt|f_N(x)|+1\lt M_n+1
$$

$\therefore f$ bounded by $M_n+1$

$\because$ for any $n\ge N$, any $x$

$$
|f_n(x)|\lt|f(x)|+1\lt M_n+2
$$

$\therefore$ let $M=\max\{M_1,\cdots, M_N,M_N+2\}$, we have for any $n$

$$
\forall x|f_n(x)|\le M\enspace\square
$$
</details>
</details>

<details class="theorem" open>
<summary>uniform convergence iff cauchy</summary>

for $(f_n:A\to \mathbb{R})$, $f_n\rightrightarrows f$ iff

$$
\forall\varepsilon\gt0\quad\exists N\gt0\quad\forall n,m\ge N\quad\forall x\in A\quad|f_n(x)-f_m(x)|\lt\varepsilon
$$

<details class="list-none">
<summary>**Proof.**</summary>

$(\implies )$ assume $f_n\rightrightarrows f$

let any $\varepsilon\gt0$

$\therefore\exists N\quad\forall n\ge N\quad\forall x\quad|f_n(x)-f(x)|\lt\varepsilon/2$ 

$\therefore$ for any $n,m\ge N$

$$
|f_n(x)-f_m(x)|\le|f_n(x)-f(x)|+|f(x)-f_m(x)|\lt\varepsilon/2+\varepsilon/2=\varepsilon
$$

$(\impliedby )$ assume cauchy

let any $\varepsilon\gt0$

$\therefore\exists N$ st for any $n\ge N$, any $x\in A$

$$
\begin{align*}
  &&\forall k\ge n\quad|f_n(x)-f_k(x)|\lt\varepsilon\\
\therefore&&|f_n(x)-f(x)|=\lim_{k\to\infty}|f_n(x)-f_k(x)|\lt\varepsilon
\end{align*}
$$

- where $f_k(x)\mapsto|f_n(x)-f_k(x)|$ is perserves limit

$\therefore f_n\rightrightarrows f\enspace\square$
</details>
</details>

## Series of Functions

<details class="definition" open>
<summary>pointwise/uniform convergence</summary>

for $(f_n:A\to \mathbb{R})$

<div class="flex justify-center">

$\sum f_n$ converges p/u $\iff (s_k:A\to \mathbb{R})$ converges p/u where
</div>

$$
s_k(x)=\sum_{n=i}^kf_n(x)
$$

</details>

<details class="theorem" open>
<summary>Weierstrass test</summary>

for $(f_n:A\to \mathbb{R})$ where $f_n$ is bounded by $M_n\in\mathbb{R}^+$

<div class="flex justify-center">

$\sum M_n$ converges $\implies \sum f_n$ uniform converges
</div>

<details class="list-none">
<summary>**Proof.**</summary>

let $m_k$ be $k$th partial sum of $(M_n)$

$\therefore\exists N\quad\forall i\gt j\gt N\quad m_i-m_j=|m_i-m_j|\lt\varepsilon$<span class="float-right">(cauchy)</span>

let any $i\gt j\ge N$, any $x\in A$

$$
\begin{align*}
  \therefore&&|s_i(x)-s_j(x)|&=\left|\sum_{k=j+1}^if_n(x)\right|\le\sum_{k=j+1}^i|f_n(x)|\\
  &&&\le\sum_{k=j+1}^iM_k=m_i-m_j\lt\varepsilon
\end{align*}
$$

$\therefore (s_k)$ cauchy, thus $\sum f_n$ uconv $\enspace\square$
</details>

</details>

<details class="example" open>
<summary>power series</summary>

let $f_n(x)=x^n/n!$



<details class="list-none">
<summary>**Proof.** $\sum f_n$ pconv on $\mathbb{R}$</summary>

let any $x\in\mathbb{R}$

by root test

- let any $a_n=x^n/n!$

$$
\therefore\lim_{n\to\infty}\left|\frac{a_n}{a_{n-1}}\right|=\lim_{n\to\infty}\frac xn=0
$$

- $\therefore\sum a_n$ abs conv, thus conv $\enspace\square$
</details>

<details class="list-none">
<summary>**Proof.** $\sum f_n$ <s>uconv</s> on $\mathbb{R}$</summary>

wts not cauchy

let any $N\in\mathbb{N}$

$\therefore |s_N(N)-s_{N+1}(N)|= N^{N+1}/(N+1)!\ge 1$, thus not cauchy

$\therefore$ not uconv
</details>

<details class="list-none">
<summary>**Proof.** $\sum f_n$ uconv on any compact $A\subseteq\mathbb{R}$</summary>

$\because A$ bounded by some $M\gt 0$<span class="float-right">(HB)</span>

$\because f_n$ is odd/even in increases on $[0,M]$

$\therefore f_n$ is bounded by $f_n(M)$ on $A$

$\because f_n(M)$ converges<span class="float-right">($\sum f_n$ pconv)</span>

$\therefore (f_n)$ uconv<span class="float-right">(W)</span>
</details>
</details>

<details class="theorem" open>
<summary>uconv perserves continuity</summary>

for continuous $f_n:A\to \mathbb{R}$

<div class="flex justify-center">

$\sum f_n\rightrightarrows s\implies s:A\to \mathbb{R}$ continuous on $A$
</div>

<details class="list-none">
<summary>**Proof.**</summary>

$\because \sum f_n\rightrightarrows s$

$\therefore s_n\rightrightarrows s$ where $s_n=f_1+\cdots+f_n$ is finite sum of continuous function, thus continuous

$\therefore s$ continuous $\enspace\square$
</details>

</details>
