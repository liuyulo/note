## Topological Spaces

<details class="definition" open>
<summary>metrics</summary>

Euclidean metric on $\mathbb{R}^n$

$$
d(x,y)=\sqrt{\sum_{i=1}^n(x_i-y_i)^2}
$$

Square metric on $\mathbb{R}^n$

$$
d(x,y)=\max_{1\le i\le n}|x_i-y_i|
$$

Discrete metric on $X$


$$
d(x,y)=\begin{cases}
  1&x\ne y\\
  0&x=y
\end{cases}
$$

</details>

<details class="definition" open>
<summary>Eunclidean topology</summary>

space $(\mathbb{R},\mathcal{T}_E)$ is generated by

$$
\{B(x,\varepsilon):x\in \mathbb{R}\enspace \varepsilon\gt0\}
$$

</details>

<details class="definition" open>
<summary>ray topology</summary>

space $(\mathbb{R},\mathcal{T}_R)$ is generated by

$$
\{(a,\infty):a\in\mathbb{R}\}
$$

</details>

## Pointful is Painful

<details class="definition" open>
<summary>in $\mathbb R$</summary>
 
- $A\sube\mathbb R$ is **open** $\iff\partial A\sube A^c$
- $A\sube\mathbb R$ is **closed** $\iff\partial A\sube A$
- **neighborhood** of $a\in A$ is $N(a,\varepsilon)=(a-\varepsilon,a+\varepsilon)$ for some $\varepsilon\gt0$
- **deleted** neighborhood of $a\in A$ is $N^*(a,\varepsilon)=N(a,\varepsilon)-\{a\}$
- **closure** of $A\sube\mathbb R$ is $\overline A=A\cup A'$
- $a\in A$ is **isolated** $\iff$ has neighborhood only intersects $A$ at $a$
- **interior**, **boundary**, **derived** are same
</details>


<details class="theorem" open>
<summary>unions of open intervals</summary>

$$
\mathbb R=\bigcup_{n\in\mathbb N}(-n,n)
$$
</details>

<details class="definition" open>
<summary>dense set</summary>

<div class="flex justify-center">

$A\subseteq X$ **dense** $\iff\overline A=X$
</div>

</details>

<details class="example" open>
<summary>let any $A=\{1,2,\cdots,n\}\sube\mathbb N\sube\mathbb R$</summary>

<details class="list-none" open>
<summary>**Proof.** $\partial A=A$</summary>

</details>

<details class="list-none">
<summary>**Proof.** $A^\circ=\varnothing$</summary>
$\because A$ is closed

$\therefore\bar A=A$

$\because\partial A=A$

$\therefore A=\bar A=A^\circ\sqcup\partial A=A^\circ\sqcup A$

$\therefore A^\circ=\varnothing$
</details>

</details>

<details class="example" open>
<summary>closed iff contains limit points ($\mathbb R$)</summary>
for $A\sube X$

<div class="flex justify-center">
$A$ closed $\iff A'\sube A$
</div>

<details class="list-none">
<summary>**Proof.**</summary>
$(\implies)$ assume $A'\not\sube A$

- $\therefore\exists x\in A'-A$
- let any $\varepsilon\gt 0$
- $\therefore N^*(x,\varepsilon)$ intersects $A$ (say at $a\in A$)
- $\therefore  N(x,\varepsilon)$ intersects $A$ (at $a$) and intersects $A^c$ (at $x$)
- $\therefore x\in\partial A$ but $x\notin A$
- $\therefore A$ is not closed

$(\impliedby)$ assume $A$ not closed

- $\therefore\exists x\in\partial A-A$
- let any $\varepsilon\gt0$ 
- $\therefore N(x,\varepsilon)$ intersects $A$ and $A^c$
- $\therefore N^*(x,\varepsilon)$ intersects $A$
- $\therefore x\in A'$ but $x\notin A$
- $\therefore A'\not\sube A\quad\square$
</details>
</details>

<details class="example" open>
<summary>any limit point can be converged by some sequence without it</summary>

for any $A\sube\mathbb R$ and any $\alpha\in A'$

<div class="flex justify-center">

$\exists (a_n\in A)_{n=1}^\infty$ st $a_n\to\alpha$ and $a_n\ne\alpha$
</div>

<details class="list-none">
<summary>**Proof.**</summary>
let any $A\sube\mathbb R$ and any $\alpha\in A'$

- $\therefore$ for any $n\in\mathbb N$, the deleted neighborhood $N^*(\alpha,1/n)$ intersects $A$

- $\therefore\forall n\in\mathbb N\enspace\exists a_n\in N^*(\alpha,1/n)\cap A$, notice $a_n\ne\alpha$
- $\therefore a_n\to\alpha$ as wanted
</details>
</details>
