<details class="theorem" open>
<summary>(metric) continuous on compact is ucont</summary>

for any continuous $f:A\to B$ on metric spaces

<div class="flex justify-center">

$A$ compact $\implies f$ uniform continuous
</div>


<details class="list-none">
<summary>**Proof.**</summary>

let any $\varepsilon\gt 0$

$\therefore\forall x\in A\quad\exists \delta_x\gt0\quad f[B(x,\delta_x)]\subseteq B(f(x),\varepsilon)$

let $\mathcal{C}=\{B(x,\delta_x/2)\}_{x\in A}$ be open cover of $A$

$\therefore\exists\mathcal{C}_0=\{B(x_i,\delta_i/2)\}_{i=1}^n\subseteq\mathcal{C}$ covers $A$<span class="float-right">($A$ compact)</span>

let $\delta=\min_i\{\delta_i/2\}\gt0$

thus for any $a,b\in A$ where $d(a,b)\lt\delta$

- $\therefore\exists i\quad a\in B(x_i,\delta_i/2)\subseteq B(x_i,\delta_i)$ and <span class="float-right">($C_0$ covers $A$)</span>

$$
\begin{align*}
  d(b,x_i)&\le d(b,a)+d(a,x_i)\lt\delta+\delta_i/2\lt\delta_i
\end{align*}
$$

- $\therefore a,b\in B(x_i,\delta_i/2)$, thus $f(a),f(b)\in B(f(x_i),\varepsilon/2)$

- $\therefore d(f(a),f(b))\lt\varepsilon/2+\varepsilon/2=\varepsilon\enspace\square$
</details>

</details>

for $f_n\rightrightarrows f$

<div class="flex justify-center">

</div>

<details class="list-none">

$\therefore\exists N\gt 0\quad |f_N-f|\lt 1$, thus $|f_N|\lt 1+|f|\le 1+M$

<details class="example" open>
<summary>compactly converge</summary>

$f:\mathbb{R}\to \mathbb{R}$ **comapactly converge** iff $f_n\rightrightarrows f$ on every compact subset

<details class="list-none">
<summary>**Proof.** $x/n$ cconv, <s>uconv</s> on $\mathbb{R}$</summary>

wts <s>uconv</s>

$$
\forall N\quad\frac{N+1}N\gt1
$$

wts cconv, let any compact $K\subseteq R$

$\because |x|$ continuous on $K$

$\therefore$ image of $|x|$ on $K$ is compact, thus bounded by some $M$

thus

$$
\frac xn\le\frac{|x|}n\le\frac Mn
$$

</details>

</details>

<details class="list-none">
<summary>**Proof.** $1/(x^{2n}+1)$ conv, <s>uconv</s> on $\mathbb{R}$</summary>

on $|x|\lt 1\quad x^{2n}\to 0$, thus $f_n(x)\to 1$

on $|x|=1\quad x^{2n}\to 1$, thus $f_n(x)\to 1/2$

<details class="list-none">
<summary>**Proof.** $x/(1+nx^2)$ uconv on $\mathbb{R}$</summary>

let $s=\sqrt nx$, thus

$$
\left|\frac x{1+nx^2}\right|=\left|\frac{\sqrt nx}{\sqrt n+\sqrt n(\sqrt nx)^2}\right|=\frac 1{\sqrt n}\left|\frac s{1+s}\right|\lt\frac 1{\sqrt n}
$$

</details>

<details class="list-none">
<summary>**Proof.** $\sum x^{2n}/(n+x)^2$ uconv on $[0,1]$</summary>

$$
\because\frac{x^{2n}}{(n+x)^2}\le\frac 1{(n+x)^2}\le\frac 1{n^2}
$$

- where $\sum 1/n^2$ conv

$\therefore\sum f_n$ uconv $\enspace\square$<span class="float-right">(WM)</span>

</details>

<details class="list-none">
<summary>**Proof.** $\sum (1/n)\sqrt{|\sin nx|/n}$ uconv on $\mathbb{R}$</summary>


$$
\because\frac 1n\sqrt{\frac{|\sin nx|}n}\le\frac 1n\sqrt{\frac 1n}=\frac 1{n^{3/2}}
$$

- where $\sum\frac 1{n^{3/2}}$ conv

$\therefore\sum f_n$ uconv $\enspace\square$<span class="float-right">(WM)</span>


</details>

<details class="list-none">
<summary>**Proof.** $\sum 1/(nx)^2$ pconv, <s>uconv</s> on $\mathbb{R}$</summary>

wts pconv, let any $\varepsilon\gt0$, any $x\in\mathbb{R}$

$$
\therefore\sum \frac 1{(nx)^2}=\frac 1{x^2}\sum \frac1{n^2}\lt\infty
$$

wts <s>uconv</s>, let any $N\in\mathbb{N}$

$$
\left|S_N\left(\frac 1{N+1}\right)-S_{N+1}\left(\frac 1{N+1}\right)\right|=\frac1{(N+1)^2(N+1)^{-2}}=1
$$



</details>

<details class="list-none">
<summary>**Proof.** $\sum 1/(nx)^2$ uconv on $[1,\infty)$</summary>

$\because\forall n\quad 1/(nx)^2\gt0$ and decreases on $[1,\infty)$

$\therefore\forall n\quad 1/(nx^2)\le 1/n^2$ on $[1,\infty)$ where $\sum 1/n^2\lt\infty$

$\therefore\sum f_n$ uconv $\enspace\square$<span class="float-right">(WH)</span>

</details>

<details class="list-none">
<summary>**Proof.** $\sum n^{-x}$ uconv on $(\sqrt 3,\infty)$</summary>

$\because\forall n\quad n^{-x}\gt 0$ decreases

$\therefore\forall n\gt 2\quad n^{-x}\le n^{-n}\le n^{-2}$ where $\sum 1/n^2\lt\infty$

$\therefore \sum_{n=2}^\infty f_n$ uconv, thus $\sum f_n$ uconv $\enspace\square$<span class="float-right">(WH)</span>


</details>

<details class="list-none">
<summary>**Proof.** $\sum x^2/n^2$ uconv on $[0,\sqrt 3]$</summary>

$\because\forall n\quad x^2/n^2$ increases 

$\therefore\forall n\quad x^2/n^2\le \sqrt{3}^2/n^2=3/n^2$ where $\sum 3/n^2\lt\infty$

$\therefore\sum f_n$ uconv $\enspace\square$<span class="float-right">(WH)</span>


</details>
