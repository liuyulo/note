Orthogonal Sets

\{v_i\} is othogonal iff all:

\forall i\enspace v_i\ne 0\quad\land\quad\forall i\ne j\enspace v_i\cdot v_j=0

is onthogonal iff all:

\textsf{is orthogonal}\quad\land\quad\forall i\enspace ||v_i||=1
Pythagorean Theorem ||v_1+\cdots+v_n||^2=||v_1||^2+\cdots+||v_n||^2
prove PyTheorem given \forall i\ne j\enspace v_i\cdot v_j=0 \begin{align*} ||v_i+\cdots+v_n||^2&=(v_1+\cdots+v_n)\cdot(v_1+\cdots+v_n)\\ &=(v_1\cdot v_1+\cdots+v_n\cdot v_n)+\sum_{i\ne j}v_i\cdot v_j\\ &=||v_1||^2+\cdots+||v_n||^2+0\quad\blacksquare\\ \end{align*}
Prove orthogonal sets are always indep let \{v_i\} be orthogonal and suppose a_1v_1+\cdots+a_nv_n=0 \begin{align*} \textsf{ suppose }&a_1v_1+\cdots+a_nv_n=0\\ \because&\,||x||=0\iff x=0\\ \therefore&\,||a_1v_1+\cdots+a_nv_n||=0\\ \because&\,\left|\left|\sum a_iv_i\right|\right|^2=\sum ||a_iv_i||^2\tag{\sf Pythagorean}\\ \therefore&\,||a_1v_1||^2+\cdots+||a_nv_n||^2=0\\ \because&\,||av||^2=(|a|||v||)^2\tag{\sf norm prop}\\ &=|a|^2||v||^2\tag{\sf exp law}\\ \therefore&\,|a_1|^2||v_1||^2+\cdots+|a_n|^2||v_n||^2=0\\ \because&\,||v_i||\ne 0\tag{\sf orthogonal}\\ \therefore&\, |a_1|^2=\cdots=|a_n|^2=0\\ \therefore&\,\forall i\enspace a_i=0\\ \therefore&\,\textsf{ linearly indep} \end{align*}

Diagonisation

Characteristic Polynomial of A\in\mathcal L(V^n)

c_A(\lambda)=|\lambda I-A|

by Fundamental Theorem of Algebra, |\lambda I-A| always has n non-distinct roots
Diagonisation for A\in\mathcal L(V^n)
  1. find each root of c_A(\lambda)=|\lambda I-A| as eigenval \lambda_i
  2. for each \lambda_i, find solution of (\lambda_i I-A)x=0 as eigenvec x
  3. if \{x_i\} is a basis of V^n, then
    • P=\begin{bmatrix} x_1&x_2&\cdots&x_n\\ \end{bmatrix} is invertible
    • PAP^{-1}=D=\mathrm{diag}(\lambda_i)
Distict Eigenvals \implies Indep Eigenvecs for T\in\mathcal L(V^n) \begin{align*} &\{\lambda_i\}_{i=1}^m\textsf{ are \textbf{distinct} eigenvals of }A\\ \implies&\{v_i\}_{i=1}^m\textsf{ as corresponding eigenvecs are \textbf{indep}} \end{align*}
prove above (by contradiction) given T\in\mathcal L(V^n) with \lambda_1,\cdots,\lambda_m distinct eigenvals \begin{align*} &\textsf{suppose }v_1,\cdots,v_m\textsf{ is dep}\\ \therefore&\,\exist k\textsf{ st }v_k\in\mathrm{sp}\{v_i,\cdots,v_{k-1}\}\quad\{v_i\}_{i=1}^{k-1}\textsf{ is indep}\\ \therefore&\,\exist a_i\in F\textsf{ st }v_k=a_1v_1+\cdots+a_{k-1}v_{k-1}\tag{\sf def of span (1)}\\ \therefore&\,\lambda_kv_k=a_1\lambda_1v_1+\cdots+a_{k-1}\lambda_{k-1}v_{k-1}\tag{\sf apply $T$ (2)}\\ &\,\lambda_kv_k=a_1\lambda_kv_k+\cdots+a_{k-1}\lambda_kv_{k-1}\tag{\sf apply $\lambda_k$ (3)}\\ \therefore&\,\textsf{(3)}-\textsf{(2) gives }0=a_1(\lambda_k-\lambda_1)v_1+\cdots+a_{k-1}(\lambda_k-\lambda_{k-1})v_{k-1}\\ \because&\,v_1,\cdots,v_{k-1}\textsf{ is indep}\quad\lambda_i\ne\lambda_j\enspace\forall i\ne j\tag{\sf given distinct}\\ \therefore&\,a_1=\cdots=a_{k-1}=0\\ \therefore&\,v_k=0\tag{\sf from (1)}\\ \therefore&\,\textsf{contradicts that }v_k\ne0\tag{\sf $v_k$ is eigenvec}\\ \therefore&\,v_1,\cdots,v_m\textsf{ is indep}\quad\blacksquare \end{align*}

Vector Space

Subspaces

U is a subspace of V iff all:
  • U\subseteq V
  • U is vecspc
Check if U\subseteq V is subspc
  1. 0\in U (has additive identity)
  2. u,w\in U\implies u+w\in U (closure add)
  3. \lambda\in F,u\in U\implies \lambda u\in U (closure scalar mul)
  • \{0\} is the smallest subspc of V
  • V is the largest subspc of V

Sum

for U_i\subseteq V \sum U_i=\left\{\sum u_i:u_i\in U_i\right\}
  • U,W\subseteq V\implies U\cap W\subseteq V
  • U\cup W is rarely a subspc
given U,W\subseteq V show U\cup W\subseteq V\iff U\subseteq W\lor W\subseteq U
  • wts U\cup W\subseteq V\implies U\subseteq W or W\subseteq U
    • assume U\cup W\subseteq V and U\not\subseteq W\land W\not\subseteq U \begin{align*} &\therefore\exists u\in U,w\in W\textsf{ st }u\not\in W,w\not\in U\\ &\because u,w\in U\cup W\subseteq V\\ &\therefore u+w\in U\cup W\\ &\therefore\textsf{WLOG assume }u+w\in U\subseteq V\\ &\therefore (u+w)-u=w\in U\\ &\therefore\textsf{ contradiction }\\ &\therefore U\subseteq W\lor W\subseteq U\quad\blacksquare \end{align*}
  • wts U\subseteq W\lor W\subseteq U\implies U\cup W\subseteq V
    • assume U\subseteq W\lor W\subseteq U \begin{align*} &\therefore\textsf{WLOG assume }U\subseteq W\\ &\therefore U\cup W=W\subseteq V\quad\blacksquare \end{align*}

Direct Sum

\displaystyle\sum U_i=\bigoplus U_i iff any:
  • for \displaystyle u_i\in U_i\enspace\sum u_i=0\implies u_i=0
  • \displaystyle\bigcap U_i=\{0\}
  • \displaystyle\sum\dim U_i=\dim \sum U_i (proof here)
show U+W=U\oplus W\iff U\cap W=\{0\}

  • wts U+W=U\oplus W\implies U\cap W=\{0\} \begin{align*} \textsf{let any}&\,v\in U\cap W\\ \therefore&\,v+(-v)=0\quad v\in U,-v\in W\\ \because&\,\textsf{repesentation of the sum must be unique}\\ \therefore&\,v=0\\ \therefore&\,U\cap W=\{0\}\\ \end{align*}

  • wts U\cap W=\{0\}\implies U+W=U\oplus W \begin{align*} \textsf{given }&U\cap W=\{0\}\\ \textsf{suppose }&u+w=0\quad u\in U,w\in W\\ \therefore&\,u=-w\in W\\ \therefore&\,u\in U\cap W\\ \therefore&\,u=0\quad w=-u=0\\ \because&\,U+W=U\oplus W\iff u+w=0\implies u=w=0\\ \therefore&\,U+W=U\oplus W\quad\blacksquare\\ \end{align*}

disprove U_1\oplus W=U_2\oplus W\implies U_1=U_2 \begin{align*} \textsf{let }&W=\{(x,x)\in\R^2\}\\ &U_1=\{(0,x)\in\R^2\}\quad U_2=\{(x,0)\in\R^2\}\\ &\therefore U_1+W=W=U_2+W\\ &\textsf{but }U_1\ne U_2 \end{align*}
show \R^\R=U_e\oplus U_o
  • wts \R^\R=U_e+U_o
    • let any f\in\R^\R, choose f_e(x)=\frac{f(x)+f(-x)}2\quad f_o(x)=\frac{f(x)-f(-x)}2 \begin{align*} &\because f_e(-x)=\frac{f(-x)+f(x)}2=\frac{f(x)+f(-x)}2=f_e(x)\\ &\because f_o(-x)=\frac{f(-x)-f(x)}2=-\frac{f(x)-f(-x)}2=-f_o(x)\\ &\therefore f_e\in U_e\enspace f_o\in U_o\\ &\because f_e(x)+f_o(x)=\frac{f(x)+f(-x)}2+\frac{f(x)-f(-x)}2=f(x)\\ &\therefore\forall f\in\R^\R\enspace\exists f_e\in U_e\enspace f_o\in U_o\textsf{ st }f=f_e+f_o\\ &\therefore \R^\R=U_e+U_o\\ \end{align*}
  • wts \R^\R=U_e\oplus U_o
    • let any f\in U_e\cap U_o \begin{align*} &\because f\in U_e\\ &\therefore f(x)=f(-x)\\ &\because f\in U_o\\ &\therefore f(x)=-f(-x)\\ &\therefore 2f(x)=f(-x)-f(-x)=0\implies f(x)=0\enspace\forall x\in\R\\ &\therefore f=0\forall f\in U_e\cap U_o\\ &\therefore U_e\cap U_o=\{0\}\\ &\therefore \R^\R=U_e+U_o=U_e\oplus U_o\quad\blacksquare\\ \end{align*}

Linear Maps

Vector Space of Linear Maps

Map T:V\to W is linear iff all:
  • \forall u,v\in V\quad T(u+v)=Tu+Tv\implies T(0)=0
  • \forall\lambda\in F,v\in V\quad T(\lambda v)=\lambda(Tv)
Add and Scalar-Mul on \mathcal L(V,W)

for S,T\in\mathcal L(V,W) (S+T)v=Sv+Tv\quad(\lambda T)v=\lambda(Tv)

  • \forall v\in V
  • commutativity, associativity, distributivity
Product of Linear Maps

for T\in\mathcal L(U,V) and S\in\mathcal L(V,W) ST\in\mathcal L(U,W)\quad(ST)u=S(Tu)

  • \forall u\in U
  • associativity, distributivity
Map basis if \{v_i\} is a basis of V^n and \{w_i\} is a basis of W^n, then \exist!T\in\mathcal L(V,W)\textsf{ st }Tv_i=w_i

Null and Image Spaces

Null Space & Injectivity

null space for T\in\mathcal L(V,W) \mathrm{null}\,T=\{v\in V:Tv=0\}\subseteq V
T\in\mathcal L(V,W) is injective iff any:
  • Tu=Tv\implies u=v
  • \mathrm{null}\,T=\{0\}

Image Space & Surjectivity

image space for T\in\mathcal L(V,W) \mathrm{im}\,T=\{Tv:v\in V\}\subseteq W
T\in\mathcal L(V,W) is surjective iff any:
  • \mathrm{im}\,T=W
  • \forall w\in W\enspace\exist v\in V\textsf{ st }Tv=w

Finite Dimensional

Fundamental Theorem of Linear Maps

for T\in\mathcal L(V^n,W^m) \begin{align*} \dim V&=\dim \mathrm{null}\,T+\dim \mathrm{im}\,T\\ n&=\dim \mathrm{null}\,T+\mathrm{rank}\,T\\ \end{align*}

\therefore T:V^n\to W^m is

  • injective iff any
    • \dim \mathrm{null}\,T=0
    • \mathrm{rank}\,T=n (aka full rank)
  • surjective iff any
    • \mathrm{rank}\,T=m
    • \mathrm{im}\,T=W^n
Prove Fundamental Theorem of Linear Maps for T\in\mathcal L(V,W)
  • wts \dim V=\dim\mathrm{null}\,T+n \begin{align*} \textsf{let }&\{u_i\}^m_{i=1}\textsf{ be a basis of }\mathrm{null}\,T\\ \therefore&\,\dim\mathrm{null}\,T=m\\ \because&\,\forall i\enspace u_i\in\mathrm{null}\,T\textsf{ is a basis}\\ \therefore&\,\forall i\enspace u_i\in V\textsf{ is indep}\\ \therefore&\,\{u_1,\cdots,u_m\}\textsf{ can be extended to }\\ &\,\{u_i,\cdots,u_m,v_1,\cdots,v_n\}\textsf{ as a basis of }V\\ \therefore&\,\dim V=m+n=\dim\mathrm{null}\,T+n\\ \end{align*}
  • wts n=\dim\mathrm{im}\,T
    • wts \{Tv_i\}^n_{i=1} is a basis of \mathrm{im}\,T
      • wts \mathrm{im}\,T=\mathrm{sp}\{Tv_i\}
      \begin{align*} \textsf{let any }&v\in V\\ \because&\,v=\mathrm{span}\{u_1,\cdots,u_m,v_i,\cdots,v_n\}\\ \therefore&\,v=\sum_{i=1}^m a_iu_i+\sum_{i=1}^nb_iv_i\quad a_i,b_i\in F\\ \therefore&\,Tv=\sum_{i=1}^m a_iTu_i+\sum_{i=1}^nb_iTv_i\tag{\sf $T$ is linear}\\ \because&\,u_i\in\mathrm{null}\,T\\ \therefore&\,Tv=0+\sum_{i=1}^nb_iTv_i=\sum_{i=1}^nb_iTv_i\\ \therefore&\,\forall Tv\in\mathrm{im}\,T\enspace\exist b_i\in F\textsf{ st }Tv=\sum_{i=1}^nb_iTv_i\\ \therefore&\,\mathrm{im}\,T=\mathrm{sp}\{Tv_i\} \end{align*}
      • wts \{Tv_i\} is indep
      \begin{align*} \textsf{suppose }&\,c_1Tv_1+\cdots+c_nTv_n=0\quad c_i\in F\\ \therefore&\,T(c_1v_1+\cdots+c_nv_n)=0\tag{\sf $T$ is linear}\\ \therefore&\,\sum_{i=1}^nc_iv_i\in\mathrm{null}\,T\\ \because&\,\mathrm{null}\,T=\mathrm{sp}\{u_i\}_{i=1}^m\\ \therefore&\,\sum_{i=1}^nc_iv_i=\sum_{i=1}^md_iu_i\quad d_i\in F\\ \therefore&\,\sum_{i=1}^nc_iv_i-\sum_{i=1}^md_iu_i=0\\ \because&\,\{u_1,\cdots,u_m,v_1,\cdots,v_n\}\textsf{ is indep}\\ \therefore&\,c_1=\cdots=c_n=0\\ \therefore&\,\{Tv_i\}^n_{i=1}\textsf{ is indep} \end{align*}
      • \therefore\{Tv_i\}^n_{i=1} is a basis of \mathrm{im}\,T
    • \therefore\dim\mathrm{im}\,T=n
  • \therefore\dim V=m+n=\dim\mathrm{null}\,T+\dim\mathrm{im}\,T\quad\blacksquare

Product Space

Cross Product \prod V_i=\{(v_1,\cdots,v_n):v_i\in V_i\}
Dimension of Product \dim \prod V_i=\sum\dim V_i

With Direct Sum

\displaystyle\Gamma:\prod U_i\to\sum U_i\quad (a finite dimension map)

\Gamma(u_1,\cdots,u_n)=u_1+\cdots+u_n

  • \Gamma is surjective by obviousness
\displaystyle\sum U_i=\bigoplus U_i\iff\Gamma is injective

Proof. assume \sum U_i=\bigoplus U_i

\begin{align*} &\therefore\sum u_i=0\iff u_i=0\\ &\therefore\Gamma(u)=0\iff u=0\\ &\therefore\mathrm{null}\,\Gamma=\{0\}\\ \end{align*}
\displaystyle\sum U_i=\bigoplus U_i\iff\dim \sum U_i=\sum\dim U_i Proof. \begin{align*} &\because\Gamma\textsf{ is \textbf{surjective}}\\ &\therefore\dim \mathrm{im}\,\Gamma=\dim \sum U_i\\ &\therefore\dim \prod U_i=\dim \mathrm{null}\,\Gamma+\dim \sum U_i\\ &\because\dim \prod U_i=\sum\dim U_i\\ &\therefore\Gamma\textsf{ is \textbf{injective}}\iff\sum\dim U_i=\dim \sum U_i\\ &\therefore\sum U_i=\bigoplus U_i\iff\sum\dim U_i=\dim \sum U_i \end{align*}

Quotient Space

\displaystyle v+U\textsf{ for }v\in V,U\subseteq V

v+U=\{v+u:u\in U\}

  • is a affine subset of V
  • is parallel to U
\displaystyle V/U\textsf{ for }U\subseteq V

V/U=\{v+U:v\in V\}

  • is set of all affine subsets parallel to U
Two affine subsets parallel to U are equal ⊻ disjoint

for U\subseteq V\enspace v,w\in V, equiv:

  • v-w\in U
  • v+U=w+U
  • (v+U)\cap(w+U)\ne\varnothing
Add and Scalar-Mul on V/U

for v,w\in V\enspace U\subseteq V\enspace\lambda\in F \begin{align*} (v+U)+(w+U)&=v+w+U\\ \lambda(v+U)&=\lambda v+U\\ \end{align*}

Quotient Map \pi:V\to V/U

\pi(v)=v+U

  • \mathrm{null}\,\pi=U
  • \mathrm{im}\,\pi=V/U (aka surjective)
Dimension of Quotient Space for finite-dimensional V and U\subseteq V \dim V/U=\dim V-\dim U
\tilde T:V/\mathrm{null}\,T\to W for T\in\mathcal L(V,W) \tilde T(v+\mathrm{null}\,T)=Tv
First Isomorphism Theorem for T\in\mathcal L(V,W) V/(\mathrm{null}\, T)\cong\mathrm{im}\,T
prove First Isomorphism Theorem
  • given T\in\mathcal L(V,W)
  • let K=\mathrm{null}\,T
  • define \tilde T:V/K\to\mathrm{im}\,T\textsf{ st }\tilde T(v+K)=Tv\enspace\forall v\in V

  1. wts \tilde T is well defined \begin{align*} &&\textsf{let any }&v,w\in V\textsf{ st }v+K=w+K\\ \implies&&v-w&\in\mathrm{null}\,T\\ \implies&&T(v-w)&=0\tag{\sf def of null}\\ \implies&&T(v)-T(w)&=0\tag{\sf $T$ is linear}\\ \implies&&T(v)&=T(w)\\ \implies&&\tilde T(v+K)&=\tilde T(w+K)\tag{\sf def of $\tilde T$} \end{align*}

  2. wts \tilde T is linear \begin{align*} \textsf{let any }&u,v\in V\quad\lambda\in F\\ \therefore&\,\tilde T((u+K)+\lambda(v+K))=\tilde T((u+\lambda v)+K)\\ &=T(u+\lambda v)=Tu+\lambda Tv\tag{\sf $T$ is linear}\\ &=\tilde T(u+K)+\lambda\tilde T(v+K)\tag{\sf by def}\\ \therefore&\,\tilde T\textsf{ is linear} \end{align*}

  3. wts \tilde T is isomorphism i.e. V/K\cong\mathrm{im}\,T

    • wts \tilde T is injective \begin{align*} \textsf{let any }&v\in V\textsf{ st }\tilde T(v+K)=0\qquad\\ \therefore&\,\tilde T(v+K)=Tv=0\tag{\sf def of $\tilde T$}\\ \therefore&\,v\in\mathrm{null}\,T=K\tag{\sf def of $\mathrm{null}\,T$}\\ \because&\,v-0=v\in K\\ \therefore&\,v+K=0+K=K\tag{\sf affine subset prop}\\ \therefore&\,\mathrm{null}\,\tilde T=\{K\}\tag{\sf null is trivial}\\ \therefore&\,\tilde T\textsf{ is \textbf{injective}} \end{align*}
    • wts \tilde T is surjective \begin{align*} \textsf{let any }&Tv\in\mathrm{im}\,T\\ \because&\,T\in\mathcal L(V,W)\\ \therefore&\,v\in V\\ \therefore&\,v+K\in V/K\\ \therefore&\,\tilde T(v+K)=Tv\in\mathrm{im}\,T\\ \therefore&\,\forall Tv\in\mathrm{im}\,T\enspace\exists v+K\in V/K\textsf{ st }\tilde T(v+K)=Tv\\ \therefore&\,\tilde T\textsf{ is \textbf{surjective}} \end{align*}
    • \therefore V/K\cong\mathrm{im}\,T\quad\blacksquare

Eigenvalues/vectors

Invariant Subspace

for T\in\mathcal L(V)\quad\lambda\in F is an eigenvalue iff \exist v\ne0\in V\textsf{ st }Tv=\lambda v
for T\in\mathcal L(V^n)\enspace\lambda\in F, equiv:
  • \lambda is an eigenval of T
  • T-\lambda I is not injective
  • T-\lambda I is not surjective
  • T-\lambda I is not invertible
  • |T-\lambda I|=0
for T\in\mathcal L(V)\enspace\lambda\in F is an eigenval of T
then v\ne0\in V is an \lambda-eigenvector of T iff Tv=\lambda v\iff(T-\lambda I)v=0\iff v\in\mathrm{null}(T-\lambda I)
