4 Sampling Distributions

Central Limit Theorem

CLT

for any iid (X_i) with same |\mu|<\infty and same \sigma^2<\infty

Z_n=\frac{\overline X_n-\mu}{\sigma/\sqrt n}\stackrel D\to N(0,1)

\therefore\overline X_n\stackrel{appr}\sim N(\mu,\sigma^2/n)

Large Number Laws

WLLN vs SLLN

Weak: for any independent (X_i) with common mean \mu and \sigma_i^2<\infty \overline X_n\stackrel P\rarr\mu

Strong: for any iid (X_i) with common mean \mu \overline X_n\stackrel{a.s}\to\mu

4.2 Convergence

Converges in Probability
(X_n) converges in probability iff
\displaystyle\forall\varepsilon>0\enspace\lim_{n\to\infty}P(|X_n-X|\ge\varepsilon)
X_n\stackrel P\rarr X
Theorem: for any continuous g X_n\stackrel P\rarr X\implies g(X_n)\stackrel P\rarr g(X)
Converges in Distribution

\forall x\enspace P(X=x)=0\implies\lim_{n\to\infty}P(X_n\le x)=P(X\le x)

i.e. X_n\stackrel D\rarr X
Converges Almost Surely

P(\lim_{n\to\infty} X_n=X)=1

i.e. X_n\stackrel{a.s}\rarr X
Implication of Convergences X_n\stackrel{a.s}\rarr X\implies X_n\stackrel P\rarr X\implies X_n\stackrel D\rarr X
Average (Sample Mean)

for any (X_i) with common mean \mu and common variance \sigma^2

\overline X_n=\frac1n\sum X_i

then E(\overline X_n)=\mu\quad V(\overline X_n)=\frac{\sigma^2}n

4.1 Definition

  • random sample: collection of iid RVs X_i
  • statistic: any fn h:\R^n\to\R where h(X_1,\cdots,X_n)
  • sampling distribution: distribution of the statistic

Markov’s Inequality (Markov’s Upper Bound) for any X\ge0 and a>0 P(X\ge a)\le\frac{E(X)}a

for any X with |\mu_X|<\infty and a>0

Jensen Inequality

for any convex g:\R\to\R (i.e. \forall x\enspace g''(x)>0)

3.3 Variance \sigma_X^2

Variance V(X)

V(X)=E((X-E(X))^2)=E(X^2)-\mu_X^2=\sigma_X^2 where \mu_X=E(X) and \sqrt{\sigma^2}=\sigma is Standard Deviation (SD)

  • V(aX+b)=a^2V(X)+V(b)=a^2V(X)
  • X\perp Y\implies V(X+Y)=V(X)+V(Y)
Covariance and Correlation

Covariance: \mathrm{Cov}(X,Y)=E((X-\mu_X)(Y-\mu_y))=E(XY)-E(X)E(Y)

  • \mathrm{Cov}(aX+bY,c+Z)=a\mathrm{Cov}(X,Z)+b\mathrm{Cov}(Y,Z)
  • \mathrm{Cov}(X,X)=V(X)
  • \displaystyle V\left(\sum X_i\right)=\sum V(X_i)+2\sum_{i<j}\mathrm{Cov}(X_i,X_j)
  • X\perp Y\implies\mathrm{Cov}(X,Y)=0

Correlation: \rho_{XY}=\dfrac{\mathrm{Cov}(X,Y)}{\sigma_X\sigma_Y}\in[-1,1]

2.8.3 Indep RV

X\perp Y iff

\begin{align*} P(a\le X\le b,c\le Y\le d)&=P(a\le X\le b)P(c\le Y\le d)\\ p_{XY}(x,y)&=p_X(x)p_Y(y)\quad f_{XY}(x,y)=f_X(x)f_Y(y)\\ p_{Y|X}(y|x)&=p_Y(y)\quad f_{Y|X}(y|x)=f_Y(y) \end{align*}

  • X\perp Y\implies\forall f,g:\R\to\R\enspace f(X)\perp g(Y)
  • p_{XY}(x,y)=g(x)h(y) or f_{XY}(x,y)=g(x)h(y)\implies X\perp Y
given X\sim\textsf{Exp}(\lambda_1)\perp Y\sim\textsf{Exp}(\lambda_2), find P(X<Y)

\begin{align*} P(X<Y)&=\int_0^\infty\int_0^yf_{XY}(x,y)\,dx\,dy=\int_0^\infty\int_0^yf_X(x)f_Y(y)\,dx\,dy\\ &=\int_0^\infty\int_0^y\lambda_1e^{-\lambda_1x}\underline{\lambda_2e^{-\lambda_2y}}\,dx\,dy\\ &=\int_0^\infty\underline{\lambda_2e^{-\lambda_2y}}\int_0^y\lambda_1e^{-\lambda_1x}\,dx\,dy\\ &=\int_0^\infty\lambda_2e^{-\lambda_2y}\left[-e^{-\lambda_1x}\right]_0^y\,dy=\int_0^\infty\lambda_2e^{-\lambda_2y}(1-e^{-\lambda_1y})\,dy\\ &=\int_0^\infty\left(\lambda_2e^{-\lambda_2y}-\lambda_2e^{-(\lambda_1+\lambda_2)y}\right)\,dy\\ &=1-\left[-\frac{\lambda_2}{\lambda_1+\lambda_2}e^{-(\lambda_1+\lambda_2)y}\right]_0^\infty=1-\frac{\lambda_2}{\lambda_1+\lambda_2}\\ &=\frac{\lambda_1}{\lambda_1+\lambda_2} \end{align*}

Discrete/AC

P(a<Y\le b|X=x) \begin{align*} p_{Y|X}(y|x)&=\frac{p_{XY}(x,y)}{p_X(x)}\\ P(a<Y\le b|X=x)&=\frac{P(a<Y\le b,X=x)}{P(X=x)}\\ f_{Y|X}(y|x)&=\frac{f_{XY}(x,y)}{f_X(x)}\\ P(a\le Y\le b|X=x)&=\int_a^bf_{Y|X}(y,x)\,dy\\ \end{align*}

2.7 Multivariate Distributions

2.7 Multivariate Discrete

Joint CDF F_{XY}:\R^2\to[0,1] of X and Y F_{XY}(x,y)=P(X\le x,Y\le y)=P(\{X\le x\}\cap\{Y\le y\})
Marginal CDF from joint CDF F_{XY} \begin{align*} F_X(x)&=\lim_{y\to\infty}F_{XY}(x,y)\\ F_X(y)&=\lim_{x\to\infty}F_{XY}(x,y)\\ \end{align*}
Marginal PMF from joint PMF \begin{align*} p_X(x)=\sum_yp_{XY}(x,y)=\sum_yP(X=x,Y=y) \end{align*}
p_{XY}(x,y)=p^2q^{x+y} for x,y\in\mathbb N \begin{align*} p_X(x)&=\sum_{y=0}^\infty p_{XY}(x,y)=\sum_y p^2q^xq^y\\ &=p^2q^x\sum_yq^y=\frac{p^2q^x}{1-q}=pq^x\quad y\in\mathbb N\\ F_{XY}(x,y)&=P(X\le x,Y\le y)=\sum_{i=0}^x\sum_{i=0}^yp_{XY}(i,j)=\sum_i^x\sum_j^yp^2q^iq^j\\ &=p^2\sum_i^xq^i\sum_j^yq^j=p^2\cdot\frac{1-q^{x+1}}{1-q}\cdot\frac{1-q^{y+1}}{1-q}\\ &=\left(1-q^{x+1}\right)\left(1-q^{y+1}\right)\quad x,y\in\mathbb N\\\ P(X=Y)&=\sum_{i=0}^\infty p_{XY}(i,i)=\sum p^2q^{2i}=\frac{p^2}{1-q^2}\\ &=\frac{p^2}{(1-q)(1+q)}=\frac p{1+q}=\frac p{2-p} \end{align*}
