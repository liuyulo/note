## Group

<details class="definition" open>
<summary>group</summary>

**monoid**: associativity, identity

**group**: monoid, inverses

**abelian** group: commutative
</details>

<details class="definition" open>
<summary>order</summary>

for group $G$

<div class="flex justify-center">

$|G|$ is order of group
</div>

additive group

- assume $\mathbb{Z}$ is group with $+$
- assume $\mathbb Z_n$ is group with $+_n$

for element $a\in G$
<div class="flex justify-center">

$|a|$ is order of element, that is, min $n\in\mathbb Z^+$ st $a^n=e$ or $|a|=\infty$
</div>
</details>

<details class="theorem" open>
<summary>subgroup test</summary>
for nonempty $H\sube G$

- $H$ closed under $(a,b)\mapsto ab^{-1}\implies H\le G$
- $H$ closed under $(a,b)\to ab$ and $a\to a^{-1}\implies H\le G$
</details>

<details class="definition" open>
<summary>cyclic subgroup</summary>
for $a\in G$, the **cyclic** subgroup generated by $a$ is 

$$
\langle a\rangle=\{a^n:n\in\mathbb Z\}\le G
$$
- cyclic groups are at most countable
</details>

<details class="theorem" open>
<summary>fundamental theorem of finite cyclic groups</summary>
for $|a|=n$ and $k|n$

<div class="flex justify-center">
$\langle a\rangle$ has only one subgroup of order $k$, namely $\langle a^{n/k}\rangle$
</div>
</details>

<details class="definition" open>
<summary>normal subgroup</summary>
for group $G$ and $H\le G$ is **normal** subgroup of $G$ iff

$$
H\triangleleft G\iff\forall a\enspace aH=Ha
$$

normaliser of $H\le G$ is

$$
N(H)=\{a\in G:aHa^{-1}=H\}\le G
$$
</details>

<details class="theorem" open>
<summary>langrange theorem</summary>
for any finite group $G,H$ where $|G|=n$ and $|H|=k$

<div class="flex justify-center">
$H\le G\implies k|n$
</div>


- $|G:H|=|G|/|H|$ is number of distinct cosets
- converse is false, $|A_4|=12$ but has no subgroup of order $6$

</details>

## Fundamental Theorem of Finite Abelian Groups


<details class="theorem" open>
<summary>fundamental theorem of finite abelian groups</summary>
for finite abelian group $G$ where $n=|G|$

- $\exists!$ factorisation $n=q_1\cdots q_n$ for some prime power $q_i=p_i^{n_i}$ such that

$$
G\cong\mathbb Z_{q_1}\oplus\cdots\oplus\mathbb Z_{q_k}=\bigoplus_{1\le i\le k}\mathbb Z_{q_i}
$$

where

- $p_1\le p_2\le\cdots\le p_n$
</details>



<details class="algorithm" open>
<summary>enumarate abelian groups of given order</summary>

for $p^n$

- each partition of $n$ gives an abelian group of order $p^n$

for $p^n\cdot m$

- each partition of $n$, product with each group of order $m$ gives an abelian group of order $p^n\cdot m$

**Corollary.** for abelian group $G$ with order $n$

<div class="flex justify-center">

$m\mid n\implies \exists H\le G$ with order $m$
</div>
</details>
