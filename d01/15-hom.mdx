<details class="definition" open>
<summary>ring homomorphism</summary>

function $\varphi:R\to S$ is a ring **homomorphism** iff

$$
\begin{align*}
  \forall a\forall b&&\varphi(a+b)&=\varphi(a)+\varphi(b)\\
  &&\varphi(ab)&=\varphi(a)\varphi(b)\\
\end{align*}
$$

- bijective homomorphism is called **isomorphism**

</details>

<details class="theorem" open>
<summary>for $\varphi: R\to S$</summary>

- $A$ is subring of $R\implies \varphi(A)$ is subring of $S$
- $A$ is ideal of $R\implies \varphi(A)$ is ideal of $\varphi(R)$
- $B$ is ideal of $S\implies \varphi^{-1}(B)$ is ideal of $R$
- $R$ commutative $\implies \varphi(R)$ commutative
- when $\varphi$ nontrivial and $R$ has unity, $\varphi$ perserves unity and unit IN $\varphi(R)$


<details class="list-none">
<summary>**Proof.** $\varphi(A)$ is ideal</summary>

two step ideal test

1. $\varphi(A)$ nonempty ($A$ nonempty), WLOG let any $\varphi(a),\varphi(b)\in\varphi(A)$

  - $\because a-b\in A$<span class="float-right">($A$ ring)</span>
  - $\therefore\varphi(a)-\varphi(b)=\varphi(a-b)\in\varphi(A)$

2. since $\varphi$ onto and $A$ absorbes, we have
  $$
  \begin{align*}
    S\varphi(A)&=\varphi(R)\varphi(A)=\varphi(RA)\subseteq\varphi(A)\\
    \varphi(A)S&=\varphi(A)\varphi(R)=\varphi(AR)\subseteq\varphi(A)\\
  \end{align*}
  $$

$\therefore\varphi(A)$ ideal $\enspace\square$
</details>

<details class="list-none">
<summary>**Proof.** $\varphi(1)$ is unity</summary>

WLOG let any $\varphi(r)\in\varphi(R)=S$

- $\therefore\varphi(r)\varphi(1)=\varphi(r1)=\varphi(r)$
- $\therefore\varphi(1)$ is unity $\enspace\square$

</details>
</details>



<details class="theorem" open>
<summary>first isomorphism theorem</summary>

for any $\varphi:R\to S$

$$
R/\ker\varphi\cong\operatorname{ran}\varphi
$$

<details class="list-none">
<summary>**Proof?**</summary>

let $\eta:R\to R/\ker\varphi$ be the natural homomorphism

$$
\eta(r)=r+\ker\varphi
$$

let $\psi$ be defined on domain $R/\ker\varphi$

$$
\psi=\{(a+\ker\varphi,\varphi(a)):a\in R\}
$$

wts $R/\ker\varphi$ and $\operatorname{ran}\varphi$ are rings

wts $\psi:R/\ker\varphi\stackrel\sim\to\operatorname{ran}\varphi$

- is function
- is injective
- is surjective
- is homomorphism

wts $\varphi=\psi\circ\eta$




</details>
</details>

<details class="theorem" open>
<summary>kernels are ideals, ideals are kernels</summary>

for any ideal $A$ of $R$, let $\varphi:R\to R/A$ be the natural homomorphism

<div class="flex justify-center">

$A$ is kernel of $\varphi$
</div>

for any homomorphism $\varphi:R\to S$

<div class="flex justify-center">

$\ker\varphi$ is ideal of $R$

<details class="theorem" open>
<summary>any ring contains $\mathbb{Z}_n$ or $\mathbb{Z}$</summary>

for any ring $R$

- $\operatorname{char}R=n\gt0\implies R$ has a subring isomorphic to $\mathbb{Z}_n$
- $\operatorname{char}R=0\implies R$ has a subring isomorphic to $\mathbb{Z}$

<details class="list-none">
<summary>**Proof.**</summary>

let homomorphism $\varphi:\mathbb{Z}\to R$ where $\varphi(n)=n\cdot 1$

$\therefore\mathbb{Z}/\ker\varphi\cong\varphi(R)$<span class="float-right">(1st iso thm)</span>

$\because\ker\varphi=n\mathbb{Z}$ where $n$ is additive order of $1$, that is, $n=\operatorname{char}R$

case $\operatorname{char}R=0$

- $\therefore\mathbb{Z}\cong\mathbb{Z}/\langle 0\rangle\cong\varphi(R)$

case $\operatorname{char}R=n\gt0$

- $\therefore\mathbb{Z}_n\cong\mathbb{Z}/n\mathbb{Z}\cong\varphi(R)$

where in both cases, $\varphi(R)$ is subring of $R\enspace\square$
</details>
</details>

<details class="theorem" open>
<summary>any field contains $\mathbb{Z}_p$ or $\mathbb{Q}$</summary>

for any field $F$

- $\operatorname{char}F=p\gt0\implies F$ has a subfield isomorphic to $\mathbb{Z}_p$
- $\operatorname{char}F=0\implies F$ has a subfield isomorphic to $\mathbb{Q}$

<details class="list-none">
<summary>**Proof.** (?)</summary>

case $\operatorname{char}F=p$
- $\therefore$ holds since $F$ is ring

case $\operatorname{char}F=0$

- let $Q=\{ab^{-1}:a,b\in F\quad b\ne0\}$
- wts $Q\cong \mathbb{Q}$
</details>

such subfield is called the **prime subfield** of $F$

- which is also the intersection of all subfields

</details>

<details class="example" open>
<summary></summary>

<details class="list-none">
<summary>**Proof.** determine all homomorphisms $\varphi:\mathbb{Z}\to \mathbb{Z}$</summary>

$\because\varphi(1)=\varphi(1^2)=\varphi(1)^2$ 

$\therefore\varphi(1)(1-\varphi(1))=\varphi(1)-\varphi(1)^2=0$

$\therefore\varphi(1)=0$ or $\varphi(1)=1$<span class="float-right">($\operatorname{cod}\varphi=\mathbb{Z}$ ID)</span>

$\because\varphi(n)=n\cdot\varphi(1)$ for any $n\in\mathbb{Z}$

$\therefore\varphi=0$ or $\varphi=1_\mathbb{Z}$


</details>

<details class="list-none">
<summary>**Proof.** any nontrivial ring homomorphism from a field is injective</summary>

let any hom $\varphi:F\to R$

$\because\ker\varphi$ is ideal of $F$ where the only ideals of $F$ are trivial and $F$

$\because\ker\varphi=F$ means $\varphi$ trivial

$\therefore\ker\varphi=\{0\}$, thus $\varphi$ injective $\enspace\square$


</details>
