---
title: Divisibility in Integral Domains
---

<details class="definition" open>
<summary>associates, irreducibles, primes</summary>

for any integral domain $D$


<div class="flex justify-center">


| | |
|-:|:-|
| $a,b\in D$ are **associates** | $a=ub$ for some unit $u\in D$
| nonunit $a\in D^*$ is **irreducible** | $\forall a\forall b\quad a=bc\implies b$ or $c$ is unit
| nonunit $p\in D^*$ is **prime** | $\forall a\forall b\quad p\mid ab\implies p\mid a$ or $p\mid b$
</div>

<details class="list-none">
<summary>**Proof.** prime element iff generates nontrivial prime ideal</summary>

for any nonunit $p\in D^*$ and any $ab\in \langle p\rangle$, that is, $p\mid ab$

- $p$ is prime
- $\iff p\mid a$ or $p\mid b$
- $\iff a\in\langle p\rangle$ or $b\in\langle p\rangle$
- $\iff\langle p\rangle$ is prime
</details>

<details class="list-none">
<summary>**Proof.** associates iff generates same principal ideal</summary>

$(\implies )$ let any $a=ub$

- $\therefore ra=(ru)b\in \langle b\rangle$ for any $ra\in\langle a\rangle$
- $\therefore rb=(ru^{-1})ub=(ru^{-1})a\in\langle a\rangle$ for any $rb\in\langle b\rangle$

$(\impliedby )$ let any $\langle a\rangle=\langle b\rangle$ 

- $\therefore a=rb$ and $b=sa$ for some $r,s\in D$
- $\therefore b=rsb$, thus $rs=1$<span class="float-right">($D$ intdom)</span>


</details>
</details>

<details class="theorem" open>
<summary></summary>

<details class="list-none">
<summary>**Proof.** (ID) prime implies irreducible</summary>

let any prime $p=ab\in D^*$, WLOG assume $a\in D^*$

- $\therefore p\mid a$, thus $a=pt$ for some $t\in D^*$<span class="float-right">($a\in D^*$)</span>
- $\therefore a=pt=abt$, thus $bt=1$
- $\therefore b$ is unit

$\therefore p$ irreducible $\enspace\square$

</details>

<details class="list-none">
<summary>**Proof.** (PID) prime iff irreducible</summary>

wts irreducible implies prime

let any irreducible $a\in D^*$

let any $b,c\in D$, assume $a\mid bc$

let ideal $A=\langle a,b\rangle=\langle d\rangle$ for some $d \in D$

- $\therefore a\in A$, thus $a=dr$ for some $r\in D$
- $\therefore d$ unit or $r$ unit<span class="float-right">($a$ irreducible)</span>

case $d$ unit

- $\therefore A=\langle d\rangle=D\ni 1$
- $\therefore\exists x,y\in D$ such that $1=ax+by$, that is, $c=acx+bcy$
- $\because a\mid \underline acx$ and $a\mid\underline{bc}y$
- $\therefore a\mid c$ as wanted

case $r$ unit

- $\therefore\langle a\rangle=\langle d\rangle=A\ni b$<span class="float-right">($a,d$ associates)</span>
- $\therefore a\mid b$ as wanted

$\therefore a$
</details>
</details>


<details class="example" open>
<summary></summary>

<details class="list-none">
<summary>**Proof.** any nontrival prime in PID is maximal</summary>

let any nontrivial prime $\langle p\rangle\subseteq D$

let any $\langle b\rangle\supset\langle p\rangle$, wts $\langle b\rangle=D$

- $\therefore b\mid p$, thus $bd=p$ for some $d\in D$
- $\because p$ irreducible<span class="float-right">($p$ prime, since $D$ PID)</span>
- $\therefore b$ is unit ($d$ is not unit, since $b,p$ not associates)
- $\therefore\langle b\rangle=D\enspace\square $

</details>

</details>
