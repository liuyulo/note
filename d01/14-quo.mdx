## Ideals

<details class="definition" open>
<summary>ideal (analogue to normal subgroups)</summary>

a subring $A\sube R$ is an **ideal** iff *absorbes* $R$
$$
RA,AR\sube A
$$

- e.g. $n\mathbb Z$ is an ideal of $\mathbb Z$

**Corollary.** $A\triangleleft R$ under addition

<details class="list-none">
<summary>**Proof.**</summary>
let idea $A\sube R$

- $\because A$ is subring
- $\therefore A$ is group under addition, thus subgroup of $R$
- $\because R$ is abelian
- $\therefore A$ is normal $\quad\square$

</details>
</details>

<details class="theorem" open>
<summary>ideal test</summary>

subset $A$ of ring $R$ is ideal if

- $A$ nonempty, closed under difference
- $AR,RA\sube A$
</details>

<details class="example" open>
<summary></summary>

<details>
<summary>$R^X$ is ring for any nonempty $X$</summary>

- $R^X$ is ring (pointwise add, mul)
- $\{f:\in R^X:f(x)=0\}$ is ideal for any $x\in X$

</details>

<details>
<summary>ideal is closed under intersection</summary>

let ring $R$ and ideals $\{A_\alpha\}$, then

$$
A=\bigcap_\alpha A_\alpha
$$

is ideal

<details class="list-none">
<summary>**Proof.** two step ideal test</summary>

let any $ar\in AR$

- $\therefore\forall\alpha\quad a\in A_\alpha$, thus $ar\in A_\alpha R\subseteq A_\alpha$ (since $A_\alpha$ ideal)
- $\therefore ar\in A$, similar argument for $RA\subseteq A$

let any $a,b\in A$

- $\because\forall\alpha\quad a,b\in A_\alpha$, thus $a-b\in A_\alpha$ (since $A_\alpha$ ideal)
- $\therefore a-b\in A\enspace\square$

</details>

</details>
<details>
<summary>ideal union is ideal iff one contains other</summary>

let ideal $A,B$ of ring $R$

<div class="flex justify-center">

$A\cup B$ is ideal of $R\iff A\subseteq B$ or $B\subseteq A$
</div>

<details class="list-none">
<summary>**Proof.**</summary>

$(\implies )$ assume $A\cup B$ ideal

- BWOC assume $\exists a\in A-B$ and $\exists b\in B-A$
- $\therefore a+b\in A\cup B$ <span style="float: right;">($A\cup B$ ideal)</span>
- case $a+b\in A$
- $\therefore b=(a+b)-a\in A$, thus contradicts that $b\notin A$<span class="w-1/4"/> <span style="float: right;">($A$ ideal)</span>
- case $a+b\in B$
- $\therefore a=(a+b)-b\in B$, thus contradicts that $a\notin B$ <span style="float: right;">($B$ ideal)</span>

$(\impliedby )$ trivial
</details>
</details>


</details>


<details class="definition" open>
<summary>ring generated by subset</summary>

for ring $R$ and nonempty $S\subseteq R$, the **subring** $\langle S\rangle$ is

$$
\langle S\rangle=\bigcap\{A\subseteq R:A\text{ is ideal and }S\subseteq A\}
$$

- that is, smallest (minimal in $\subseteq$) ideal that contains $S$

</details>

<details class="theorem" open>
<summary>subring of comm ring generated by finite subset</summary>

for comm ring $R$ and finite $S_0=\{s_i\}_{i=1}^n\subseteq R$

$$
\langle S_0\rangle=\left\{\sum_{i=1}^nr_is_i:r_i\in R\right\}
$$

notice $S\subseteq\langle S\rangle$ since $0,1\in R$
</details>

### Principal Ideal

<details class="definition" open>
<summary>principal ideal of commutative unity ring</summary>

for comm $R$ with unity and $a\in R$, the **principal ideal** generated by $a$ is

$$
\langle a\rangle=Ra
$$

</details>


<details class="definition" open>
<summary>principal ideal ring</summary>

for comm ring $R$ with unity

<div class="flex justify-center">

$R$ is **PIR** $\iff$ every ideal of $R$ is principal
</div>

</details>

<details class="example" open>
<summary>$\mathbb{Z}$ is PIR</summary>

<details class="list-none">
<summary>**Proof.**</summary>

1. $\mathbb{Z}$ is comm ring with unity

2. let any ideal $A$ of $\mathbb{Z}$

case $A$ trivial

- $\therefore A=0\mathbb{Z}$

case $A$ nontrivial

- let $A^+=\{x\in A:x\gt0\}$

<details>
<summary>$\therefore A^+$ nonempty</summary>

for any $n\in A$

$$
\begin{align*}
  n\gt0&\implies n\in A^+\\
  n\lt0&\implies -n\in A^+
\end{align*}
$$
</details>

let $a=\min A^+$<span class="float-right">(well order)</span>

wts $A\subseteq a\mathbb{Z}$, let any $x\in A$

- $\therefore\exists q\exists r$ st $x=qa+r$ and $0\le r\lt a$<span class="float-right">(division algorithm)</span>

- $\therefore r=x-qa\in A$ where $x,qa\in A$<span class="float-right">($A$ is ring)</span>
- $\therefore r=0$<span class="float-right">($a$ is least positive in $A$)</span>
- $\therefore x=qa\in a\mathbb{Z}$

$\because a\mathbb{Z}\subseteq A\mathbb{Z}\subseteq A$<span class="float-right">($A$ ideal)</span>

$\therefore a\mathbb{Z}=A$, thus $A$ is principal ideal $\enspace\square$


</details>
</details>


<details class="definition" open>
<summary>principal ideal domain</summary>
integral domain $D$ is a **principal ideal domain** $\iff$ every ideal of $D$ is principal
</details>

### Prime, Maximal Ideals


<details class="definition" open>
<summary>prime ideal, maximal ideal of commutative rings</summary>

proper ideal $A$ of comm $R$ is **prime** iff
<div class="flex justify-center">
$\forall a,b\in R\quad ab\in A\implies a\in A$ or $b\in A$
</div>

proper ideal $A$ of comm $R$ is  **maximal** iff

<div class="flex justify-center">
for any ideal $B\sube R\quad A\sube B\sube R\implies A=B$ or $B=R$
</div>
</details>

<details class="theorem" open>
<summary>ideal $A\sube R$ contains a unit $\iff A=R$</summary>

<details class="list-none">
<summary>**Proof.**</summary>
$(\implies)$ assume $A$ contains unit $u$
- let any $r\in R$
- $\therefore r=r(u^{-1}u)=(ru^{-1})u\in A$

$(\impliedby) 1_R\in  R=A\quad\square$
</details>

**Corollary.** $a$ is unit $\implies\langle a\rangle=R$
</details>

### Sum, Product Ideals

<details class="definition" open>
<summary>sum of ideals</summary>

for ideals $A,B$ of ring $R$

$$
A+B=\{a+b:a\in A\quad b\in B\}
$$

<details class="list-none">
<summary>**Proof.** $A+B$ is ideal</summary>

ideal test

(1) notice $A+B$ nonempty since $A,B$ nonempty (are ideals)

(2) let any $a_1+b_1,a_2+b_2\in A+B$

$$
\therefore (a_1+b_1)-(a_2+b_2)=(a_1-a_2)+(b_1-b_2)\in A+B
$$

(3) let any $r\in R$ and $a+b\in A+B$

$$
\begin{align*}
  \therefore&& r(a+b)&=ra+rb\in rA+rB\subseteq A+B\\
  &&(a+b)r&=ar+br\in Ar+Br\subseteq A+B\\
\end{align*}
$$
</details>
</details>

<details class="definition" open>
<summary>product of ideals</summary>

for ideals $A,B$ of ring $R$

$$
AB=\left\{\sum_{i=1}^n a_ib_i:a_i\in A\quad b_i\in B\right\}
$$

- notice $AB$ is smallest ideal that contains $\{ab:a\in A\quad b\in B\}$

</details>

<details class="example" open>
<summary></summary>

for ideals $A,B$ of commutative ring $R=A+B$ with unity

$$
AB=A\cap B
$$

<details class="list-none">
<summary>**Proof.**</summary>

$(\subseteq)$ let any $\sum a_ib_i\in AB$

- $\because  a_ib_i\in Ab_i\subseteq A$
- $\therefore \sum a_i b_i\in A$<span class="float-right">($A$ is ring)</span>
- similarly $\sum a_ib_i\in B$

$(\supseteq)$ let some $a+b=1\in R$

- let any $c\in A\cap B$

$$
\therefore c=1c=(a+b)c=ac+cb\in AB
$$

thus $A\cap B\subseteq AB\enspace\square$
</details>

</details>


## Quotient Rings

<details class="definition" open>
<summary>factor rings</summary>
for ring $R$ and ideal $A\sube R$, the factor ring is

$$
R/A=\{r+A:r\in R\}
$$

where
$$
\begin{align*}
  (a+A)+(b+A)&=(a+b)+A\\
(a+A)(b+A)&=ab+A
\end{align*}
$$
</details>

<details class="theorem" open>
<summary>quotient ring iff ideal</summary>

for ring $R$ and subring $A$

<div class="flex justify-center">

$R/A$ is ring $\iff A$ is ideal
</div>

<details class="list-none">
<summary>**Proof.** wts $A$ is ideal $\iff$ mul of $R/A$ is well-defined</summary>
$(\implies)$ assume subring $A\sube R$ is ideal

- let any $r+A=r'+A$ and $s+A=s'+A$
  - wts $rs+A=r's'+A$
- $\because\exists a,b\in A$ st $r=r'+a$ and $s=s'+b$ by def
- $\because A$ <span title="contains">absorbes</span> $a's,r'b,ab$

$$
\begin{align*}
  \therefore&& rs&=(r'+a)(s'+b)=r's'+a's+r'b+ab\\
  \therefore&& rs+A&=r's'+a's+r'b+ab+A=r's'+A
\end{align*}
$$

- $\therefore$ well-defined

$(\impliedby)$ assume $A$ not ideal, that is, $\exists a\in A$ and $r\in R$ but $ar\notin A$ or $ra\notin A$

- WLOG assume $ar\notin A$

$$
\begin{align*}
  \therefore&&(a+A)(r+A)&=(0+A)(r+A)=0r+A=A\\
  &&(a+A)(r+A)&=ar+A\ne A
\end{align*}
$$

- $\therefore$ not well-defined $\quad\square$


</details>
</details>

<details class="theorem" open>
<summary>quotient integral domain iff prime ideal</summary>

for comm ring $R$ and ideal $A$

<div class="flex justify-center">

$R/A$ is integral domain $\iff A$ is prime
</div>

<details class="list-none">
<summary>**Proof.**</summary>
let any commutative ring $R$ and ideal $A$

$(\implies)$ assume $R/A$ is intdom

- let any $ab\in A$
- $\therefore(a+A)(b+A)=ab+A=A$ (i.e. the add identity)
- $\therefore a+A=A$ or $b+A=A$
- $\therefore a\in A$ or $b\in A$, thus $A$ prime

$(\impliedby)$ assume $A$ is prime

- let any $(a+A),(b+A)\in R/A$ such that product is $A$
- $\therefore (a+A)(b+A)=ab+A=A$, thus $ab\in A$
- $\therefore a\in A$ or $b\in A$ since $A$ is prime
- $\therefore a+A=A$ or $b+A=A$ (i.e. the add identity)
- $\therefore R/A$ has no zero divisor
- $\because R/A$ is comm ring
- $\therefore R/A$ is intdom $\quad\square$
</details>
</details>

<details class="theorem" open>
<summary>quotient field iff maximal ideal</summary>

for comm ring $R$ and ideal $A$

<div class="flex justify-center">

$R/A$ is field $\iff A$ is maximal 
</div>
<details class="list-none">
<summary>**Proof.**</summary>
$(\implies)$ let $R/A$ be field and any ideal $B\supset A$

- $\therefore\exists b\in B\setminus A$
- $\therefore b+A\ne A$, that is, $b+A\in (R/A)^*$
- $\therefore\exists c+A\in R/A$ st $1+A=(b+A)(c+A)=bc+A\in R/A$, that is, $b+A$ has a mul inverse in field $R/A$
- $\therefore 1-bc\in A\sub B$
- $\therefore 1=(1-bc)+bc\in B$, thus $B=R$
- $\therefore A$ is maximal

$(\impliedby)$ assume $A$ is maximal
- let any $b+A\in(R/A)^*$, that is, $b\in R\setminus A$, wts $b+A$ has mul inv
- let $B=\{br+a:r\in R\enspace a\in A\}\supset A$ be ideal generated by $A;b$
- $\therefore B=R$ since $A$ is maximal
- $\therefore 1\in B$, let $1=bc+a$ for some $c\in R$ and $a\in A$

$$
\therefore (b+A)(c+A)=bc+A=(bc+a)+A=1+A
$$
- $\therefore b+A$ has mul inv
- $\therefore R/A$ is field $\quad\square$
</details>

**Corollary.** $A$ is maximal ideal $\implies A$ is prime ideal
</details>
