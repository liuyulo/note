## Class Equation

<details class="definition" open>
<summary>conjugacy class</summary>

for group $G$ and $a\in G$

$$
\operatorname{cl}a=\{gag^{-1}:g\in G\}
$$

**Corollary.** conjugacy class is equivalence relation on $G$
</details>

<details class="theorem" open>
<summary>conjugacy class equinumerous to index of centraliser</summary>

for finite $G$ and $a\in G$

$$
|\operatorname{cl}a|=|G:C(a)|
$$

<details class="list-none">
<summary>**Proof.**</summary>

this is bijective mapping

$$
gC(a)\mapsto gag^{-1}
$$
</details>

**Corollary.** $|\operatorname{cl}a|$ divides $|G|$

**Corollary.** class equation

$$
|G|=\sum|G:C(a)|
$$

where sums over each $a$ in a conjugacy class
</details>

<details class="theorem" open>
<summary>better class equation</summary>

$$
|G|=|Z(G)|+\sum|G:C(a)|
$$

where sums over each $a\notin Z(G)$

<details class="list-none" open>
<summary>**Proof.** $a\in Z(G)\iff\operatorname{cl} a=\{a\}$</summary>

</details>
</details>

<details class="example" open>
<summary>prime squared order is abelian</summary>

<div class="flex justify-center">

$|G|=p^2\implies  G$ abelian
</div>

<details class="list-none">
<summary>**Proof.**</summary>

$|Z(G)|=p\implies |G/Z(G)|=p$, thus $G/Z(G)$ cyclic, thus $G$ abelian

$|Z(G)|=p^2\implies G=G/Z(G)$, thus $G$ cyclic, thus abelian $\square$
</details>
</details>

## Sylow
<details class="definition" open>
<summary>p-group</summary>

for prime $p$

<div class="flex justify-center">
group $G$ is a $p$-group iff $|G|=p^n$ for some power $n$
</div>
</details>

<details class="theorem" open>
<summary>p-groups have nontrivial centers</summary>

for p-group $G$

<div class="flex justify-center">

$Z(G)$ is nontrivial, that is, has more than one element
</div>
</details>

<details class="definition" open>
<summary>sylow p-subgroup</summary>
<div class="flex justify-center">
p-subgroup $H\le G$ is **sylow p-subgroup** iff $H$ is maximal p-subgroup of $G$
</div>

let $\operatorname{Syl}_p(G)$ denote the set of sylow p-subgroups of $G$
</details>

<details class="theorem" open>
<summary>sylow's first theorem</summary>
for finite $G$ with order $n$ and any prime power $p^k$

$$
p^k|n\implies\exists H\le G\enspace|H|=p^k
$$
</details>

<details class="theorem" open>
<summary>sylow's second theorem</summary>
for finite $G$ and $H\le G$, for any $p$

<div class="flex justify-center">
$H$ is p-subgroup of $G\implies\exists K\in\operatorname{Syl}_p(G)$ such that $H\le K\le G$
</div>
</details>

<details class="theorem" open>
<summary>sylow's third theorem</summary>
for finite $G$ with order $p^km$ where $p\nmid m$
- let $n_p=|\operatorname{Syl}_p(G)|$

<div class="flex justify-center">

$n_p\equiv 1\mod p$ and $n_p\mid m$
</div>

- and every sylow $p$-subgroups are conjugates

**Corollary.** for any $H\in\operatorname{Syl}_p(G)$

$$
\operatorname{Syl}_p(G)=\{H\}\iff H\triangleleft G
$$
</details>

<details class="example" open>
<summary>cyclic groups order pq</summary>

for $G$ with order $pq$ where $p\lt q$

$$
p\nmid q-1\implies G\cong\mathbb{Z}_{pq}
$$
</details>

<details class="example" open>
<summary>nonabelian group of order pq has exactly q+3 subgroups</summary>

let nonabelian $G$ with order $pq$ where $p\lt q$ and 

<div class="flex justify-center">

$G$ has exactly $q+3$ subgroups
</div>

<details class="list-none">
<summary>**Proof.**</summary>

one trivial, one itself, wts has $q+1$ more subgroups (namely sylow subgroups)

$\because n_p\equiv 1\mod p$ and $n_p|q$

$\phantom\because n_q\equiv 1\mod q$ and $n_q|p$ 

$\therefore n_q=1$<span class="float-right">($n_q\le p\lt q$)</span>

$\phantom\therefore n_p=1$ or $n_p=q$

BWOC assume $n_p\ne 1$ (thus found $q$ sylow p-subgroups and $1$ sylow q-subgroup)

- let $H_p,H_q\triangleleft G$ be the unique sylow p-subgroup, sylow q-subgroup
- $\therefore H_p,H_q$ intersects trivially (coprime order) and $|H_pH_q|=pq=G$

- $\therefore G=H_p\times H_q\cong H_p\oplus H_q$, thus cyclic, contradicts that $G$ abelian $\square$

</details>

</details>

<details class="example" open>
<summary>order p(p+1) is not simple</summary>

let any group $G$ with order $n=p(p+1)=p^2+p$
<div class="flex justify-center">

$G$ is not simple
</div>

<details class="list-none">
<summary>**Proof.**</summary>

$\because n_p\equiv 1\mod p$ and $n_p\mid p+1$<span class="float-right">(sylow 3)</span>

$\therefore n_p=1$ or $n_p=p+1$

case $n_p=p$

- $\therefore$ not simple<span class="float-right">(sylow 3 cor)</span>

case $n_p=p+1$, that is, $p+1$ sylow p-subgroups

- $\because$ any sylow-p subgroup has order $p$<span class="float-right">($p^2\nmid n$)</span>
- $\therefore$ eath sylow p-subgroup contains $p-1$ elements of order $p$<span class="float-right">($p$ prime + lagrange)</span>
- $\because$ each of the $p+1$ sylow p-subgroups are cyclic<span class="float-right">($p$ prime)</span>
- $\therefore G$ has $(p-1)(p+1)$ elements of order $p$<span class="float-right">(cycilc subgroups intersect trivially)</span>
- $\because$ none of these $p^2-1$ elements can be in a sylow (p+1) subgroup<span class="float-right">($p\nmid p+1$)</span>
- $\therefore G$ has $p+1$ elements left, which forms the only sylow (p+1) subgroup<span class="float-right">(sylow 1)</span>
- $\therefore$ not simple $\square$
</details>

</details>

<details class="example" open>
<summary></summary>

let any group with order $p^nq^m$ st $H_p,H_q$ abelian (unique sylow subgroup)

- that is, $n_p=n_q=1$

<div class="flex justify-center">

$G$ is abelian
</div>


<details class="list-none">
<summary>**Proof.**</summary>


$\because H_p$ intersects $H_q$ trivially <span class="float-right">($|H_p|$ and $|H_q|$ coprime + Lagrange)</span>

$\therefore |H_pH_q|=|H_p||H_q|/|H_p\cap H_q|=|H_p||H_q|=|G|$, thus $G=H_pH_q$

$\because H_p,H_q$ normal<span class="float-right">(sylow 3 cor)</span>

$\therefore G=H_p\times H_q\cong H_p\oplus H_q$

$\because H_p$ and $H_q$ abelian

$\therefore G$ abelian $\square$
</details>

</details>

<details class="example" open>
<summary>p-groups have normal subgroups of each prime power order</summary>

for $G$ with order $p^n$

$$
\forall k\quad\exists N\triangleleft G\text{ st }|N|=p^k
$$
 
<details class="list-none" open>
<summary>**Proof.** induction</summary>

base $n=0$

assume true for any $m\lt n$ as IH

$\because Z(G)$ is nontrivial and $z|p^n$ for $z=|Z(G)|$

$\therefore Z(G)$ has a subgroup $N_1$ of order $p$ <span class="float-right">(Sylow 1)</span>

$\therefore N_1\triangleleft G$ <span class="float-right">($K\subseteq Z(G)$)</span>

consider $G/K$ with order $p^{n-1}$

$\therefore\exists H'_1,\cdots,H'_{n-1}\triangleleft G/K$ where $|H'_k|=p^k$
</details>
</details>
